# PMP Exam Master Brief: Direct and Manage Project Work

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Direct and Manage Project Work is the central execution engine of any project — the process where approved plans transform into deliverables, decisions, and documented outcomes. The PM leads and performs the work defined in the project management plan, implements approved changes, and manages interfaces across all knowledge areas simultaneously. This is not a planning process; it is where budget is consumed, teams are mobilized, and stakeholders receive tangible outputs. On regulated projects in banking or capital markets — such as a Bangladesh Bank-mandated core banking upgrade — this process carries the full weight of governance compliance: every work package executed must trace to an approved scope baseline, every change to a corrective action request. Exam weight is significant because it is the execution anchor for Integration Management and bleeds into every other knowledge area (scope, schedule, cost, quality, risk, communications, resources, procurement). Work performance data — the raw observations generated here — flows directly into Control processes and ultimately becomes work performance reports. Understanding the distinction between work performance data, information, and reports is a guaranteed exam topic. **In one sentence:** Direct and Manage Project Work is the process of leading execution of the project management plan, producing deliverables and work performance data, and implementing approved change requests across all domains.

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Predictive | PM directs work according to a frozen baseline; change requests are formal, go through Integrated Change Control, and no work begins on a change until approved. |
| Agile | Team pulls work from a prioritized backlog; the product owner manages scope dynamically each sprint; "directing" is servant-leadership, not command-and-control. |
| Hybrid | Regulatory deliverables (audit trails, compliance sign-offs) follow predictive discipline; iterative development tracks follow sprint cadence; PM reconciles both in a single execution rhythm. |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Work performance data is raw — never report it directly.** Real-world PMs often escalate raw metrics straight to steering committees. PMI insists data must flow through Control processes and become information first, then reports.
2. **Approved change requests drive work, not verbal direction.** Experienced PMs issue verbal instructions constantly. PMI requires all changes — even minor correctives — to follow Integrated Change Control before execution begins.
3. **Defect repair requests are inputs to Control Quality, not direct re-work orders.** Real-world instinct is to fix the defect immediately. PMI routes defect repair through formal approval loops before repair work starts.
4. **The PM implements approved changes; the CCB approves them.** Many PMs believe they have authority to approve scope-impacting changes themselves. PMI is firm: PM implements, CCB (or delegated authority) approves.
5. **Lessons learned are captured during execution, not just at project close.** Real-world teams document lessons at retrospectives or close-out. PMI expects continuous capture throughout Direct and Manage Project Work.

### Ethics Angle

- **Responsibility:** PM is accountable for ensuring only approved work is executed; executing unapproved scope violates the duty to the organization and sponsor.
- **Honesty:** Work performance data must be reported accurately — padding schedule metrics to avoid bad news violates PMI's Code of Ethics.
- **Fairness:** Resource allocation during execution must be transparent; preferential task assignment based on personal relationships undermines fair project governance.
- **Respect:** Directing team work must preserve psychological safety; authoritarian command undermines the trust required for teams to surface issues early.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Work Performance Data | Raw observations and measurements collected during execution (% complete, costs incurred, defect counts) | Do not confuse with Work Performance Information (analyzed data from Control processes) or Work Performance Reports (formatted communication outputs) |
| Deliverable | Any unique, verifiable product, result, or capability produced to complete a process or phase | Deliverables are outputs of Direct and Manage Project Work, not of planning processes |
| Change Request | Formal proposal to modify documents, deliverables, or baselines — includes corrective/preventive actions and defect repairs | A verbal instruction is NOT a change request; PMI requires documentation before action |
| Corrective Action | Intentional activity realigning project performance with the management plan | Reactive to current variance; do not confuse with Preventive Action (proactive, before variance occurs) |
| Preventive Action | Activity ensuring future project performance conforms to the plan | Forward-looking; exam stems with "to avoid future problems" signal preventive action |
| Defect Repair | Identifying and correcting a defective component that does not meet requirements | Must be formally approved before repair work begins; defect found ≠ defect repaired immediately |
| Issue Log | Document recording problems, concerns, and conflicts requiring resolution during execution | Not the risk register; risks that materialize become issues |
| Lessons Learned Register | Ongoing capture of knowledge gained throughout execution | Updated during execution, not just at project close — a common exam trap |
| Organizational Process Assets (OPA) | Plans, processes, policies, procedures, and knowledge bases from the performing organization | Bangladesh Bank compliance frameworks and internal audit templates are OPAs in a banking context |
| Enterprise Environmental Factors (EEF) | Conditions outside the project team's immediate control that influence the project | BSEC regulations, Bangladesh Bank guidelines, and prevailing market volatility are EEFs in capital markets projects |
| Project Management Information System (PMIS) | Information systems (scheduling tools, document management, configuration management) used to manage project information | PMIS is a tool/technique, not an output; exam sometimes asks where PMIS fits |
| Approved Change Requests | Output of Perform Integrated Change Control; become inputs to Direct and Manage Project Work | Only approved (not submitted) change requests are inputs to execution |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| "A team member identifies a defect in a delivered component" | Document the defect as a change request (defect repair) and route through change control | Defect repair requires formal approval before rework begins — PMI does not allow self-authorized repairs |
| "Sponsor asks PM to add a feature verbally during a status meeting" | Log it as a change request and submit to Integrated Change Control | Verbal direction never overrides the formal change process, even from sponsor |
| "Work is behind schedule and PM wants to add resources" | Submit a corrective action change request and obtain approval before acting | Corrective actions must be formally approved; no self-execution of scope-impacting responses |
| "Team discovers new risk during execution" | Update the risk register and route to Risk Management processes | Newly identified risks during execution are inputs to Monitor Risks, not to Direct and Manage directly |
| "Stakeholder requests a progress update" | Provide work performance reports generated through Control processes | Raw data (work performance data) is not shared directly; it must be processed first |
| "A regulatory body (e.g., Bangladesh Bank) issues a new directive mid-project" | Assess impact as a change request before altering any deliverable | External regulatory changes are EEFs triggering formal change control, not immediate re-planning |
| "PM notices actual costs exceeding plan" | Analyze variance and issue a corrective action through proper change control | Performance variances trigger corrective actions through ICC, not direct plan modification |
| "Team member bypasses quality gate and delivers directly to client" | Stop the delivery, document the deviation as a defect, and initiate corrective action | Quality gates exist to protect the customer and organization; bypassing them requires formal corrective action, not retroactive acceptance |
| "Project sponsor demands execution begin before all approvals are complete" | Escalate to sponsor in writing, citing risks of proceeding without approved baselines | PMI requires formal approval before execution; escalating (not complying) protects project integrity |
| "A previously closed risk materializes during execution" | Create an issue log entry and activate the contingency plan if one exists | Materialized risks become issues; issue log is the correct repository, not the risk register |

**Topic-Specific Weak-Spot Scenarios — Escalation Edge Cases**

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| "Functional manager reassigns a key resource without informing PM during execution" | Address the conflict with the functional manager first; escalate to sponsor only if unresolved | PMI always tries resolution at lowest level before escalating — direct conversation precedes sponsor involvement |
| "Senior stakeholder instructs PM to skip a deliverable review to meet a deadline" | Document the risk and issue a change request to formally acknowledge scope/quality trade-off | PMI does not allow silent compliance; the PM must formally document and route any skip of a planned control activity |

        |
       YES

**Mnemonic: D-A-C-L**
**"Deliver, Analyze, Control, Loop"**

- **D**eliver — execute work and produce deliverables
- **A**nalyze — collect raw work performance data
- **C**ontrol — route data through control processes
- **L**oop — approved change requests feed back into execution

Every cycle of execution produces outputs, those outputs feed controls, controls approve changes, and changes re-enter execution. The loop never short-circuits.

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project
- All execution work is authorized by a formally approved project management plan and scope baseline
- Work package owners must be clearly identified in the WBS Dictionary before execution begins
- Configuration management system must track all deliverable versions throughout execution
- Change requests are written, tracked in a change log, and not executed until CCB approval

### Agile / Hybrid
- Sprint backlog items replace formal work authorization for the iteration; PO-approved backlog is the execution authority
- Daily standups are execution monitoring instruments — impediments surfaced here are the equivalent of issues in an issue log
- In hybrid: regulated deliverables (audit logs, compliance artifacts) still follow formal work authorization; iterative components use sprint-pull model
- Velocity and burndown charts replace traditional work performance data metrics for agile tracks
- Retrospectives capture lessons learned continuously — no waiting for project close

### Industry / Regulatory Factors
- **Bangladesh Bank / Core Banking Projects:** Every execution artifact (test evidence, sign-off records) must satisfy Bangladesh Bank IT Risk Guidelines; direct execution must include compliance documentation as a deliverable category
- **BSEC-Regulated Projects:** Capital market system changes require execution audit trails for regulatory inspection; work performance data must be archived per BSEC record-keeping rules
- **Capital Markets:** Real-time market system projects may require execution in maintenance windows only; work authorization must align with market hours and blackout calendars
- **Banking IT Governance:** Execution in banking often requires dual-control sign-off for high-risk deliverables; PM must incorporate this into work package design

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Implement the sponsor's verbal scope change immediately to avoid schedule delay" | Sponsors have authority; acting quickly prevents slippage; experienced PMs trust relationship with sponsor | Verbal instructions never override formal change control; unauthorized scope work violates the baseline and exposes project to uncontrolled scope creep |
| "Update the project management plan to reflect the work being done" | Experienced PMs adapt plans to reality to keep documentation current | Plans are not updated during execution without approved change requests; changing the plan to match reality hides variances and corrupts baseline integrity |
| "Share raw work performance data directly with the steering committee" | Raw data is timely and shows transparency; executives appreciate unfiltered numbers | Raw data is not actionable and may be misinterpreted; PMI requires data to be processed through Control processes into Work Performance Reports before stakeholder communication |
| "Fix the defect found by the team before the customer notices" | Speed saves customer relationships; experienced PMs resolve quietly to protect trust | Defect repair requires a formal change request and approval; unilateral repair bypasses quality controls and may introduce new defects |
| "Begin work on the approved phase before the previous phase is formally closed" | Phase-gate overlap is common in fast-track projects; experienced PMs overlap phases to save time | PMI requires phase closure formalities before proceeding; fast-tracking is a schedule compression technique applied at planning, not an execution shortcut |
| "Add the new regulatory requirement directly to the project scope" | The regulatory requirement is real and must be addressed; ignoring it is risky | Regulatory changes are EEFs triggering formal change control; direct scope addition without ICC approval violates baseline integrity |
| "Bypass the issue log and resolve team conflicts informally to save time" | Informal resolution is faster and preserves team morale | PMI requires issues to be logged; undocumented issues cannot be tracked, escalated properly, or reported to stakeholders who need awareness |
| "Delegate execution decisions entirely to the team to support self-organization" | Empowering teams is an agile principle; micromanagement reduces morale | Even in agile, the PM retains accountability for integration and delivery; full delegation without oversight is not servant leadership — it is abdication |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

A PM at a commercial bank in Bangladesh is directing a core banking system migration. The IT Director — two levels above the PM in the hierarchy — calls the PM directly and instructs her to begin parallel database migration work on the production environment without a formal change request, citing competitive pressure from a rival bank's system launch. The IT Director assures the PM that approval is "forthcoming" and that he will "take responsibility." The PM knows this work was not in the approved plan. What should the PM do FIRST?

A. Begin the migration work as directed, since the IT Director has formal authority and has accepted responsibility.
B. Ask the IT Director to submit a formal change request and wait for CCB approval before beginning any migration work.
C. Escalate directly to the project sponsor to override the IT Director's instruction.
D. Document the IT Director's verbal instruction in an email and begin preliminary migration steps while the formal request is prepared.

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

A scrum team delivering a digital trading platform for a BSEC-licensed brokerage is in Sprint 3. During sprint planning, the product owner introduces 5 new user stories not in the product backlog, citing urgent feedback from compliance officers about a new BSEC directive. The development team pushes back, saying their sprint velocity cannot absorb additional stories. How should the scrum master handle this situation?

A. Add all 5 stories to the sprint, since regulatory compliance requirements take priority over team velocity.
B. Allow the team to self-organize and decide which stories to include, respecting team autonomy.
C. Facilitate a discussion where the product owner prioritizes the stories; the team commits only to what they can complete with confidence.
D. Escalate to the project manager to make the final call on sprint scope.

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

A PM is managing a hybrid project for a Bangladesh Bank-supervised microfinance institution. The project has both a regulated compliance track (predictive) and an iterative mobile app development track (agile). Midway through execution, the compliance team lead reports that the mobile app team's last release included a feature that was not in the approved scope baseline for the regulated track. The mobile app team lead says the feature was a necessary enhancement and was approved by the product owner. What should the PM do FIRST?

A. Issue a defect notice to the mobile app team and demand removal of the feature in the next release.
B. Escalate to the steering committee to determine which authority — CCB or product owner — has precedence.
C. Assess whether the feature crosses the compliance track boundary; if it does, submit a change request to the CCB for retroactive approval.
D. Accept the feature since product owner approval is valid authority in agile, and update the lessons learned register.

**Q4 — Two-Correct, Pick-Best (Predictive)**

The project is on schedule, but the quality manager flags that three deliverables passed review but do not meet the documented acceptance criteria. D. Accept two deliverables that are closest to meeting criteria and reject the third.

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 (Delivery Performance Domain) and the PMI Code of Ethics (Responsibility, Honesty) are unambiguous: approved plans govern execution, and change requests must precede action regardless of who issues the verbal instruction. The IT Director's authority does not override the formal change control system. Option A is wrong because verbal assurance of "forthcoming approval" is not approval; executing without authorization makes the PM complicit in a process violation. Option C (escalate to sponsor) skips the appropriate first response — requesting a formal change request from the IT Director — and jumps over the head of a senior stakeholder unnecessarily. Option D begins work without approval, which is a direct violation regardless of the documentation accompanying it.
**Mindset Lesson:** Seniority of the requester does not substitute for formal approval; the PM's job is to protect the process, not the relationship.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: C**

**PMI Justification:** Agile Practice Guide (Chapter 4, Iteration-Based Agile) establishes that sprint scope is committed by the team, with the product owner accountable for prioritization but not empowered to force commitment beyond team capacity. Option A violates the principle that the team commits to sprint scope voluntarily — regulatory urgency is a priority argument, not a capacity override. Option B is partially correct but incomplete — the scrum master should facilitate, not merely step back; self-organization requires facilitation when conflict exists. Option D escalates to a PM, which in a scrum context bypasses the scrum master's facilitation role and introduces a non-scrum authority structure.
**Mindset Lesson:** The product owner owns priority; the team owns commitment — scrum masters protect the boundary between the two.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: C**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 (Integration Performance Domain) requires that scope changes affecting a regulated baseline go through ICC regardless of the change's origin. The first action is to assess whether this feature actually crosses the compliance boundary — diagnosis before action. Option A (issue defect notice) is premature; whether the feature is a defect depends on scope boundary assessment. Option B (escalate to steering committee) skips the PM's own assessment responsibility. Option D (accept product owner approval) is wrong in a hybrid context where the product owner's authority is limited to the agile track and does not extend to the regulated compliance baseline.
**Mindset Lesson:** In hybrid projects, the authority model is track-specific; product owner approval is not universal.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 (Quality Performance Domain) and the Control Quality process establish that deliverables not meeting documented acceptance criteria must be rejected and routed through defect repair. Client pressure is a business factor, not a quality acceptance criterion. Option A (conditional acceptance) is wrong because conditional acceptance without formal ICC routing allows unacceptable work to pass quality gates. Option C (sponsor authorization) cannot override documented acceptance criteria without a formal change to those criteria. Option D (accept two, reject one) is arbitrary and not grounded in any PMI process.
**Mindset Lesson:** Acceptance criteria are contractual; client pressure is not a change control mechanism.

---

**Q5 — Correct Sequence: D → B → C → A → E**

**PMI Justification:** Agile Practice Guide (Chapter 6, Implementing Agile) describes standups as impediment surfacing forums. The scrum master first facilitates clear articulation of the impediment (D), then immediately helps the team assess impact on sprint goal and identify resolution owners (B). The impediment is then logged (C), burndown updated to reflect reality (A), and only if the sprint goal is genuinely threatened does escalation to the product owner occur (E). Logging before assessing is wrong order; escalating before team self-resolution attempts bypasses agile self-organization principles.
**Mindset Lesson:** Standups surface problems; scrum masters facilitate team resolution before escalating.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 6/7 (Direct and Manage Project Work outputs): deliverables (C), issue log updates (B), change requests (D), and work performance data (A) are all direct outputs. Work performance reports (E) are outputs of the Monitor and Control project work process — they are the formatted communication products produced after work performance data flows through control processes. This is one of the highest-tested distinctions in Integration Management.
**Mindset Lesson:** Work performance data exits execution; work performance reports exit control — never confuse the two.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** PMI Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct (Honesty, Section 5) and Responsibility (Section 4) require PM to report material dishonesty to the appropriate parties immediately. Schedule convenience and vendor promises do not create exceptions. Option A (give vendor a week) allows a known integrity violation to persist and implicates the PM in a cover-up. Option C (log as risk) is a procedural deflection; fabricated test results are not a risk — they are a current active integrity violation. Option D (sponsor only) is insufficient — quality manager and governance body must be informed because the quality management system has been compromised.
**Mindset Lesson:** PMI treats honesty as a non-negotiable; schedule impact is never a valid reason to delay reporting a known integrity violation.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 (Stakeholder and Communications Performance Domains) establishes that communication must be tailored to the audience's needs and decision-making context. Executive stakeholders need summarized, decision-relevant work performance reports — not raw sprint artifacts. Option A (add steering committee to sprint reviews) creates communication overload with mismatched format and will not resolve the understanding gap for non-technical executives. Option C (convert to predictive) is a major governance change requiring formal change control and ignores the value of agile for the development stream. Option D (simplify demos) reduces technical richness of sprint reviews and does not address the steering committee's need for strategic progress information.
**Mindset Lesson:** Communication format must match the audience's decision-making needs, not the team's delivery format.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: C**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 (Planning and Delivery Performance Domains) and the principle of evidence-based decision-making require the PM to conduct an impact analysis and present options before escalating for a decision. The sponsor needs options, not just a problem statement. Option A (immediate escalation) is premature without impact analysis — escalating without a problem framing and options is an abdication of PM responsibility. Option B (use management reserve silently) bypasses sponsor authority; management reserve use must be approved and communicated. Option D (mandate overtime) is a unilateral schedule response that does not address the root cause and may not recover the target.
**Mindset Lesson:** Escalate with a structured options analysis, not just a problem — sponsors make decisions, PMs enable them.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Issue 1 (vendor delay blocking execution) is an active impediment requiring immediate issue log documentation and procurement escalation (A) — this is a direct execution blocker. Issue 2 (authority ambiguity on approved change requests) means approved work is not being executed — clarifying roles in the RACI (C) directly restores execution momentum. Option B (revise schedule baseline) is wrong — baselines require ICC approval; a 20% hour variance is a performance signal requiring corrective action, not a baseline revision. Option D (emergency all-stakeholder meeting) is premature and disproportionate for routine execution issues. Option E (corrective action for cost variance) is valid but lower priority than the two active execution blockers (vendor delay and authority gap).
**Mindset Lesson:** Prioritize actions that unblock execution; baseline revisions require formal approval and are never a FIRST response to variance.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

- Direct and Manage Project Work converts approved plans into deliverables and work performance data — the execution engine of Integration Management.
- Only approved change requests authorize deviations from the plan; verbal instructions — even from sponsors — do not substitute.
- Work performance data is raw output of execution; it must flow through Control processes before becoming information or reports.
- Defect repairs are formal change requests — never self-authorize rework.
- Corrective and preventive actions both require ICC approval before execution.
- Lessons learned are captured continuously during execution, not just at project close.
- In hybrid projects, product owner approval governs agile track scope only — regulated track changes require CCB approval.
- Escalate with impact analysis and options, not just a problem statement.
- Communication to executives uses work performance reports (processed output), never raw data.
- **When in doubt during execution: stop, document the change request, route through ICC, and execute only after approval — the process exists to protect the project, not slow it down.**
