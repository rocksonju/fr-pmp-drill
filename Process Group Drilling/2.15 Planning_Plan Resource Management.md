# PMP Exam Master Brief: Plan Resource Management

---

### 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Plan Resource Management is the process of defining how to estimate, acquire, manage, and use team and physical resources throughout the project. It sits inside the Planning Process Group and Resource Management Knowledge Area, producing the Resource Management Plan and the Team Charter as primary outputs. This process bridges scope decomposition and cost estimation — without a credible resource plan, the schedule is fiction and the budget is guesswork. On the exam, resource management questions appear in both Predictive and Agile contexts; expect cross-domain bleed into Stakeholder Engagement (RACI conflicts), Risk Management (resource availability risk), and Procurement (make-or-buy decisions for physical resources). The process demands understanding of organizational structures — functional, projectized, matrix — because each structure shapes how authority over resources is acquired and exercised. Physical resources (equipment, materials, facilities) and team resources (people, skills, competencies) follow different management logic, and the exam tests whether candidates distinguish them. A RACI matrix, OBS, and RBS are the structural artifacts that translate the resource plan into accountable action. **In one sentence:** Plan Resource Management establishes the governance framework — roles, responsibilities, escalation paths, and acquisition strategies — that makes resource decisions predictable and auditable throughout the project lifecycle.

### 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

| Lens | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Predictive | A formal Resource Management Plan is documented upfront; RACI matrix, OBS, and RBS are created before execution begins; resource calendars lock availability across the project timeline |
| Agile | Team structure is self-organizing; roles are fluid; the team charter captures working agreements and ground rules rather than rigid assignments; capacity planning happens sprint-by-sprint |
| Hybrid | Core governance roles (sponsor, PM, key leads) are defined predictively via RACI; execution-level assignments are managed iteratively; resource plans are baselined but refined each planning cycle |

**Top 5 PMI-isms**

1. The Resource Management Plan must be created before resources are acquired — real-world PMs often recruit first, then define roles; PMI requires the plan to precede acquisition to avoid structural gaps.
2. Team Charter creation is a collaborative act involving the team, not a document the PM writes alone and distributes — real-world instinct is to issue a charter top-down for speed.
3. Physical resources and team resources are governed differently — real-world PMs often apply the same management approach to both; PMI expects distinct treatment (logistics vs. development, procurement vs. HR).
4. Organizational structure analysis must inform the resource plan — real-world PMs often ignore matrix politics; PMI expects explicit acknowledgment of authority and escalation paths embedded in the plan.
5. Roles and responsibilities must be assigned at the work package level, not at the phase level — real-world PMs assign broad ownership to departments; PMI requires granular accountability tied to the WBS.

**Ethics Angle**

- **Responsibility:** The PM must plan for adequate resources — creating an unrealistic plan to win approval and then scrambling violates the obligation to make reasonable, achievable commitments.
- **Fairness:** RACI assignments must reflect actual competency requirements, not favoritism or organizational politics; every resource deserves role clarity.
- **Honesty:** Resource availability assumptions embedded in the plan must be validated — presenting optimistic assumptions as facts to stakeholders is a honesty violation.
- **Respect:** The Team Charter process must respect team input; imposing working agreements without team participation violates respect for persons.

### 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Resource Management Plan | Output of Plan Resource Management; documents how resources are identified, acquired, managed, and released | Do not confuse with the Resource Breakdown Structure — the plan is the governance framework, the RBS is the hierarchical catalog |
| Team Charter | Document establishing team values, agreements, ground rules, and operating norms; created collaboratively | Exam often frames this as the PM's responsibility alone — it is a team exercise |
| RACI Matrix | Responsibility Assignment Matrix showing Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed for each activity | Only one person can be Accountable (A) per task — multiple A's is a RACI violation PMI will test |
| OBS (Organizational Breakdown Structure) | Hierarchical representation of the project organization arranged to illustrate the relationship between work packages and the performing organizational units | Do not confuse with OBS and WBS — crossing them produces the RAM |
| RBS (Resource Breakdown Structure) | Hierarchical list of resources by category and type (not to be confused with Risk Breakdown Structure) | The acronym RBS is shared — context determines which one is meant; resource context = Resource Breakdown Structure |
| RAM (Responsibility Assignment Matrix) | Cross-reference of OBS and WBS showing resource assignments; RACI is one format of a RAM | Do not treat RACI and RAM as synonyms — RACI is a type of RAM |
| Resource Calendar | Document identifying working days, shifts, availability, and capacity of each resource | Confusion trap: resource calendar is an input to Estimate Activity Resources and Schedule processes, but its development begins here |
| Competency Framework | Catalog of required skills, knowledge, and experience levels mapped to roles in the resource plan | Exam tests whether you distinguish competency gaps (development need) from resource gaps (acquisition need) |
| Colocation / Virtual Teams | Colocation = placing team members physically together; virtual = geographically dispersed using technology | PMI recognizes both; virtual teams expand the resource pool but require explicit communication planning |
| Make-or-Buy Analysis | Decision framework for determining whether to staff internally or acquire external resources/contractors | Belongs to Plan Resource Management AND Procurement planning — expect cross-domain scenario questions |
| Halo Effect | Promoting someone to PM because they are technically excellent, not because they have PM competency | Named in PMBOK context — a recognition error in resource planning that creates execution risk |
| Tight Matrix | Physical colocation of team to improve communication — same as war room concept | Do not confuse with matrix organizational structure — these are unrelated concepts sharing similar terminology |

### 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| "The project has been authorized and you are starting planning. Resources have not been identified yet." | Develop the Resource Management Plan before identifying or acquiring any resources | PMI sequence: plan before acquire — the plan defines the acquisition strategy |
| "Team members disagree on working hours, overtime policies, and communication norms." | Facilitate a team charter session to establish working agreements collaboratively | Team Charter resolves behavioral and operational ambiguity; PM facilitates, does not dictate |
| "A resource pool is shared across three projects and availability is unclear." | Develop resource calendars and negotiate availability with the resource manager | Resource calendars capture availability constraints that must be reflected in the plan |
| "The project requires specialized skills not available in the organization." | Conduct a make-or-buy analysis and update the Resource Management Plan accordingly | Make-or-buy is a planning decision that shapes whether acquisition goes through HR or Procurement |
| "Roles and responsibilities are ambiguous and team members are duplicating work." | Create or update the RACI matrix to clarify accountabilities at the work package level | RACI eliminates role ambiguity by assigning specific accountability to one person per deliverable |
| "A functional manager refuses to commit resources to the project plan." | Escalate to the project sponsor and document the constraint in the resource plan | The sponsor has authority to negotiate across organizational boundaries; the PM documents reality |
| "Stakeholders want to know who is responsible for quality reviews." | Reference the RAM/RACI and update stakeholder communications to reflect accountabilities | The RAM is the authoritative source for role-to-deliverable accountability |
| "A new regulation requires certified specialists on certain tasks." | Update the resource plan to include competency requirements and certification as selection criteria | Regulatory requirements are a constraint that must be captured in the Resource Management Plan |

**Escalation / Weak-Spot Sub-Table**

| Scenario Signal | Correct Escalation Pattern |
|---|---|
| "Resource manager will not release team members despite sponsor approval." | Escalate to sponsor with documented impact — do not negotiate indefinitely at PM level |
| "Team charter was created by the PM alone and team members are non-compliant." | Reconvene the team to co-create working agreements; top-down charters lack buy-in and violate PMI collaborative norms |

### 7. Tailoring Considerations

**Predictive Project**
- Formal Resource Management Plan created before execution; approved and baselined
- Full RACI matrix developed at work-package level; OBS and RBS cross-referenced
- Resource calendars negotiated and locked with functional managers before schedule baseline
- Formal escalation paths documented for resource conflicts

**Agile / Hybrid**
- Team Charter replaces or supplements the formal resource plan for execution-level teams
- Self-organization principles limit how prescriptive role definitions can be — avoid over-specifying in a way that violates agile values
- Capacity planning done per sprint/iteration; resource calendars are rolling-wave, not fixed
- Hybrid: governance roles (sponsor, PM, Product Owner) defined predictively; delivery team roles defined agile-style

**Industry / Regulatory Factors**
- **Banking / Capital Markets / BSEC:** Regulatory-mandated roles (compliance officer, risk officer, BSEC-designated responsible person) must appear explicitly in RACI and the resource plan — omitting them is an audit finding
- **Construction / Engineering:** Physical resource planning dominates; equipment procurement lead times must be embedded in resource calendars
- **IT / Software:** Competency framework entries must reflect certification requirements (cloud, security clearance) that affect both acquisition timelines and cost
- **Highly regulated environments:** Training requirements for specialized roles must be captured in the resource plan as schedule and cost constraints, not afterthoughts

### 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Begin acquiring team members immediately after project charter is signed" | Experienced PMs know speed matters and want to lock down talent early before others claim it | Acquiring before planning violates PMI sequencing — the Resource Management Plan must precede acquisition to define what you are acquiring and why |
| "Assign the most technically skilled person as team lead" | Real-world instinct is to put the best technical performer in charge to maximize output quality | This is the Halo Effect — PMI rejects conflating technical competency with leadership or management competency |
| "The PM should draft the Team Charter and distribute it to the team" | PMs are responsible for project documentation; issuing a charter is a normal PM task | Team Charter must be created collaboratively — PMI requires team participation to generate buy-in and shared ownership |
| "Use a single RACI matrix with multiple Accountable owners per task to share responsibility" | Shared accountability feels fair and distributes risk | PMI requires exactly one Accountable person per task — multiple A's create diffusion of responsibility and are a RACI error |
| "Physical resources and team resources can be managed using the same plan structure" | Resources are resources — a single unified plan is simpler to maintain | PMI distinguishes team resources (development, motivation, conflict) from physical resources (logistics, procurement, inventory) with different management approaches |
| "Skip the Resource Management Plan for small projects; assign resources informally" | Small-project pragmatism is real; formal plans feel bureaucratic for limited scope | PMI expects appropriate planning for all projects — tailoring means simplifying the plan, not eliminating it |
| "Resource planning is complete once roles and responsibilities are documented in the RACI" | RACI feels comprehensive — it covers who does what | RACI is one component; the Resource Management Plan also includes acquisition strategy, training plans, team development approach, recognition systems, and release criteria |
| "The OBS and WBS are the same document organized differently" | Both are hierarchical breakdowns of the project and look structurally similar | OBS breaks down the organization; WBS breaks down the work — they intersect in the RAM but are fundamentally different structures |

### 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

**Q1**

A project manager in a large financial institution has been tasked with a regulatory compliance project. The project sponsor has verbally approved the resource plan, but the HR director has not formally reviewed it. During a planning meeting, the PM informs all stakeholders that resource availability is confirmed and the timeline is locked. Three weeks later, HR disputes two key resource assignments that conflict with existing commitments. The PM had presented the plan as confirmed without disclosing that HR approval was still pending. Which action by the PM represents the most significant ethical failure?

A. Proceeding with planning before resource availability was formally confirmed
B. Presenting unconfirmed resource commitments as finalized to stakeholders
C. Failing to escalate the HR conflict to the project sponsor
D. Not creating a formal risk entry for resource availability uncertainty

**Q2**

An agile team is beginning a new product increment. The Scrum Master has drafted a team charter and sent it to all team members via email with a request to "review and confirm." Two developers have raised concerns about the proposed working hours but received no response. The sprint begins and interpersonal friction escalates. What should the Scrum Master do FIRST?

A. Escalate the interpersonal conflict to the Product Owner as a sprint risk
B. Pause sprint activities and convene the team to co-create the working agreements
C. Update the team charter based on the developers' email feedback and redistribute
D. Document the conflict in the impediment backlog and address it after the sprint

**Q3**

A hybrid project has a predictively-defined governance structure and an agile delivery team. The PM has created a RACI matrix covering the sponsor, steering committee, and key technical leads, but left delivery team assignments undefined, assuming the agile team would self-organize. Three sprints in, the delivery team is escalating work package approval decisions to the PM because they have no documented authority. What should the PM have done during planning to prevent this?

A. Converted the entire project to a predictive approach to ensure full RACI coverage
B. Extended the RACI to include delivery team authority for sprint-level decisions
C. Delegated all authority questions to the Scrum Master during sprint execution
D. Documented escalation triggers only for sponsor-level decisions in the governance plan

**Q4**

A project manager is creating the Resource Management Plan for a software development project. The organization uses a weak matrix structure. The plan identifies required competencies, acquisition sources, and training needs. Which two components, if omitted, would most critically undermine the plan's utility during execution?

A. A recognition and rewards framework tied to project milestones
B. A RACI matrix aligned to WBS work packages
C. A resource release plan specifying when and how resources are transitioned off the project
D. An organizational chart showing the project team hierarchy

**Q5**

An agile team is planning the first release cycle. The team needs to sequence the following resource planning activities correctly. Arrange them in the proper PMI-aligned order:
1. Identify required competencies per user story category
2. Establish team working agreements via Team Charter
3. Confirm resource availability and capacity per sprint
4. Conduct retrospective to update team norms
5. Align team capacity to release roadmap velocity targets

A. 2, 1, 5, 3, 4
B. 1, 2, 3, 5, 4
C. 1, 5, 2, 3, 4
D. 2, 5, 1, 3, 4

**Q6**

Which of the following is NOT an output of the Plan Resource Management process?

A. Resource Management Plan
B. Team Charter
C. Resource Requirements
D. Changes to the Project Management Plan (for Resource)

**Q7**

A project manager is under significant schedule pressure from the sponsor. To secure approval for the project plan, the PM knowingly inflates the skill level of two team members in the Resource Management Plan, listing them as certified specialists when they hold only foundational credentials. The plan is approved. Weeks later, quality defects emerge tied to those work packages. What is the PM's primary ethical obligation at this point?

A. Quietly arrange for the team members to obtain certifications before the defects are formally reported
B. Disclose the misrepresentation to the sponsor and steering committee immediately, even if it results in personal consequences
C. Assign the defective work packages to genuinely qualified resources and update the plan going forward
D. Report the issue only if the defects escalate to a formal audit finding

**Q8**

A hybrid project team is distributed across three time zones. The PM has completed a Resource Management Plan that specifies role assignments and acquisition sources. Team members in the Asia-Pacific region report they are consistently excluded from critical decisions because synchronous meetings are scheduled in North American business hours. The Team Charter does not address asynchronous communication norms. What should the PM do NEXT?

A. Update the communications management plan to specify meeting rotation schedules
B. Reconvene the team to update the Team Charter with asynchronous working agreements
C. Escalate the time-zone inequity to the project sponsor as a resource risk
D. Accept the situation as a geographic constraint and document it in the risk register

**Q9**

A project manager leading a capital markets technology project is in the planning phase. A regulatory directive requires that a BSEC-designated compliance officer be assigned to review all trading algorithm deliverables. The compliance officer reports to a separate division and is not listed in the current OBS or RACI. The functional manager of the compliance division has verbally agreed to support the project but will not commit in writing. What is the BEST course of action?

A. Proceed with planning using the verbal commitment; document it in meeting notes as evidence
B. Escalate to the project sponsor to formalize the compliance resource commitment and update the OBS, RACI, and Resource Management Plan
C. List the compliance officer role as a risk in the risk register and continue planning
D. Remove the compliance review requirement from the plan until a formal commitment is obtained

**Q10**

A project manager is reviewing the Resource Management Plan for a large infrastructure project. The plan was developed six months ago and the project is now in execution. Which THREE of the following conditions would most justify updating the Resource Management Plan?

A. A new regulatory requirement mandates additional safety certifications for two roles
B. A team member completes an optional professional development course unrelated to the project
C. A functional manager withdraws two resources due to a higher-priority organizational initiative
D. The project schedule is compressed, requiring a change in resource acquisition timing
E. The team adopts a new internal communication tool that the charter does not address
F. A minor change in office seating arrangements affects one team member

### 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

**Q1**
**Correct Answer:** B
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 — Stewardship domain; PMI Code of Ethics — Honesty. Presenting unconfirmed resource commitments as finalized is a deliberate misrepresentation that harms stakeholder decision-making and violates the honesty obligation in the PMI Code. Option A is an error in sequencing but not an ethical breach per se. Option C (failure to escalate) is a process failure, not an ethics violation at the same severity. Option D is a risk management gap, not an honesty violation.
**Mindset Lesson:** PMI distinguishes process errors from ethical breaches — misrepresenting facts to stakeholders is always the primary ethical failure, regardless of what other process gaps exist.

**Q2**
**Correct Answer:** B
**PMI Justification:** Agile Practice Guide — Team Formation; PMI Collaborative leadership principle. A Team Charter created without genuine participation lacks legitimacy. The Scrum Master must pause and facilitate co-creation. Option A escalates prematurely; Option C (unilateral update by Scrum Master) repeats the original mistake; Option D defers resolution, letting conflict compound.
**Mindset Lesson:** Agile team working agreements derive their authority from collaborative creation — unilateral charters, regardless of intent, fail by design.

**Q3**
**Correct Answer:** B
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 6/7 — Roles and Responsibilities documentation; Resource Management Plan scope. In a hybrid project, the RACI must cover all governance and execution-level decisions. Leaving agile team authority undefined creates an escalation vacuum. Option A over-corrects by abandoning hybrid approach. Option C defers to the Scrum Master without formal authority documentation. Option D only covers sponsor-level escalation, ignoring execution-level ambiguity.
**Mindset Lesson:** Hybrid project RACI matrices must span both governance and delivery tiers — gaps at either level create execution friction.

**Q4**
**Correct Answer:** B and C
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 6 — Resource Management Plan components. The RACI aligned to WBS work packages is the accountability backbone of the entire resource plan; without it, execution has no authoritative role reference. The resource release plan is critical in a weak matrix because resources return to functional managers — unmanaged releases cause project disruption and damage organizational relationships. Option A (recognition) and Option D (org chart) are useful but not critically load-bearing.
**Mindset Lesson:** The two components most often omitted in real projects — RACI granularity and release planning — are also the two that create the most execution chaos when missing.

**Q5**
**Correct Answer:** B
**PMI Justification:** Agile Practice Guide — iteration planning sequence. Competency identification informs what skills to seek (1); Team Charter establishes the behavioral operating environment (2); capacity is then confirmed against that structure (3); velocity targets are aligned to confirmed capacity (5); retrospective refines norms based on experience (4). This follows the PMI plan-then-execute-then-improve cycle adapted for agile.
**Mindset Lesson:** Even in agile, competency identification precedes behavioral agreements — you need to know what the team must be before defining how they will work.

**Q6**
**Correct Answer:** C
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 6 — Process outputs. Resource Requirements is the output of Estimate Activity Resources, not Plan Resource Management. Plan Resource Management outputs are: Resource Management Plan and Team Charter. Options A, B, and D are all legitimate outputs or updates associated with the Plan Resource Management process.
**Mindset Lesson:** Output misattribution is a standard PMI exam trap — know which process produces which output, not just which Knowledge Area contains it.

**Q7**
**Correct Answer:** B
**PMI Justification:** PMI Code of Ethics — Honesty and Responsibility. The PM made a deliberate misrepresentation. The obligation is immediate, transparent disclosure regardless of consequences — this is the PMI honesty hardline. Option A (quiet remediation) perpetuates the cover-up. Option C (reassignment) addresses symptoms without disclosing the root cause. Option D (wait for audit) delays an obligation that exists now.
**Mindset Lesson:** PMI hardline: the obligation to disclose a misrepresentation is unconditional and immediate — consequences to the PM are irrelevant to the ethical obligation.

**Q8**
**Correct Answer:** B
**PMI Justification:** Agile Practice Guide — Team Charter; PMBOK 7 — Team performance domain. The Team Charter is the appropriate vehicle for working agreements including communication norms. The identified gap is in team working agreements, not the communications plan. Option A addresses the symptom (scheduling) without resolving the behavioral norm gap. Option C misframes an internal equity issue as a risk. Option D accepts an avoidable problem.
**Mindset Lesson:** When execution reveals a gap in team working agreements, the Team Charter is the document to update — not the risk register and not the communications plan.

**Q9**
**Correct Answer:** B
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 6 — Resource Management Plan; PMI Code of Ethics — Responsibility. A regulatory mandate requires the role to be formally incorporated into governance documents. Verbal commitments are insufficient for regulatory compliance roles. The sponsor has cross-organizational authority to formalize commitments. Option A relies on insufficient evidence for a regulatory obligation. Option C defers a known planning gap into risk management without resolving it. Option D removes a regulatory requirement, which is impermissible.
**Mindset Lesson:** Regulatory resource requirements must be formally documented in the Resource Management Plan with verified commitments — verbal agreements do not satisfy planning rigor or audit standards.

**Q10**
**Correct Answer:** A, C, D
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 6 — Resource Management Plan updates. A (new regulatory certification requirements) changes the competency requirements in the plan. C (resource withdrawal) changes availability and acquisition strategy — a material plan change. D (schedule compression) changes acquisition timing and may trigger make-or-buy reconsideration. Option B (optional personal development) does not affect project resource requirements. Option E (communication tool change) affects the communications plan and potentially the Team Charter, not the Resource Management Plan. Option F (seating arrangement) has no material impact on the plan.
**Mindset Lesson:** Update the Resource Management Plan when changes affect competency requirements, resource availability, or acquisition strategy — not for every operational adjustment.

### 12. 60-Second Recap Card

- Plan Resource Management = the governance framework for all resource decisions; precedes acquisition
- Primary outputs: Resource Management Plan + Team Charter
- RACI rule: exactly one Accountable per task — no exceptions
- Team Charter must be co-created by the team, not issued by the PM
- OBS (org breakdown) x WBS = RAM; RACI is one RAM format
- RBS = Resource Breakdown Structure (not Risk); context determines meaning
- Physical resources and team resources require different management approaches
- Resource calendars capture availability constraints that feed scheduling and cost processes
- Make-or-buy analysis is a resource planning AND procurement planning input
- Weak matrix = PM has limited resource authority; sponsor escalation is the correct path for resource conflicts
- Regulatory roles (BSEC compliance officer, certified specialists) must be formally documented with verified commitments in the plan
- **Burn-in reflex: Plan before acquire, collaborate before charter, formalize before commit — the Resource Management Plan is the contract between the PM and the organization on how resources will be governed.**
