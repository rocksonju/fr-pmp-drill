# PMP Exam Master Brief: Develop Team

---

### 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Develop Team is the Executing process that builds the project team's competencies, enhances team member interaction, and improves the overall team environment to deliver better project performance. Where Acquire Resources brings people onto the project, Develop Team transforms those people into a high-performing, cohesive unit. The process encompasses individual training, team-building activities, co-location or virtual collaboration tools, recognition and rewards, and structured assessment of team effectiveness. The Tuckman Ladder model — Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing, and Adjourning — is the canonical PMI framework for understanding team development stages, and the PM's behavioral approach must adapt to each stage. Develop Team carries significant cross-domain bleed into Leadership (People domain), Communications Management, and Quality Management (team competency directly affects deliverable quality). Exam weight falls within the People domain, which accounts for approximately 42% of total exam questions, making resource and team topics among the highest-weighted categories on the PMP. In high-skill-scarcity environments like capital markets and fintech in Bangladesh, Develop Team is a strategic imperative — retaining and developing scarce talent reduces attrition risk and builds institutional knowledge. Emotional intelligence, servant leadership, and psychological safety are PMI's preferred behavioral lenses for this process. **In one sentence:** Develop Team builds individual competencies, strengthens team cohesion, and creates the environment in which people perform at their best — converting a group of acquired resources into a project delivery team.

### 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

| Lens | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Predictive | Formal training plans tied to role requirements; co-location used for high-coordination roles; structured team-building events; individual performance assessments feed lessons learned and resource management updates |
| Agile | Team self-organization is the primary development mechanism; retrospectives surface development needs continuously; servant leadership creates psychological safety for honest feedback and improvement; cross-functional skill sharing replaces formal training programs |
| Hybrid | Formal individual development plans for specialist roles combined with agile retrospective-driven continuous improvement; co-location for the core team with structured virtual collaboration protocols for distributed members |

**Top 5 PMI-isms**

1. The PM is a servant leader, not a taskmaster — PMI expects PMs to remove obstacles, provide support, and create enabling environments; real-world PMs often default to directing and controlling team behavior.
2. Conflict in a team is healthy and expected — PMI treats the Storming stage as necessary and productive when managed constructively; experienced PMs often try to eliminate conflict rather than channel it.
3. Recognition must be tailored to the individual — PMI expects PMs to understand what motivates each team member; real-world PMs often default to generic monetary rewards or public recognition that may not suit everyone.
4. Team development is a continuous responsibility, not a one-time onboarding event — PMI expects ongoing investment in team capability throughout the project; practitioners often treat team development as a kick-off activity.
5. Co-location and virtual teams require different management approaches — PMI does not treat remote work as inherently inferior but recognizes it requires deliberate structure; real-world PMs often apply the same management approach regardless of team geography.

**Ethics Angle**

- Respect: Team development activities must respect individual learning styles, cultural backgrounds, and personal circumstances; one-size-fits-all approaches that marginalize certain team members violate respect.
- Fairness: Training, recognition, and development opportunities must be distributed fairly and based on project need, not personal favoritism.
- Honesty: Performance assessments must be accurate and constructive; inflated assessments to avoid difficult conversations are a honesty violation with downstream quality consequences.
- Responsibility: The PM is responsible for creating the conditions for team high performance; failing to invest in team development when the project has capacity is a responsibility gap.

### 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Tuckman Ladder — Forming | Initial stage: team members are polite, uncertain, testing boundaries, and dependent on the PM for direction | Forming begins at Acquire Resources (team assembly) and is addressed in Develop Team; it is not a one-day event |
| Tuckman Ladder — Storming | Conflict stage: team members push back on structure, compete for roles, and challenge the PM's approach | PMI expects the PM to manage conflict constructively here, not suppress it; suppression delays progress to Norming |
| Tuckman Ladder — Norming | Cohesion stage: team develops norms, begins collaborative working, and accepts each other's roles | The PM's role shifts from directing to supporting; micro-management at Norming is counterproductive |
| Tuckman Ladder — Performing | High-performance stage: team is self-directed, interdependent, and producing at high quality | The PM's role is to remove obstacles and protect the team from interference, not to add process overhead |
| Tuckman Ladder — Adjourning | Closure stage: team disbands after project completion or team member departure | Adjourning triggers team emotional responses; the PM must acknowledge contributions and manage transition |
| Co-location | Placing team members in the same physical workspace to increase communication speed and cohesion | Co-location is a Develop Team technique, not just a logistical arrangement; it has specific performance benefits |
| Virtual Team | A team that operates primarily without face-to-face interaction; requires deliberate communication and collaboration structure | Virtual teams are not inherently less effective — they require different, not fewer, PM behaviors |
| Team Charter | A document establishing team values, agreements, and operating guidelines; created collaboratively by the team | Team charter is a tool/technique of Develop Team; it is not the same as the project charter |
| Recognition and Rewards | Planned system for acknowledging individual and team performance in ways that motivate continued effort | Recognition must be tailored; monetary rewards are not always the most effective motivator |
| Emotional Intelligence (EI) | The ability to identify, assess, and manage personal emotions and those of others; a core PM leadership competency in Develop Team | High EI enables the PM to navigate team conflict, build trust, and adapt leadership style — it is not optional soft skill |
| Training | Formal or informal activities that improve team member skills and competencies; planned in Develop Team | Training is a cost of conformance (prevention) in COQ terms; it reduces defect risk downstream |
| Team Performance Assessments | Evaluations of team effectiveness over time; an output of Develop Team used to adjust development activities | Output of Develop Team, not Acquire Resources; assess the team's performance as a unit, not just individuals |

### 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| New team is formed and members do not know each other | Conduct a team kickoff to establish roles, norms, and expectations | Forming stage requires direction and structure from the PM; jumping into work without this creates confusion |
| Team conflict is escalating between two members | Facilitate a structured discussion to understand underlying interests | Storming requires constructive conflict management, not suppression or avoidance; the PM mediates first |
| Team is producing high-quality output with minimal PM direction | Reduce directive oversight and focus on obstacle removal | Performing stage team needs autonomy and protection from interference, not more management |
| Team member lacks a skill required for upcoming work | Plan targeted training or pair the member with a mentor | Develop Team includes planned training; addressing skill gaps proactively is prevention, not reaction |
| Remote team member feels disconnected and engagement is declining | Review virtual collaboration protocols and implement targeted engagement activities | Virtual team isolation is a Develop Team problem with a process solution; dismissing it as a personal issue is incorrect |
| Team morale is low after a project setback | Acknowledge the setback, re-establish shared purpose, and recognize recent contributions | PMI's servant leadership model requires the PM to address team morale as a project performance factor |
| Two team members are in Storming-stage conflict over work approach | Do not immediately impose a resolution; facilitate dialogue to reach shared agreement | Imposed solutions in Storming create resentment and delay Norming; facilitated agreement builds lasting cohesion |
| Senior team member dominates discussions and silences junior members | Create explicit psychological safety mechanisms (structured turn-taking, anonymous input channels) | Psychological safety is a Develop Team output; allowing silencing behavior undermines team performance |

**Escalation / Weak-Spot Scenarios**

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do |
|---|---|
| Persistent interpersonal conflict between team members is not resolving through facilitation | Escalate to HR or the sponsor if the conflict is affecting project deliverables and direct facilitation has failed |
| Team member performance is below standard despite training and coaching | Document the performance gap formally and engage the functional manager and HR; do not absorb indefinitely |

### 6. Visual Memory Aid

```
DEVELOP TEAM — TUCKMAN PROGRESSION & PM BEHAVIOR

FORMING -----> STORMING -----> NORMING -----> PERFORMING -----> ADJOURNING
  |               |               |               |                  |
PM: Direct    PM: Coach      PM: Support    PM: Delegate       PM: Recognize
  |               |               |               |                  |
Set norms    Manage conflict  Build cohesion  Remove blockers   Celebrate
Team charter  Facilitate      Reinforce norms  Protect team     Transition
Clarify roles  dialogue        Share wins      Autonomy         Lessons learned
```

**Mnemonic:** **F S N P A** — "Five Stages Need PM Adaptation"

**Memory hook:** "Forming Storms Norms Perform Adjourn" — the team lifecycle that every PM must navigate with a different leadership posture at each stage.

**Unpack:**
- F = Forming → Direct leadership, team charter, clear roles
- S = Storming → Coaching, conflict facilitation, do NOT suppress
- N = Norming → Supporting, reinforce collaboration norms
- P = Performing → Delegating, obstacle removal, protect autonomy
- A = Adjourning → Recognize contributions, manage emotional transition

### 7. Tailoring Considerations

**Predictive Project**
- Formal training plans are created upfront and budgeted as prevention costs.
- Team-building events are scheduled milestones.
- Co-location decisions are made during resource planning and executed in Develop Team.
- Individual performance assessments feed the performance reporting cycle.

**Agile / Hybrid**
- Retrospectives are the primary team development mechanism — they surface both process and team dynamic issues.
- Servant leadership and psychological safety are non-negotiable team environment standards.
- Cross-training and pair programming reduce single points of failure and build team resilience.
- In hybrid, formal development plans for specialist roles coexist with agile self-organization for the delivery team.

**Industry / Regulatory Factors**
- In Bangladesh's banking and capital markets sector, Develop Team activities must account for high talent demand and limited supply — retention through development is a competitive imperative, not a nice-to-have.
- BSEC-regulated fintech projects often deploy teams across Dhaka, Singapore, and offshore locations; virtual team development protocols must explicitly address cultural communication norms.
- Compliance and risk teams in regulated industries require ongoing CPD (Continuing Professional Development) that the PM must factor into team availability and development planning.
- In Islamic banking projects, team development activities must respect prayer times, fasting periods, and halal compliance in social/team-building events — cultural sensitivity is a PMI Respect obligation.

### 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| Suppress team conflict to maintain harmony | Experienced PMs value team cohesion and associate conflict with dysfunction | PMI treats Storming as a necessary developmental stage; suppression delays Norming and leaves underlying tensions unresolved |
| Use public recognition (team announcements) for all high performers | Public recognition seems universally positive and motivating | Recognition must be tailored; some individuals find public recognition uncomfortable or culturally inappropriate; undifferentiated recognition can breed resentment among peers |
| Assign the most skilled team member to mentor struggling members without their input | Efficient use of internal expertise; leverages team knowledge | Imposing mentorship without consent disrespects both parties; the mentor's billable project hours are also affected, creating a resource impact |
| Wait for team performance issues to escalate before addressing them | Intervention only when necessary avoids micromanagement | PMI expects proactive team development; waiting for escalation allows small dynamics issues to become performance crises |
| Treat co-located and virtual teams identically | Fairness principle; no discrimination by location | Virtual teams have structurally different communication and cohesion dynamics that require different PM behaviors; ignoring the difference is negligent, not fair |
| Measure team development success solely by deliverable quality metrics | Deliverables are the bottom line; PM accountability is for outputs | Team development produces team performance assessments as a distinct output; deliverable quality is a lagging indicator, not a real-time team health measure |
| Skip the team charter because the project charter already defines roles | Team charter and project charter seem duplicative to experienced PMs | The team charter is a behavioral and operating agreement created by the team, for the team; it covers norms, communication preferences, and conflict protocols that the project charter does not address |

### 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

A PM at a commercial bank is leading a core banking migration project. One team member — a highly skilled legacy system specialist — consistently delivers excellent individual work but regularly interrupts and dismisses junior team members in team meetings, creating an environment where junior members have stopped contributing ideas. The specialist's functional manager has told the PM informally that the specialist's behavior "comes with the territory" and should be tolerated given the technical value they provide. The PM has observed that two junior team members have reduced their engagement significantly. What should the PM do FIRST?

A. Tolerate the behavior since the specialist's technical contribution is critical to the project's success.
B. Speak privately with the specialist to address the specific behaviors, explain the impact on team performance, and set clear expectations.
C. Immediately raise a formal HR complaint against the specialist to document the behavior.
D. Reassign the junior team members to different tasks so they are not in direct interaction with the specialist.

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

An agile team delivering a digital payments feature at a fintech startup is in its fourth sprint. During the retrospective, three team members raise concerns that the team's norms around code review are not being consistently followed — some members skip reviews under time pressure, creating technical debt. The team lead says the issue has been raised before but "nothing changes." The PM is observing the retrospective. What is the BEST action?

A. Take over the retrospective and mandate that code review compliance will be tracked as a team KPI going forward.
B. After the retrospective, add a code review compliance metric to the sprint dashboard without informing the team.
C. Encourage the team to update the team charter to explicitly define code review expectations and create a team-owned accountability mechanism.
D. Raise a formal change request to add code review compliance as a quality gate controlled by the PM.

**Q3 — FIRST Action Conflict (Hybrid)**

A hybrid project PM notices that after a recent team reorganization, the project team has entered a new Storming phase — previously resolved role boundaries are being contested again, and two senior team members are openly disagreeing in stakeholder meetings. The PM's sponsor asks the PM to "just sort it out quickly" because the disagreements are creating a poor impression with external stakeholders. What should the PM do FIRST?

A. Ask the two senior team members to cease public disagreements immediately and resolve their issues privately.
B. Facilitate a structured team session to re-establish roles, clarify accountabilities, and rebuild shared working agreements.
C. Escalate to HR since the conflict has reached a level that is visible to external stakeholders.
D. Replace one of the conflicting team members to eliminate the source of tension.

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

A PM has just completed the Acquire Resources process for a six-month financial system implementation project. The team includes three experienced developers, two junior analysts, and a senior architect. The PM is designing the Develop Team approach. The sponsor is pressing for quick delivery and questions whether "team-building activities" are a good use of project time and budget. Which response BEST reflects PMI's position?

A. Team development activities directly increase team productivity and reduce rework costs; they are an investment, not a discretionary cost, and should be maintained.
B. Team development is optional for experienced teams; since three team members are senior, structured development activities can be minimized.
C. The sponsor's delivery pressure takes precedence; defer team development to the project's second phase when schedule pressure eases.
D. Team development is a PM responsibility defined in the resource management plan; sponsor preference does not override planned processes without a formal change request.

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

An agile PM is onboarding a new cross-functional team for a regulatory compliance platform project. Place the following Develop Team activities in the correct PMI-aligned sequence:

A. Conduct targeted training for team members with identified skill gaps.
B. Hold a team kickoff to establish shared purpose, initial norms, and working agreements.
C. Facilitate the team charter creation session.
D. Assess individual team member competency levels against project role requirements.

What is the correct order?

A. B → C → D → A
B. D → B → C → A
C. B → D → C → A
D. D → C → B → A

**Q6 — EXCEPT / NOT**

Which of the following is NOT a tool or technique of the Develop Team process?

A. Co-location
B. Team charter
C. Multi-criteria decision analysis
D. Recognition and rewards

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

A PM at an investment bank discovers that a team member's performance assessment, written by the team lead, significantly overstates the individual's contributions to avoid a difficult conversation before annual performance reviews. The team lead argues that the inflated assessment "protects team morale." The PM is aware that the same team member's work has required significant rework by other team members, creating hidden quality costs. What must the PM do?

A. Accept the assessment since the team lead is responsible for individual performance evaluations and the PM should not interfere.
B. Require that the performance assessment be corrected to accurately reflect the team member's contributions and the rework impact, regardless of the timing.
C. Note the concern in the lessons learned register and raise it after the project is complete to avoid disrupting team morale during delivery.
D. Discuss the concern privately with the team lead and accept whatever decision the team lead makes, since it is their direct report.

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

A hybrid project team is delivering a new AML transaction monitoring system. The core development team is in Dhaka; a compliance specialist and a data scientist are working remotely from Chittagong and Singapore respectively. After two months, the Dhaka team reports that they feel the remote members are "out of the loop" and making assumptions that create rework. The remote members say they do not receive context for decisions made in the Dhaka office. What is the BEST Develop Team response?

A. Require the remote members to travel to Dhaka for a monthly co-location week.
B. Review and update the team charter and communications plan to define explicit information-sharing protocols, decision documentation requirements, and inclusive meeting practices.
C. Assign a Dhaka-based team member as a "liaison" to brief the remote members after each meeting.
D. Move all team communication to asynchronous channels to give remote members equal access to information.

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

A PM on a hybrid trade settlement project has been coaching a mid-level developer for six weeks on both technical skills and collaborative working behaviors. Despite the coaching, the developer's code quality remains below the team's standard and the developer is becoming increasingly defensive in code reviews, refusing to accept peer feedback. Other team members have started avoiding assigning review tasks to this developer. What is the NEXT appropriate step?

A. Continue coaching for another month before taking any additional action.
B. Reassign the developer to documentation tasks where peer review is not required.
C. Formally document the performance gap, engage the functional manager and HR, and develop a structured performance improvement plan.
D. Allow the team to exclude the developer from code review responsibilities permanently.

### 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

**Q1**
**Correct Answer:** B
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 People domain / Code of Ethics (Respect, Responsibility). Psychological safety is a prerequisite for team performance; behavior that silences team members is a team development problem, not a tolerable personality trait. Tolerating the behavior (A) prioritizes individual technical value over team health and junior member dignity — a Respect violation. An immediate formal HR complaint (C) skips the PM's direct responsibility to address interpersonal dynamics first. Reassigning junior members (D) avoids the problem and penalizes the victim. The correct PMI action is private, direct, specific behavioral coaching.
**Mindset Lesson:** Psychological safety is a team performance requirement — individual technical excellence does not excuse behavior that silences teammates.

**Q2**
**Correct Answer:** C
**PMI Justification:** Agile Practice Guide / PMBOK 7 People domain. Team charter-based accountability is the agile-aligned solution because it gives the team ownership of the norm and the enforcement mechanism. PM-imposed KPIs (A) undermine team self-organization and create compliance rather than commitment. Unilateral dashboard changes (B) bypass the team and are not transparent. A formal change request (D) applies a predictive control mechanism to what is fundamentally a team behavioral issue.
**Mindset Lesson:** In agile, team behavioral norms are owned and enforced by the team — the PM facilitates the agreement, not the mandate.

**Q3**
**Correct Answer:** B
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 People domain / Tuckman Ladder. A new Storming phase following reorganization requires constructive conflict facilitation, not suppression or elimination. Ordering the team to cease public disagreements (A) suppresses Storming without resolving it, delaying progress to Norming. HR escalation (C) is premature for a team dynamic issue that the PM has not yet attempted to resolve. Replacing a team member (D) is a disproportionate response that creates additional instability.
**Mindset Lesson:** Storming, even when re-triggered by reorganization, requires facilitated re-norming — suppressing visible conflict does not resolve the underlying dynamic.

**Q4**
**Correct Answer:** A and D (A is the primary PMI answer; D is the escalation anchor)
**Correct Answer:** A
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 People domain / COQ principles. Team development activities are a prevention cost — they reduce rework, improve cohesion, and increase throughput. PMI's position is that they generate positive ROI and are not optional for high-performing delivery. Option B incorrectly assumes experience eliminates development needs. Option C defers a current-phase need inappropriately. Option D is technically correct but is a process argument rather than the compelling performance case that PMI prefers.
**Mindset Lesson:** Team development is an investment in delivery performance, not a discretionary cost — PMI positions it as essential to achieving project objectives.

**Q5**
**Correct Answer:** B (D → B → C → A)
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 People domain / Develop Team process sequence. Competency assessment (D) identifies gaps before the team convenes. Kickoff (B) establishes shared purpose and initial cohesion. Team charter (C) formalizes the behavioral agreements the team will live by. Targeted training (A) addresses specific gaps identified in the assessment. Starting with the kickoff before assessment (A option) creates an inefficient sequence — you cannot train for gaps you have not yet identified.
**Mindset Lesson:** Team development sequence: assess capabilities → establish shared purpose → formalize norms → train for gaps.

**Q6**
**Correct Answer:** C
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 / Process Groups: A Practice Guide. Multi-criteria decision analysis is a tool of Acquire Resources (used to select between resource options), not Develop Team. Co-location (A), team charter (B), and recognition and rewards (D) are all tools/techniques of Develop Team. This is a high-frequency exam trap that tests the boundary between Acquire Resources tools and Develop Team tools.
**Mindset Lesson:** Multi-criteria decision analysis belongs to Acquire Resources — Develop Team tools focus on building competency, cohesion, and environment, not on selecting resources.

**Q7**
**Correct Answer:** B
**PMI Justification:** PMI Code of Ethics (Honesty, Responsibility) / PMBOK 7 People domain. An inaccurate performance assessment that conceals quality costs is a honesty violation with downstream consequences for resource management and project performance data. Deferring to the team lead (A and D) subordinates the PM's ethical responsibility to organizational hierarchy. Noting the concern in lessons learned (C) defers a current ethical obligation to a future point, allowing the inaccurate data to be formally recorded in the project record.
**Mindset Lesson:** Performance assessments are project documents that must be accurate — inflated assessments to protect feelings violate honesty and distort project performance data.

**Q8**
**Correct Answer:** B
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 People / Communications domains / Develop Team principles. The root cause is a structural communications gap — decisions are being made and context is being created in one location without being systematically shared. Updating the team charter and communications plan addresses the structural cause. Mandatory travel (A) is an expensive and potentially disrespectful imposition on remote members. A liaison (C) creates a bottleneck and information filter. All-async communication (D) eliminates real-time collaboration that is valuable for complex problem-solving.
**Mindset Lesson:** Distributed team information gaps are communications design problems — fix the protocol, not the people.

**Q9**
**Correct Answer:** C
**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 People domain / Code of Ethics (Responsibility, Fairness). Six weeks of documented coaching without improvement, combined with defensive behavior and team avoidance, constitutes a performance issue that has exceeded the PM's individual coaching capacity. Continuing to coach without escalation (A) allows the problem to compound and is unfair to other team members absorbing the impact. Reassigning to avoid the problem (B and D) does not address the performance issue and is unfair to the individual, who deserves a structured improvement opportunity. Formal performance management with HR is the correct escalation path.
**Mindset Lesson:** When coaching fails to produce improvement after a documented period, formal performance management is the responsible path — not continued tolerance or avoidance.

**Q10**

### 12. 60-Second Recap Card

- Develop Team is in Executing: it builds competencies, improves team dynamics, and creates high-performance environments.
- Tuckman Ladder: Forming (direct) → Storming (coach/facilitate) → Norming (support) → Performing (delegate/protect) → Adjourning (recognize).
- PMI expects the PM to adapt leadership posture at each Tuckman stage — the same approach does not work across all stages.
- Conflict in Storming is healthy and necessary — suppress it and the team never reaches Norming.
- Recognition and rewards must be tailored to individual preferences; monetary rewards are not universally motivating.
- Team charter is a Develop Team output — it is the team's own behavioral contract, not the PM's directive.
- Team performance assessments are the output — they evaluate the team as a collective unit, not just individuals.
- Virtual teams require deliberate communication design; isolation is a process gap, not a personal failing.
- Co-location is a Develop Team technique with documented cohesion benefits; it is not merely a logistics decision.
- In regulated, talent-scarce environments (BSEC, Bangladesh Bank), team development is also a retention strategy — invest continuously.
- **When the team is in conflict, your first move is always to facilitate constructive dialogue — never suppress, never impose, never skip the Storming stage.**
