# PMP Exam Master Brief: Determine Budget

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Determine Budget is the Cost Management process of aggregating estimated costs of individual activities or work packages to establish an authorized cost baseline. It sits in the Planning Process Group and produces the cost baseline plus project funding requirements — the two outputs that gate all future cost monitoring and control. The process consumes cost estimates from Estimate Costs, the scope baseline, project schedule, resource calendars, risk registers, and agreements to produce a time-phased spending profile. Exam weight for Cost Management is roughly 8–10% of the 180-question exam, but budget questions bleed into Earned Value Management (EVM), change control, funding limit reconciliation, and stakeholder communication scenarios. In banking and capital markets contexts — BSEC-regulated product builds, Bangladesh Bank core-banking modernizations — the cost baseline also maps to regulatory capital allocation and audit trail requirements, making accuracy a compliance imperative, not just a planning nicety. The distinction between the cost baseline (S-curve) and project budget (baseline + management reserve) is the single most-tested concept in this process. Confusing contingency reserve (in baseline, owned by PM) with management reserve (outside baseline, owned by sponsor/organization) causes cascading wrong answers. **In one sentence:** Determine Budget rolls up all cost estimates into a time-phased authorized cost baseline that becomes the yardstick for all future cost performance measurement.

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Predictive | Full cost baseline established upfront; funding requests submitted in tranches aligned to schedule milestones; change control governs any re-baselining |
| Agile | High-level budget fixed (fixed-cost contract or program increment budget); teams work within sprint/PI cost envelopes; detailed cost roll-up tracked per iteration not per activity |
| Hybrid | Predictive baseline for infrastructure/regulatory components; rolling-wave budget refinement for agile delivery streams; separate management reserves for each track |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Contingency reserve belongs in the cost baseline; management reserve does not.** Real-world instinct: "put a buffer in the budget and call it reserve." PMI instinct: the type of reserve determines what it funds (known-unknowns vs unknown-unknowns) and who controls it.
2. **The cost baseline is time-phased, not a lump sum.** Real-world PMs hand over a total number. PMI requires an S-curve tied to the schedule, enabling period-by-period EVM measurement.
3. **Funding limit reconciliation may force schedule changes.** Real-world instinct: adjust budget to match schedule. PMI: if funding limits constrain cash flow, you re-sequence activities — not renegotiate the budget silently.
4. **Cost aggregation flows bottom-up from work packages through control accounts.** Real-world instinct: top-down allocation. PMI: validated estimates roll up through the WBS; top-down is an early rough order of magnitude, not the Determine Budget output.
5. **Historical information and analogous data are inputs, not substitutes for estimates.** Real-world instinct: use last year's actuals and add 10%. PMI: use documented estimating techniques (bottom-up, parametric) and record basis of estimates for auditability.

### Ethics Angle

- **Responsibility:** Budget padding beyond legitimate contingency to protect team from sponsor pressure violates the PM's responsibility to provide accurate information.
- **Honesty:** Presenting a cost baseline you know is under-funded to win approval is dishonest — Code of Ethics §4 (Honesty) requires proactively sharing information that stakeholders would want to know.
- **Fairness:** Allocating contingency reserves disproportionately to politically visible work packages over high-risk ones undermines fair risk treatment.
- **Respect:** Overriding sponsor-controlled management reserve without authorization disrespects governance boundaries.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Cost Baseline | Time-phased authorized budget (work package estimates + contingency reserves) against which project cost performance is measured | Does NOT include management reserve — adding MR gives project budget, not baseline |
| Project Budget | Cost Baseline + Management Reserve | Trap: using "budget" and "baseline" interchangeably; PMI treats them as distinct |
| Contingency Reserve | Budget for known-unknown risks (identified in risk register); part of cost baseline; PM-controlled | Trap: calling contingency a "buffer" — it must be tied to specific risk events |
| Management Reserve | Budget for unknown-unknowns; NOT in cost baseline; controlled by sponsor/org; requires formal change to access | Trap: PM spending MR without authorization — must go through integrated change control |
| Funding Limit Reconciliation | Comparing planned expenditure against funding availability; may require rescheduling work to stay within funding tranches | Trap: adjusting the cost baseline rather than the schedule when funding limits are hit |
| Cost Aggregation | Rolling up work package cost estimates through WBS control accounts to project level | Trap: skipping control account level — aggregation goes WP → CA → project |
| BAC (Budget at Completion) | Total planned value of all work = sum of all planned values at completion | Trap: BAC = cost baseline total (excludes MR); used as denominator in EAC formulas |
| EAC (Estimate at Completion) | AC + ETC; multiple formulas depending on variance assumption | Trap: choosing wrong EAC formula — if CPI expected to continue: EAC = BAC/CPI |
| S-Curve | Cumulative cost baseline plotted over time; slow start, steep middle, flat end | Trap: a flat budget line is not a cost baseline — PMI baseline must be time-phased |
| Basis of Estimates | Documentation explaining how estimates were derived (assumptions, tools, confidence level) | Trap: skipping this output — auditors and reviewers require it for validation |
| Cost Performance Index (CPI) | EV / AC | Trap: CPI > 1 means under budget (good), not over — direction confuses many |
| Analogous Estimating | Uses historical project data; quick but less accurate; appropriate early in project | Trap: using analogous for the final cost baseline — PMI expects bottom-up for detailed planning |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| "The sponsor wants to add funds above the approved baseline" | Assess whether the addition is for identified risks (contingency) or unknowns (MR) and document accordingly | PMI: fund type determines where it goes; don't merge reserves without categorization |
| "Funding runs out before project completion per the schedule" | Perform funding limit reconciliation and adjust activity start dates | PMI: rescheduling work is the correct response to funding limits, not re-estimating costs |
| "A new risk is identified after the baseline is approved" | Update the risk register and evaluate whether contingency reserve adjustment requires a change request | New risks require formal change control to touch the approved baseline |
| "The team's actual costs are running 15% over plan" | Calculate CPI, analyze cause, forecast EAC, and report status — do not immediately request more budget | PMI: measure first, diagnose second, escalate with data third |
| "Management asks for a cost estimate before WBS is complete" | Provide a rough order of magnitude (ROM) with clearly stated assumptions and confidence range | Providing a number without caveats anchors expectations falsely |
| "Sponsor questions why management reserve is not in the cost baseline" | Explain that MR covers unknown-unknowns and requires sponsor authorization to access, keeping it outside PM-controlled baseline | PMI governance: separation of reserve types protects both PM and sponsor |
| "The project budget has been approved but individual work packages exceed estimates" | Review contingency reserves for those work packages before requesting more funding | Use contingency before escalating — that is its purpose |
| "Team lead wants to borrow from another work package's contingency" | Reject the informal transfer; any cross-WP contingency movement requires change request | PMI: reserves are tied to specific risks; informal transfers undermine control |

**Topic-specific escalation edge cases:**

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| "PM realizes the approved cost baseline is unachievable due to market cost increases" | Document variance, prepare change request with updated estimates and schedule impact, present to CCB | PMI: never work silently outside the baseline; escalate through change control with full analysis |
| "Bangladesh Bank regulatory audit requires detailed cost allocation per system component" | Ensure cost baseline is structured at control account level matching regulatory reporting categories before audit | Regulatory context: traceability from baseline to deliverable is a compliance requirement |

```
DETERMINE BUDGET — Decision Flow

Cost Estimates (per WP)
        |
        v
  Aggregate by WBS
  (WP → Control Account → Project)
        |
        v
  Add Contingency Reserves (known-unknowns)
        |
        v
  = COST BASELINE (time-phased S-curve)
        |
        v
  Perform Funding Limit Reconciliation
  [Planned spend > funding tranche?]
        |              |
       YES             NO
        |              |
  Reschedule        Proceed
  activities
        |
        v
  Add Management Reserve (unknown-unknowns)
        |
        v
  = PROJECT BUDGET
        |
        v
  Document Funding Requirements
  (periodic + total)
```

**Mnemonic: "CAFE-R"**
- **C** — Cost estimates aggregated
- **A** — Add contingency (known risks)
- **F** — Funding limit reconciliation
- **E** — Establish baseline (S-curve)
- **R** — Reserve (management) added → full budget

Memory hook: "Running a CAFE-R needs a budget before opening day."

Unpack: C→A gives the cost baseline; F ensures cash flow matches funding tranches; E formalizes the time-phased baseline; R adds the management reserve to complete the project budget. Each letter is a sequential step — skip F and you'll face cash shortfalls mid-project even with a valid baseline.

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project
- Full bottom-up cost aggregation from finalized WBS before project execution begins
- Cost baseline approved through formal governance gate; changes require CCB approval
- Contingency reserve amounts derived from quantitative risk analysis (Monte Carlo outputs)
- Funding tranches aligned to phase gates or milestone-based payment schedules
- Basis of estimates documented and version-controlled for audit

### Agile / Hybrid
- Budget often fixed at program increment or release level; teams work within envelope
- No activity-level cost baseline; velocity and capacity drive cost tracking per sprint
- Rolling-wave refinement: outer iterations have ROM budgets; near-term iterations have detailed budgets
- Hybrid: infrastructure stream uses predictive baseline; feature delivery stream uses capacity-based budgeting
- Cost burn tracked against release backlog budget, not WBS-based baseline

### Industry / Regulatory Factors
- **Bangladesh Bank / Core Banking Systems:** Regulatory capital models require cost traceability to system components; cost baseline must map to regulatory reporting categories
- **BSEC-regulated projects (capital market infrastructure):** Procurement regulations may require cost estimates and baselines submitted with bid documents; unauthorized cost overruns trigger regulatory disclosure requirements
- **Banking / Capital Markets generally:** Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) analysis required alongside project cost baseline; operational cost handoff to BAU must be documented at project close
- **Audit readiness:** Cost baseline with basis of estimates is primary audit artifact; gaps in documentation can trigger regulatory findings independent of actual cost performance
- **Funding source restrictions:** Donor-funded or foreign-currency-funded projects (common in Bangladesh development banking) require separate cost baselines per funding tranche to comply with disbursement conditions

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Add management reserve to the cost baseline when approving budget" | Real-world: "budget" means the total pot including all reserves | PMI: MR is outside the cost baseline by definition; merging them eliminates governance separation and removes sponsor authority over unknowns |
| "Re-estimate the cost baseline when actual costs exceed plan" | Feels proactive; shows responsiveness to overruns | PMI: re-baselining requires formal change control; re-estimating without CCB approval is unauthorized scope to cost linkage |
| "Use analogous estimating to finalize the cost baseline" | Fast, uses real history, gets sponsor buy-in quickly | PMI: analogous is for early ROM; final baseline requires bottom-up estimates from a detailed WBS |
| "Adjust the schedule to reduce costs when contingency is depleted" | Schedule compression seems like a problem-solving move | PMI: schedule changes require integrated change control; unilateral schedule adjustments to save money bypass governance |
| "Transfer unused contingency from low-risk WPs to over-running WPs informally" | Pragmatic reallocation within total reserve pool | PMI: contingency is tied to specific identified risks; informal transfers are unauthorized changes to the baseline |
| "Funding limit reconciliation means requesting more money from the sponsor" | When money runs short, the instinct is to ask for more | PMI: first response is rescheduling activity start dates to smooth expenditure within existing limits |
| "Present the cost baseline without management reserve to appear more competitive" | Sponsors and clients often prefer lower numbers; PM wants approval | PMI Code of Ethics (Honesty): knowingly omitting material information from a stakeholder is dishonest |
| "The cost baseline is complete once all work package estimates are summed" | Summing estimates = done, right? | PMI: baseline requires time-phasing against the schedule; a lump sum is not a cost baseline |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

A PM at a Dhaka-based investment bank is finalizing the cost baseline for a BSEC-mandated trading system upgrade. Senior leadership has instructed the PM to reduce the reported contingency reserve by 40% to make the project appear more cost-competitive during board approval. The PM knows the contingency is backed by quantitative risk analysis showing a 35% probability of vendor cost escalation. The PM has already privately raised concerns with the functional manager, who said, "Just make it work." The board presentation is tomorrow.

A. Reduce contingency as instructed; document the instruction in the project file and proceed  
B. Present the full baseline with contingency justified by QRA data; inform leadership the reduction would misrepresent risk exposure  
C. Withdraw the contingency entirely and add an equivalent amount as management reserve  
D. Delay the presentation and escalate to the PMO director first

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** PMI Code of Ethics §4 (Honesty) requires PMs to proactively share information that stakeholders need to make informed decisions. A cost baseline backed by quantitative risk analysis represents material information. Presenting a reduced baseline knowing it misrepresents risk exposure violates honesty obligations regardless of organizational pressure. Option A documents the instruction but still participates in the misrepresentation — documentation does not absolve ethical breach. Option C is creative but dishonest in effect — moving the same reserve to a different bucket doesn't fix the misrepresentation and removes PM oversight. Option D delays rather than resolves — the presentation is tomorrow; escalating before informing the decision-making stakeholders directly is a weaker response than direct disclosure.

**Mindset Lesson:** Documentation of a bad instruction is not protection — PMI expects the PM to push back and correct material misrepresentation before it reaches decision-makers.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: A**

**PMI Justification:** Agile Practice Guide §6: In agile/hybrid environments, cost forecasting happens at the release/PI level, not sprint-by-sprint. When sprint-level estimates prove inaccurate, the correct response is to reforecast at the next planning horizon (PI planning) using updated velocity data. Option B (change request to CCB) is premature — a sprint-level variance doesn't immediately require CCB action unless it breaches release budget thresholds. Option C (informal balancing) is an unauthorized baseline change. Option D (document and ignore) violates the PM's obligation to proactively manage cost performance.

**Mindset Lesson:** In agile cost management, reforecast at the planning horizon level — sprint-level noise is managed through PI replanning, not immediate escalation.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 / Integrated Change Control: Any change to scope that impacts cost and schedule must go through formal change control. The sponsor saying "handle it within budget" is a stakeholder request, not an authorization to bypass change control. Option A is unauthorized — using contingency for out-of-scope work without a change request misappropriates reserved funds. Option C (descoping) is a significant decision that also requires change control. Option D bypasses the project's governance structure — external escalation is not the FIRST step.

**Mindset Lesson:** "Handle it within budget" from a sponsor is not change authorization — any scope addition requires a formal change request regardless of funding pressure.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 6/7 Determine Budget: The cost baseline requires (A) aggregated WP estimates with contingency tied to identified risks, and (C) time-phasing against the schedule to produce the S-curve. These two steps are what transform a list of estimates into an authorized cost baseline. Option B (ROM) is an early-stage tool, not part of finalizing the baseline. Option D (MR funding request) may happen but is not part of creating the cost baseline itself. Option E (CCB approval) occurs after the baseline is prepared, not during its construction.

**Mindset Lesson:** A cost baseline is not complete without time-phasing — a lump sum estimate is not a baseline.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: A**

**PMI Justification:** Agile Practice Guide §5 / PI Planning: The logical sequence is: estimate team capacity and cost per sprint (1) → assign backlog items based on velocity (2) → compare total planned cost to PI budget (3) → identify budget risk items (4) → obtain PO acknowledgment of constraints (5). Option B skips capacity estimation before assignment. Option C compares to budget before assigning work, which is premature. Option D assigns work before understanding team cost capacity.

**Mindset Lesson:** In agile budgeting, capacity comes before commitment — you cannot assign work to a sprint budget you haven't estimated.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: D**

**PMI Justification:** Work performance data is an output of project execution (Monitoring & Controlling processes) and is not available during Planning. Determine Budget is a Planning process; it uses cost estimates, schedule, risk register, resource calendars, scope baseline, agreements, and organizational process assets — all of which exist before execution begins. Options A, B, C, and E are all valid inputs per PMBOK.

**Mindset Lesson:** Determine Budget is a planning process — any input generated during execution (work performance data, actuals) cannot logically be an input.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: C**

**PMI Justification:** PMI Code of Ethics §4 (Honesty) and §2 (Responsibility): The PM has a responsibility to the donor agency as a key stakeholder. The discrepancy is material — BDT 6M on a donor-funded project affects disbursement compliance and financial reporting. "Monitor internally" without disclosure is a concealment strategy that violates both honesty and responsibility values. Option A follows an unethical instruction. Option B fixes the baseline but doesn't disclose to the donor — incomplete. Option D uses MR to cover a known problem without disclosure, which is deceptive.

**Mindset Lesson:** Donor/external funding contexts create higher disclosure obligations — PMI's honesty principle requires proactive disclosure to external stakeholders who have a right to the information.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 Stakeholder Performance Domain / Communications Management: Effective communication tailors information to stakeholder needs, roles, and decision-making requirements. Each audience in this scenario has different information needs: the CFO needs financial summary, the sponsor needs milestone-linked spend visibility, the technical lead needs resource-level detail, and the compliance officer needs regulatory allocation mapping. Option A (one-size-fits-all) is inefficient and risks confusing stakeholders with irrelevant detail. Option C (total budget only) is insufficient for decision-making. Option D (self-service) abdicates PM communication responsibility.

**Mindset Lesson:** Cost baseline communication is not one document to all — tailor the view to what each stakeholder needs to make decisions.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: C**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 / Measurement Performance Domain: When EAC projects a significant overrun (BDT 11M, ~10% of total project), the PM's obligation is to brief the sponsor with complete data — EAC, root cause, and corrective action options — before taking action. Option A (access MR) requires sponsor authorization and cannot happen before the sponsor is briefed. Option B (descope agile stream) is a significant decision that requires sponsor and stakeholder concurrence, not unilateral PM action. Option D (continue monitoring) fails the PM's transparency obligation — EAC trend is already clear.

**Mindset Lesson:** A double-digit EAC overrun projection demands immediate sponsor briefing with complete analysis — continued monitoring without escalation is never the right answer when the trend is statistically clear.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, B, D**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK Determine Budget outputs: (A) Cost baseline — the primary output; (B) Project funding requirements — periodic and total funding needs; (D) Project document updates, including cost estimates updates, assumption log updates, and risk register updates. Option C (contingency per risk event) is determined during Estimate Costs and documented in the risk register — it is an input to Determine Budget, not an output. Option E (cost management plan) is produced by Plan Cost Management, a prior process. Option F (basis of estimates) is an output of Estimate Costs, not Determine Budget.

**Mindset Lesson:** Determine Budget outputs are the baseline, funding requirements, and document updates — not the management plan or the estimating documentation.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

- Determine Budget aggregates cost estimates into a time-phased cost baseline (S-curve) authorized against the project schedule
- Cost baseline = work package estimates + contingency reserves (known-unknowns); excludes management reserve
- Project budget = cost baseline + management reserve (unknown-unknowns, sponsor-controlled)
- Contingency reserve: PM-controlled, tied to specific risk events in the risk register, inside the baseline
- Management reserve: org/sponsor-controlled, for unknowns, outside the baseline, requires formal change to access
- Funding limit reconciliation = reschedule activities to fit cash flow tranches — never silently re-estimate costs
- Basis of estimates documents how every estimate was derived — required for audit and governance
- Analogous estimating is for ROM; final baseline requires bottom-up estimates from the WBS
- In regulated contexts (Bangladesh Bank, BSEC), cost baseline must map to regulatory reporting categories with full traceability
- Ethics: presenting a knowingly under-funded baseline to win approval violates PMI Code of Ethics §4 (Honesty) regardless of organizational pressure

**Reflex burn-in: The cost baseline is a time-phased S-curve excluding management reserve — if the answer adds MR to the baseline or skips time-phasing, it is wrong.**
