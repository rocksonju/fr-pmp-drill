# PMP Exam Master Brief: Build a Team (People · Task 6)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Building a team is the practice of assembling the right people with the right skills, helping them form into a cohesive working unit, and progressing them through the Tuckman stages to performing. PMI treats team formation as a deliberate design activity — selecting cross-functional capability, structuring the team for collaboration, and accelerating storming/norming through ground rules and team charter. The People domain is 42% of the exam; team-building threads into leadership, performance, ground rules, and shared understanding. Topics include resource calendar, Tuckman stages, cross-functional design, hiring, team charter creation, and team launch. In real organizations, teams are assembled from available bodies; on the exam, PMI rewards the option that designs the team intentionally and accelerates team formation through structured kickoff and agreements.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about intentionally designing, assembling, and launching a team to reach performing — through skill match, cross-functional design, and structured formation.

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Team Building Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Resource plan, formal hiring, team charter at kickoff, role-based assembly |
| **Agile** | Cross-functional, T-shaped team, team agreement co-created, lightweight kickoff |
| **Hybrid** | Resource plan + cross-functional team design + agile-style working agreement |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Cross-functional > role-siloed.** Whole-team capability beats handoffs.
2. **Tuckman stages cannot be skipped.** Storming is part of formation.
3. **Team charter is co-created, not imposed.** PM-authored charter lacks team ownership.
4. **Co-location preferred when possible; virtual deliberately designed when not.**
5. **Adding people late slows delivery.** Brooks' law applies.

### Ethics Angle

- **Fairness:** Selection by competence and fit, not favoritism.
- **Respect:** Each member's input shapes team agreement.
- **Honesty:** Disclose role and expectations transparently at hiring.
- **Responsibility:** Team-formation work is PM's, even with sponsor pressure to "just start."

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Tuckman Model** | Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing → Adjourning | Trap: assuming linear; teams regress |
| **Cross-Functional Team** | All skills needed within team | Trap: assuming each individual is multi-skilled |
| **T-Shaped Team Members** | Deep in one area, broad in others | Trap: insisting all members be generalists |
| **Resource Calendar** | Availability of people over time | Trap: confusing with project schedule |
| **Resource Histogram** | Visual of resource usage over time | Trap: ignoring over/under allocation |
| **RAM (Responsibility Assignment Matrix)** | Maps roles to work; RACI is common form | Trap: confusing R and A |
| **Team Charter** | Co-created document of team values, agreements, norms | Trap: PM-authored |
| **Team Working Agreement** | Agile version of team charter | Trap: skipping co-creation |
| **Kickoff** | Formal team launch event | Trap: skipping or under-investing |
| **Co-located Team** | Same physical location | Trap: assuming required |
| **Virtual / Distributed Team** | Across locations/timezones | Trap: applying co-located practices unchanged |
| **Onboarding** | Structured introduction of new team member | Trap: skipping for "experienced" hires |
| **Brooks' Law** | Adding people to a late project makes it later (short-term) | Trap: reflex to add capacity |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Project just chartered; team not yet assembled | Build resource plan; identify cross-functional skill needs | Foundation before assembly |
| Team is **forming** | Provide directive style; share vision and roles | New teams need direction |
| Sponsor wants to **skip kickoff** to start sprint 1 | Push back; explain cost of skipping formation | Storming will be longer if skipped |
| **Critical skill** missing from team | Hire, contract, or train; identify SPOF risk | Capability gap = risk |
| Team is **stuck in storming** | Reinforce ground rules; coach through conflict | Skip not possible |
| **New member** joining mid-project | Structured onboarding; pair with mentor | Member integration affects velocity |
| Team needs to **scale up** mid-project | Caution Brooks' law; gradual addition | Adding people slows delivery short-term |
| Team is **virtual** | Design ceremonies for time zones, equity, connection | Virtual needs deliberate design |
| Team **regressed** from performing to storming | Style shift; investigate cause (change, scope, leadership) | Tuckman regression real |
| Adjourning team needs **transition** | Capture lessons, celebrate, transfer knowledge | Closure is team-building too |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Critical role cannot be filled despite PM efforts | Escalate to sponsor with skill gap, impact, options (hire, contract, defer, train) |
| Sponsor demands team members the PM did not select and who lack required skills | Engage sponsor on skill gap and impact; document; escalate to governance if pattern recurs |

## 6. Visual Memory Aid

### Team-Building Flow

```text
PROJECT CHARTERED
        │
        ▼
Identify cross-functional skill needs
        │
        ▼
Build resource plan (calendar, RAM, headcount)
        │
        ▼
Assemble team (internal, hire, contract)
        │
        ▼
Hold KICKOFF (vision, roles, principles)
        │
        ▼
Co-create TEAM CHARTER / WORKING AGREEMENT
        │
        ▼
Tuckman: Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing
        │
        ▼
Maintain through changes; adapt style;
plan ADJOURNING (lessons, celebrate, transfer)
```

### Team-Building Mnemonic: **S-A-K-A-T**

> **S**kill needs identified
> **A**ssemble cross-functional team
> **K**ickoff (vision + roles + principles)
> **A**greement co-created
> **T**uckman progression supported

Memory hook: **"Skills, Assembly, Kickoff, Agreement, Tuckman — build it on purpose."**

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Resource plan formal; hiring through HR
- Team charter signed at kickoff
- Cross-functional design less common; role-siloed teams more frequent

### Agile / Hybrid

- Cross-functional team is standard
- Working agreement co-created
- Lightweight kickoff; team agreement evolves through retrospectives

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** hiring slow due to background checks; plan early
- BSEC / Bangladesh Bank may require certified roles — verify at planning
- **Vendor teams:** integrate vendor staff into team charter; otherwise two-class system
- **Multinational:** language, time-zone, cultural design for virtual teams

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Skip kickoff to save time" | Productivity focus | Storming lengthens; team formation costs more later |
| "PM authors team charter for consistency" | Faster than co-creation | Lacks ownership; ground rules ignored |
| "Add people to recover schedule" | Capacity = throughput | Brooks' law; slows short-term |
| "Assemble team from whoever is available" | Practical | Skill gaps become risks |
| "Force team through storming with directive style" | Faster | Cannot skip storming; resurfaces later |
| "Treat virtual team like co-located" | Familiar approach | Equity and connection fail |
| "Hire for project specifics only; no T-shape" | Specialists deliver | T-shape enables collaboration |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Your sponsor instructs you to hire a specific candidate without skills assessment because "they are a friend." The candidate lacks two required skills. What should you do FIRST?

A. Hire as instructed.
B. Refuse without skills assessment; propose objective evaluation; document the skill gap impact.
C. Hire and supplement with contractor.
D. Escalate to HR.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

You are forming a new agile team. PO and sponsor want sprint 1 to start in 2 days. You have not held kickoff or co-created team agreement. What is the BEST action?

A. Start sprint 1 immediately; do formation work in parallel.
B. Negotiate a 3-day formation window: kickoff + team agreement + first backlog refinement before sprint 1.
C. Skip kickoff; rely on retrospective adjustment.
D. Run kickoff during sprint 1 standups.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

A team is forming. Two senior members disagree on the technical approach in early discussions. The team has not yet reached norming. What should you do FIRST?

A. Decide the technical approach yourself.
B. Facilitate structured disagreement; reinforce that storming is normal; coach toward shared understanding.
C. Reassign one senior.
D. Escalate to sponsor.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

You need to build a 12-person team for a 6-month project. The skills required include three rare specializations. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Identify the cross-functional skill mix needed across the project lifecycle.
B. Post all positions externally immediately.
C. Engage with functional managers and HR for internal candidates and external sourcing.
D. Start the project with 8 members and add 4 later.
E. Train existing team members on the rare specializations.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Predictive)**

Project just chartered. Arrange team-building actions:

1. Hold team kickoff covering vision, roles, principles.
2. Build resource plan and identify skill needs.
3. Co-create team charter with the team.
4. Assemble team (internal, hire, contract).

A. 2 → 4 → 1 → 3
B. 4 → 2 → 1 → 3
C. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
D. 2 → 1 → 3 → 4

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following is NOT a Tuckman stage?

A. Forming
B. Storming
C. Norming
D. Negotiating
E. Performing

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

During hiring, you discover a candidate misrepresented their experience on their resume. The candidate is otherwise strong. What do you do?

A. Hire anyway since the candidate is strong.
B. Decline the candidate; explain the basis honestly; document.
C. Hire and address misrepresentation later.
D. Negotiate a lower role.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

You are building a team with members across three time zones and two cultural contexts. What is the BEST approach to team-building?

A. Require all ceremonies during one time zone's working hours.
B. Design ceremonies and communication for time-zone equity; build cultural fluency through team agreement and structured introductions.
C. Have one ceremony per time zone separately.
D. Use written communication only.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

You cannot fill a critical compliance role despite three weeks of internal/external search. The role is required for go-live in two months. What is the BEST next step?

A. Continue searching silently.
B. Brief sponsor with the gap, the search history, the impact, and options (defer, scope adjust, contract, contract-to-hire); request decision.
C. Defer the role and assign work to other team members.
D. Cancel the project.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

A team has formed and is in storming. Members disagree openly; PM is tempted to intervene. Which THREE actions are MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Reinforce ground rules and team agreement.
B. Decide all technical disagreements yourself to speed past storming.
C. Coach team to address conflict directly; surface in retrospective.
D. Replace conflicting members to skip storming.
E. Provide structured facilitation when needed; not constant intervention.
F. Disband the team and reform.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Fairness requires skills-based hiring. Refuse + propose evaluation + document.

**Mindset Lesson:** Hiring requests outside competence basis are refused with alternative.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Three-day formation window protects sprint 1 quality without abandoning agile delivery.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sprint 1 quality depends on prior formation work.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Storming is normal; facilitation + coaching builds team.

**Mindset Lesson:** Cannot skip storming; coach through.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Skill mix identification + engaging FM/HR cover internal + external sourcing. Posting all externally skips internal candidates; staging delays cause Brooks risk; training existing members may delay.

**Mindset Lesson:** Skill mix + internal/external engagement is sourcing first move.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: A (2 → 4 → 1 → 3)**

**PMI Justification:** Plan → assemble → kickoff → charter co-creation.

**Mindset Lesson:** Cannot charter team before team exists.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: D**

**PMI Justification:** Stages are Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing, Adjourning. Negotiating is not a stage.

**Mindset Lesson:** Memorize five stages exactly.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty principle; misrepresentation disqualifies regardless of strength.

**Mindset Lesson:** Misrepresentation in hiring is non-negotiable.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Equity + fluency design.

**Mindset Lesson:** Virtual + multicultural needs design, not workaround.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Sponsor briefing with gap + history + impact + options + decision request.

**Mindset Lesson:** Critical role gap escalated with options, not absorbed silently.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Ground rules reinforce.
- C: Coach to address directly.
- E: Structured facilitation when needed.
- B: Skipping storming via PM decisions doesn't work.
- D: Replacement skips formation.
- F: Disband + reform discards investment.

**Mindset Lesson:** Storming = reinforce + coach + facilitate. Not decide, replace, or disband.

- Cross-functional > role-siloed. T-shape members ideal.
- Resource plan + skill mix identification before assembly.
- Kickoff = vision + roles + principles. Don't skip.
- Team charter / working agreement co-created. Not PM-authored.
- Brooks' law: adding people slows late projects short-term.
- Virtual + multicultural team = deliberate ceremony + communication design.
- New member onboarded with structured intro + mentor pairing.
- Hiring on competence + fit, not favoritism.
- Adjourning = lessons + celebrate + transfer.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates critical role gaps to sponsor with skill gap, search history, impact, and options — never absorbs silently, never hires outside competence basis.*
