# PMP Exam Master Brief: Engage Stakeholders (Process · Task 4)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Engaging stakeholders is the continuous discipline of identifying who is affected by or influences the project, understanding their interests, classifying their power and engagement, and moving them deliberately to productive engagement. PMI distinguishes engagement (active two-way work) from communication (information transfer) and treats engagement as a live process running from charter through closure. Topics include stakeholder identification, register, power/interest grid, salience model, engagement assessment matrix, and continuous adjustment. The Process domain is 50% of the exam; engagement is one of the most-tested topics, threading into communications, conflict, and risk. In real projects, stakeholders are managed as audience; on the exam, PMI rewards continuous engagement, especially of resistant stakeholders.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about relentlessly understanding, influencing, and adapting to the people who can make or break the project.

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Stakeholder Engagement Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Register, power/interest grid, engagement matrix, formal communication plan, change-controlled updates |
| **Agile** | PO as proxy; sprint reviews as engagement; continuous feedback; information radiators |
| **Hybrid** | Register + agile ceremonies as structured engagement touchpoints |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Engage before escalating.** Resolution at lowest level first.
2. **Communication plan ≠ engagement.** Plan + execution + monitoring.
3. **Negative stakeholders get MORE attention.** Resistance is data.
4. **PM owns engagement.** Cannot fully delegate.
5. **Understand before acting.** Diagnose why.

### Ethics Angle

- **Respect:** All stakeholders engaged equally.
- **Fairness:** Information shared consistently.
- **Honesty:** No selective disclosure to favor allies.
- **Responsibility:** PM owns engagement; absorption is failure.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Stakeholder Register** | Identity + classification + assessment | Trap: one-time artifact |
| **Power/Interest Grid** | 2×2 strategy matrix | Trap: "manage closely" = all high-power |
| **Engagement Assessment Matrix** | Current vs Desired engagement | Trap: confusing current with desired |
| **Communication Management Plan** | What, when, how, who | Trap: plan ≠ execution |
| **Pull vs Push Communication** | Recipient-driven vs sender-driven | Trap: matching to convenience |
| **RACI Matrix** | Responsible/Accountable/Consulted/Informed | Trap: multiple A |
| **Salience Model** | Power + Legitimacy + Urgency | Trap: power alone |
| **5C's of Communication** | Correct/Concise/Clear/Coherent/Complete | Trap: skipping Concise |
| **Active Listening** | Paraphrase + confirm | Trap: hearing only |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Stakeholder **unaware** | Provide targeted info + invite engagement | Cannot engage unaware |
| Stakeholder **resistant** | Meet to understand root cause | Resistance = unmet need |
| Two stakeholders **conflict** | Joint session; collaborative resolution | Don't pick sides |
| Stakeholder **escalates** to sponsor | Don't retaliate; meet stakeholder; understand | Bypass = communication gap |
| Team says stakeholder gives **wrong info** | Validate first | Don't act on second-hand |
| Sprint review feedback **ignored** | Raise in retro; adjust agreement | Retro = improvement engine |
| **New stakeholder** mid-project | Register; assess; engage | Late identification ≠ reduced need |
| Stakeholder requests **format change** | Update plan; honor request | Tailor to preference |
| **Sponsor unavailable** | Assess urgency; document; use governance | Don't make unilateral decisions |
| Stakeholder **stopped responding** | Proactively reach out; investigate | Silence ≠ disinterest |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Issue cannot be resolved at team level after genuine attempts | Escalate to PM → sponsor if needed; document |
| Sponsor is source of conflict | Escalate to steering committee/governance — not around sponsor informally |

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Formal register; engagement at phase gates
- Pre-defined escalation paths

### Agile / Hybrid

- PO as proxy; sprint reviews = engagement
- Continuous; information radiators

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** BSEC, Bangladesh Bank formal stakeholders; salience model
- **Government:** political stakeholders with high power
- **Multinational:** cultural engagement norms

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why PMs Pick It | Why PMI Rejects |
|---|---|---|
| "Escalate immediately" | Authority | Abdication |
| "Send status report to resistant" | Communication | Reports are push; resistance needs dialogue |
| "Exclude difficult stakeholder" | Productive meetings | All stakeholders engaged |
| "Update plan at closure" | Lessons | Living document |
| "Delegate engagement to team" | Distributed | PM owns |
| "Document and move on" | Risk discipline | Documenting ≠ resolving |
| "Inform only when bad news confirmed" | Accuracy | Proactive required |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Sponsor instructs you to exclude a regulatory stakeholder from status meetings because "they slow everything." Regulator has approval authority. Three weeks later, regulator contacts you frustrated. What should you do FIRST?

A. Follow sponsor's instruction.
B. Meet privately with regulator; understand concerns; re-evaluate communication approach.
C. Escalate to steering immediately.
D. Update plan and present to sponsor for approval.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

Sprint reviews held consistently; business stakeholders rarely attend; when they do, minimal feedback. Team making product decisions independently. PO concerned about drift. What is the BEST action?

A. Cancel sprint reviews; UAT at end.
B. Investigate why stakeholders not attending; work with PO to increase value and accessibility.
C. Allow team to continue (implicit delegation).
D. Escalate to management for mandatory attendance.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

Senior stakeholder initially supportive has become resistant after learning agile won't produce formal requirements doc. Lobbying others against project. Sponsor asked you to handle. What should you do FIRST?

A. Update plan to mark stakeholder as threat.
B. Meet to understand concerns; explore tailored artifact options.
C. Ask sponsor to formally communicate approach.
D. Document resistance in risk register.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

Two key stakeholders received conflicting scope info — one from team, one from vendor. Both making decisions on different assumptions. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Joint meeting; single source of truth.
B. Issue formal scope clarification memo immediately.
C. Escalate vendor communication to legal.
D. Review and update communications plan to prevent recurrence.
E. Suspend all stakeholder communications.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

Sprint review feedback reveals feature doesn't meet needs. PO disagrees. Stakeholders insisting on changes. Arrange:

1. Facilitate conversation between PO and stakeholders on user needs.
2. Add rework to backlog if changes agreed.
3. Root cause analysis on misalignment.
4. Update Definition of Done with earlier stakeholder validation.

A. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
B. 3 → 1 → 2 → 4
C. 1 → 3 → 2 → 4
D. 2 → 1 → 4 → 3

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which is NOT a valid input into stakeholder engagement planning?

A. Project charter
B. Stakeholder register
C. Lessons learned register
D. Risk report
E. Team performance assessment

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

3 months into 12-month project. Key deliverable delayed 6 weeks due to underestimated dependency. Sponsor has board presentation in 2 weeks where project cited as on-track. Sponsor asks you to keep this quiet. What do you do?

A. Agree to delay disclosure since sponsor has authority.
B. Tell sponsor you cannot withhold material information; prepare updated status report.
C. Disclose to board without informing sponsor first.
D. Document sponsor's request and delay by two weeks.

---

**Q8 — Agile, Communication Strategy**

Agile team building trading platform. Regulators require formal monthly status. Business stakeholders prefer real-time. Team uses kanban accessible to all. What is the BEST communication approach?

A. Monthly reports for all to satisfy regulators.
B. Discontinue kanban as it doesn't satisfy regulators.
C. Maintain kanban + monthly formal reports for regulators — tailor.
D. Ask regulators to waive formal requirement.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

Conflict between two business unit leaders over resource allocation. Two facilitated sessions failed. Both report to sponsor. One leader threatens to escalate to sponsor directly. What is the BEST next step?

A. Allow the leader to escalate.
B. Proactively brief sponsor with conflict, attempts, request involvement.
C. Escalate to steering committee.
D. One more facilitation; escalate if fails.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

Stakeholder engagement scores declined over last three sprints. Sprint review attendance dropped; feedback weakening. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Investigate what's causing the decline before corrective action.
B. Shorten sprint reviews to make less demanding.
C. Work with PO to make reviews more relevant and outcome-focused.
D. Replace sprint reviews with formal monthly report.
E. Add standing retrospective agenda item to evaluate engagement.
F. Escalate decline to sponsor; request mandatory attendance.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Regulator with approval authority cannot ethically be excluded. Understand stakeholder perspective first.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sponsor instruction conflicting with stakeholder ethics → engage affected stakeholder first.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Declining attendance is engagement design problem.

**Mindset Lesson:** Weak engagement is system problem; PMI never blames stakeholder.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Diagnose before prescribing; tailored artifacts may address.

**Mindset Lesson:** Resistance is symptom; diagnose first.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and D**

**PMI Justification:** Joint meeting + plan update = corrective + preventive.

**Mindset Lesson:** Conflict of information requires meeting + plan update — both.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: A (1 → 2 → 3 → 4)**

**PMI Justification:** Align → backlog → root cause → DoD.

**Mindset Lesson:** Alignment first; process improvement after.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** Team performance assessment is People domain output, not input to engagement.

**Mindset Lesson:** Know which process generates what.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty principle absolute.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sponsor authority does not override ethical obligation.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: C**

**PMI Justification:** Tailoring satisfies all audiences.

**Mindset Lesson:** Tailoring = matching tool to audience.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Proactive sponsor brief after exhausted attempts.

**Mindset Lesson:** Escalation is active management, not reaction.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Diagnose.
- C: Make reviews more relevant.
- E: Embed in retrospective.
- B: Shortening reduces value.
- D: Replace ceremony with waterfall.
- F: Mandatory attendance coercive.

**Mindset Lesson:** Engagement decline = diagnose + relevance + retro embed.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**ENGAGE STAKEHOLDERS — Exam Morning Card**

- Engagement is continuous, not file-and-forget.
- U-R-N-S-L: Unaware → Resistant → Neutral → Supportive → Leading.
- Resistant = highest priority.
- Escalation sequence: Resolve → Facilitate → Sponsor → Steering. No level jump.
- Communication plan failure = PM's fault. Tailor to audience.
- Agile: sprint review = engagement; declining attendance = process problem.
- Ethics override sponsor instructions. Always.
- New stakeholder mid-project → register, assess, engage.
- "Inform" rarely best when dialogue possible.
- Understand FIRST. Act SECOND. Document THIRD.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates as last resort, never first instinct, always with documentation.*
