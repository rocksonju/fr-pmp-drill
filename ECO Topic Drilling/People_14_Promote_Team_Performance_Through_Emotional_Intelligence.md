# PMP Exam Master Brief: Promote Team Performance Through Emotional Intelligence (People · Task 14)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Emotional intelligence (EI) is the developable skill of recognizing and managing one's own emotions and recognizing and influencing others' emotions to produce better outcomes. PMI treats EI as a foundational PM competency — the multiplier on every other People-domain skill. The four EI domains (per Daniel Goleman) are self-awareness, self-management, social awareness (empathy), and relationship management. The People domain is 42% of the exam; EI threads into leadership, conflict, communications, and team performance. Topics include emotional self-awareness, regulating reactions, reading group dynamics, empathy, conflict de-escalation, and motivating others. In real teams, EI is treated as personality; on the exam, PMI rewards the option that demonstrates EI as deliberate practice — pause, observe, choose response.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about deliberate practice of self-awareness, self-management, empathy, and relationship management to multiply team performance.

---

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | EI in Practice Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Formal stakeholder analysis; conflict resolution through structured process |
| **Agile** | Continuous attunement in ceremonies; retrospective surfacing of dynamics; empathy in PO/team interactions |
| **Hybrid** | Structured analysis + continuous attunement |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **EI is developable, not innate.** Practice; do not delegate to personality.
2. **Self-awareness first.** You cannot regulate what you don't recognize.
3. **Pause before reacting.** Emotional response is reflex; EI is choice.
4. **Empathy ≠ agreement.** Understanding the other side does not require capitulation.
5. **Read the room.** Dynamics often matter more than words.

### Ethics Angle

- **Respect:** Empathy is respect in action.
- **Honesty:** Honest feedback delivered with EI; sugar-coating fails Honesty.
- **Fairness:** EI without favoritism; same empathy for difficult and easy stakeholders.
- **Responsibility:** EI failures are owned; emotional reactions managed.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Emotional Intelligence (EI)** | Recognize + manage own + others' emotions | Trap: confusing with charisma |
| **Self-Awareness** | Recognize own emotions and triggers | Trap: assuming self-knowledge |
| **Self-Management** | Regulate reactions; pause before responding | Trap: suppression ≠ management |
| **Social Awareness (Empathy)** | Recognize others' emotions and context | Trap: empathy = agreement |
| **Relationship Management** | Influence positively through interaction | Trap: manipulation |
| **Active Listening** | Paraphrase + confirm + reflect | Trap: hearing words ≠ listening |
| **Empathy** | Understanding another's perspective | Trap: cognitive empathy ≠ sympathy |
| **De-escalation** | Lower emotional intensity to enable dialogue | Trap: jumping to logic when emotion high |
| **Pause Practice** | Brief interval between stimulus and response | Trap: reflex response |
| **Tone Matching / Mirroring** | Aligning energy and pace to build rapport | Trap: mimicry |
| **Conflict De-escalation** | Lower emotional intensity in conflict | Trap: forcing problem-solving while emotion high |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| You feel **triggered** in a meeting | Pause; name internally; respond from choice not reflex | Self-management |
| Team member is **visibly upset** | Acknowledge emotion; provide space; do not jump to logic | De-escalation |
| Stakeholder is **angry** | Active listening; paraphrase; show understanding before responding | Empathy + de-escalation |
| **Group dynamic** is tense | Read the room; name dynamic if helpful; adjust | Social awareness |
| You **reacted poorly** to feedback | Reflect; own publicly if appropriate; apologize | Self-awareness + Responsibility |
| Conflict escalates with **emotions high** | De-escalate first; logic after emotion settled | Emotion first, logic after |
| Sponsor is **stressed** | Acknowledge stress; tailor communication; reduce friction | Empathy with sponsor |
| Team member **withdraws** after critique | Engage 1:1; explore impact of feedback; adjust delivery | Self-awareness + relationship |
| You notice **your own bias** influencing decision | Surface; pause; seek diverse input | Self-awareness |
| Stakeholder **disagrees** strongly | Empathize before counter; understanding before persuading | Empathy ≠ agreement |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Sustained emotional volatility affecting team performance | Engage individual; if unresolved, joint conversation with functional manager / HR with empathy and care |
| Sponsor emotional reactivity damaging stakeholder relationships | Engage sponsor privately; offer EI-informed reframing; escalate to next governance level if persisting |

## 6. Visual Memory Aid

### Four EI Domains (Goleman)

```text
                   SELF                OTHER
RECOGNIZE   →   Self-awareness      Social awareness
                (notice own         (empathy, read room)
                 emotion + trigger)

MANAGE      →   Self-management     Relationship management
                (regulate response, (influence positively,
                 pause, choose)      build rapport)
```

### EI Mnemonic: **S-S-S-R**

> **S**elf-awareness (notice own)
> **S**elf-management (regulate)
> **S**ocial awareness (read others)
> **R**elationship management (influence)

Memory hook: **"SSSR — Sense yourself, Steer yourself, Sense others, Relate."**

### Pause Practice: **N-A-R**

> **N**otice (the trigger)
> **A**cknowledge (internally)
> **R**espond (from choice, not reflex)

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Formal stakeholder analysis; EI in structured interactions
- Less continuous attunement; more episodic

### Agile / Hybrid

- Continuous attunement in ceremonies
- Retrospective surfacing of dynamics

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** hierarchical context can suppress EI signals; build deliberately
- **Multinational:** emotional expression norms vary; calibrate
- **High-pressure deadlines:** EI critical when stress runs high

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Stick to logic; emotions are noise" | Rationality | Ignoring emotion fails de-escalation |
| "React quickly to show responsiveness" | Decisiveness | Reflex ≠ choice; pause is EI |
| "Sugar-coat feedback to preserve relationship" | Politeness | Dishonest + ineffective |
| "Empathize means agreeing" | Conflict avoidance | Empathy is understanding, not agreement |
| "EI is personality; can't develop" | Defeatism | EI is skill |
| "Suppress own emotions" | Self-control | Suppression ≠ management |
| "Manipulate emotions for outcome" | Influence | Crosses into manipulation |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

In a steering meeting, the sponsor makes a comment that triggers you personally. You feel anger rising. The sponsor is awaiting your response on a project decision. What should you do FIRST?

A. React immediately to assert position.
B. Pause internally; notice the trigger; respond from choice — address the decision factually; address the personal comment privately after if needed.
C. Walk out of the meeting.
D. Defer the decision indefinitely.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

During retrospective, a team member becomes visibly upset and starts speaking emotionally about a missed deadline. Others are silent. What is the BEST action?

A. Cut off the emotional comment.
B. Acknowledge the emotion; provide space; de-escalate ("I hear how frustrating this has been"); when settled, return to structured retro discussion.
C. End the retrospective.
D. Ask the upset member to leave.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

Two stakeholders are in heated conflict during a planning session. Both are talking over each other. Other stakeholders are uncomfortable. What should you do FIRST?

A. Pick a side.
B. De-escalate: pause the session; acknowledge emotion in the room; name the dynamic; offer to reconvene after both have cooled; structure subsequent conversation around interests.
C. Continue the session.
D. Cancel the project.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

You realize after a feedback conversation that you delivered critique poorly and the team member withdrew. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Reflect on what triggered the poor delivery; identify pattern.
B. Ignore and continue.
C. Hold a 1:1 with the team member; own the impact; restore relationship.
D. Defend your delivery.
E. Send written apology only.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

You feel triggered by a stakeholder comment. Arrange:

1. Respond from choice with factual content.
2. Notice the trigger internally.
3. Acknowledge the emotion (internally).
4. If appropriate, address the personal comment privately after.

A. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
B. 2 → 3 → 1 → 4
C. 3 → 2 → 1 → 4
D. 4 → 1 → 2 → 3

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following is NOT one of Goleman's four EI domains?

A. Self-awareness
B. Self-management
C. Social awareness
D. Relationship management
E. Intellectual rigor

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

A team member shares emotional vulnerability about a personal challenge during 1:1. The sponsor later asks you about the team member's recent dip in output. What do you do?

A. Share the personal information to explain.
B. Provide honest aggregated performance picture; acknowledge dip is being addressed privately; decline to disclose personal circumstance without consent.
C. Deny the dip.
D. Volunteer the personal information.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

A stakeholder consistently sends angry, demanding emails. You have responded factually. The dynamic is not improving. What is the BEST approach?

A. Continue factual responses.
B. Move to a synchronous conversation; lead with empathy and active listening; surface underlying interest behind the demands; address interest if possible.
C. Stop responding.
D. Escalate to sponsor immediately.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

A team member's emotional volatility (frequent outbursts, walk-outs) has been affecting team morale for three weeks. You have engaged the team member privately twice. What is the BEST next step?

A. Continue private engagement.
B. With care, brief functional manager / HR with documented pattern, team impact, support provided; request joint action that protects both the team and the affected member.
C. Replace the team member immediately.
D. Publicly address in retrospective.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

You want to strengthen EI across your project team. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Model pause practice in your own reactions.
B. Mandate that no one express emotion in meetings.
C. Include EI-related items in retrospectives (dynamics, reactions, patterns).
D. Replace difficult team members.
E. Coach active listening and empathy explicitly.
F. Require formal EI certification.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** NAR pause + factual response + private follow-up = self-management.

**Mindset Lesson:** Trigger ≠ reflex; pause + choice.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Acknowledge → space → de-escalate → return.

**Mindset Lesson:** Emotion first; logic after.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** De-escalate + name dynamic + reconvene + structure around interests.

**Mindset Lesson:** Heated conflict needs de-escalation before structure.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Reflection + relationship restoration.

**Mindset Lesson:** Own EI failures; reflect + restore.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: B (2 → 3 → 1 → 4)**

**PMI Justification:** Notice → acknowledge → respond → private follow-up.

**Mindset Lesson:** NAR sequence + private follow-up.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** Intellectual rigor is not an EI domain.

**Mindset Lesson:** Four EI domains: self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship management.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty + Respect — aggregate truthfully, protect privacy.

**Mindset Lesson:** Vulnerability shared in 1:1 is confidential.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Sync + empathy + surface interest + address.

**Mindset Lesson:** Hostile email = move to sync with empathy and interest exploration.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Joint action with FM/HR protects team and individual.

**Mindset Lesson:** Persistent volatility joint action with care, not unilateral removal or public addressing.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Model pause practice.
- C: Retrospective EI items.
- E: Coach explicitly.
- B: No emotion mandate is suppression.
- D: Replacement avoids EI work.
- F: Certification is not behavior change.

**Mindset Lesson:** EI culture = model + retro + explicit coaching. Not mandate, replacement, or certification.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**PROMOTE TEAM PERFORMANCE THROUGH EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE — Exam Morning Card**

- SSSR: Self-aware · Self-manage · Social aware · Relationship manage.
- EI is developable, not personality.
- Pause practice: NAR (Notice, Acknowledge, Respond).
- Self-awareness first; cannot regulate what you don't see.
- Empathy ≠ agreement. Understanding before persuading.
- Read the room; dynamics often matter more than words.
- De-escalate emotion before applying logic.
- Active listening: paraphrase + confirm.
- Vulnerability in 1:1 is confidential.
- Own EI failures; reflect + restore.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates persistent emotional volatility with care, documentation, and joint FM/HR action — never absorbs damage, never replaces unilaterally, never disclosures vulnerability shared in confidence.*
