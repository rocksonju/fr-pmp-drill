# PMP Exam Master Brief: Plan and Manage Project Compliance (Business Environment · Task 1)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Planning and managing project compliance is the discipline of identifying applicable regulatory, legal, contractual, and internal policy obligations, embedding them into project deliverables and processes, and ensuring evidence of compliance is maintained for audit. PMI treats compliance as a constraint to embed proactively, not catch at the end. Topics include regulatory mapping, compliance requirements in DoD, audit trail, evidence retention, and escalation for compliance breach. The Business Environment domain is 8% of the exam; compliance is the single most-tested topic in this domain. In real banking/capital-markets projects, compliance is the dominant constraint; on the exam, PMI rewards embedding compliance into project artifacts and DoD, not separate review.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about embedding compliance into project artifacts and processes from the start, with evidence retained for audit — not catching at the end.

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Compliance Management Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Compliance plan; audits; documented evidence; sign-offs |
| **Agile** | Compliance criteria embedded in DoD; CI ensures evidence; retro improves |
| **Hybrid** | Compliance plan + DoD embedding |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Embed, don't bolt on.**
2. **Evidence retained from start.**
3. **PM owns compliance even with compliance team.**
4. **Compliance breach = immediate escalation.**
5. **Regulator stakeholders are definitive.**

### Ethics Angle

- **Honesty:** Compliance status truthful.
- **Responsibility:** PM owns continuous compliance.
- **Fairness:** Standards applied consistently.
- **Respect:** Regulators engaged respectfully.

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| **Compliance requirement** identified | Embed in DoD; capture in plan | Embed |
| **New regulation** during execution | Assess impact; update DoD; route through ICC | Adapt |
| **Audit** approaching | Verify evidence + state matches; close gaps | Readiness |
| **Compliance breach** discovered | Immediate brief; assess impact; remediate; document | Escalate |
| **Regulator stakeholder** identified | Salience-model treatment; engage | Engagement |
| **Compliance training** required | Plan and deliver | JIT |
| **Sponsor demands skip compliance** | Refuse; explain risk; document | Non-negotiable |
| **Vendor compliance** | Verify; integrate into contract | Vendor scope |
| **Records retention** | Per regulator + internal | Retention |
| **Compliance evidence** scattered | Centralize; standardize | Audit-ready |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Compliance breach with regulator exposure | Immediate brief to sponsor + compliance officer + legal with impact, remediation, regulator-engagement plan |
| Sponsor demands compliance bypass | Refuse; document; escalate to next governance level |

## 6. Visual Memory Aid

### Compliance Embedding Flow

```text
PROJECT INITIATED
        │
        ▼
Identify regulatory + contractual + internal policy obligations
        │
        ▼
Embed in PM plan + DoD + acceptance criteria
        │
        ▼
Plan evidence capture + retention
        │
        ▼
Execute: produce evidence as work happens
        │
        ▼
Periodic audit / verification
        │
        ▼
Closeout: archive evidence per retention
        │
        ▼
If breach: immediate escalation + remediation + regulator engagement
```

### Compliance Mnemonic: **I-E-E-A-A**

> **I**dentify obligations
> **E**mbed in plan + DoD
> **E**vidence captured + retained
> **A**udit periodically
> **A**rchive per retention

Memory hook: **"IEEAA — five steps of compliance discipline."**

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Sponsor instructs you to skip AML compliance review for next release to meet deadline. AML breach exposure if skipped. What should you do FIRST?

A. Skip.
B. Refuse; explain regulatory exposure; propose accelerated review; brief compliance officer; if sponsor refuses, escalate to next governance level.
C. Skip and document.
D. Defer release.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

DoD doesn't include compliance criteria. Compliance team reviewing at release end, finding issues that require rework. What is the BEST action?

A. Continue current.
B. Co-create updated DoD with team + PO + compliance team to embed compliance criteria; rework prevention.
C. Add separate compliance review.
D. Skip compliance.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

New regulation issued during execution. Affects scope. Sponsor wants to ignore until next release. What should you do FIRST?

A. Ignore.
B. Assess impact; embed in DoD; route through ICC with scope/cost/timeline impact; brief sponsor + compliance officer; if regulatory deadline, prioritize.
C. Embed silently.
D. Defer.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

Audit in 6 weeks. Compliance evidence scattered across tools. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Centralize evidence in audit-ready repository.
B. Hide gaps.
C. Audit current evidence state against regulatory requirements; remediate gaps.
D. Skip audit.
E. Backdate evidence.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

New compliance requirement identified mid-sprint. Arrange:

1. Update DoD with compliance criteria; route through ICC if baseline impact.
2. Identify regulatory source + applicability.
3. Brief team + PO + compliance officer.
4. Embed evidence capture in CI/development workflow.

A. 2 → 3 → 1 → 4
B. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
C. 4 → 2 → 1 → 3
D. 3 → 2 → 1 → 4

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which is NOT typically a compliance source?

A. Regulator (BSEC, Bangladesh Bank, etc.)
B. Contract
C. Internal policy
D. Industry standards
E. PM's personal preference

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

Regulator audit discovers a compliance breach 3 months old. The breach was internally known but not surfaced. What do you do?

A. Hide from regulator.
B. Disclose breach honestly to regulator with impact assessment + remediation plan; brief sponsor + compliance officer + legal; document.
C. Disclose only what regulator asks.
D. Backdate documentation.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

Regulator stakeholders require monthly formal reports. Sponsor wants weekly summary. Compliance officer wants real-time evidence access. What is the BEST approach?

A. Same monthly report to all.
B. Tailor: monthly formal report for regulators; weekly summary for sponsor; real-time evidence repository for compliance officer.
C. Stop reporting.
D. Combined daily report.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

Vendor failed to deliver compliance evidence per contract. Two engagement attempts. Vendor manager defers. Audit in 3 weeks. What is the BEST next step?

A. Continue engaging.
B. Brief sponsor + compliance + procurement + legal with breach evidence, contract terms, attempts, and contractual remedies; expedite vendor delivery or activate alternatives.
C. Tolerate gap.
D. Hide from audit.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

Compliance approach is reactive: review at release end produces rework. Multiple releases affected. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Embed compliance criteria in DoD with team + compliance team.
B. Add more compliance reviews at release end.
C. Build compliance evidence capture into CI/development.
D. Replace team members.
E. Engage compliance team in backlog refinement to shift left.
F. Skip compliance entirely.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Regulatory non-negotiable.

**Mindset Lesson:** Compliance breach exposure not waived by sponsor.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Embed in DoD prevents rework.

**Mindset Lesson:** Compliance shifted left through DoD.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Assess + embed + ICC + brief.

**Mindset Lesson:** New regulation assessed and embedded immediately.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Centralize + audit + remediate.

**Mindset Lesson:** Audit prep = evidence + gap remediation.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: A (2 → 3 → 1 → 4)**

**PMI Justification:** Source → brief → DoD/ICC → embed in workflow.

**Mindset Lesson:** Compliance change sequence: source → align → embed → capture.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** PM preference not compliance source.

**Mindset Lesson:** Compliance sources: regulator, contract, policy, standards.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty + immediate disclosure.

**Mindset Lesson:** Regulator breach disclosed honestly; never hidden.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Tailored audience-specific.

**Mindset Lesson:** Compliance communication tailored.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Brief multi-functional + contractual remedies.

**Mindset Lesson:** Vendor compliance breach = governance + legal engagement.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Embed in DoD.
- C: Build into CI.
- E: Engage compliance team early.
- B: More end reviews = more rework.
- D: Replacement avoids cause.
- F: Skip non-negotiable.

**Mindset Lesson:** Compliance shift-left = DoD embed + CI evidence + early compliance engagement.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**PLAN AND MANAGE PROJECT COMPLIANCE — Exam Morning Card**

- IEEAA: Identify · Embed · Evidence · Audit · Archive.
- Embed compliance in DoD + plan from start.
- Evidence captured continuously, not at end.
- PM owns compliance even with compliance team.
- Compliance breach = immediate escalation.
- Regulator stakeholders are definitive (salience).
- Records retention per regulator + internal.
- Vendor compliance verified through contract.
- Sponsor cannot waive regulatory obligations.
- Audit-ready = continuous evidence + traceability.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates compliance breaches and regulator-affecting risks immediately with impact, remediation, and regulator-engagement plan — never absorbs, never hides, never accepts sponsor bypass instructions.*
