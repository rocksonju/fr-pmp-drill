# PMP Exam Master Brief: Plan and Manage Procurement (Process · Task 11)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Planning and managing procurement is the discipline of deciding what to buy vs build, selecting contract types that match work characteristics, sourcing vendors fairly, managing performance, and closing procurement properly. PMI treats procurement as legally-bound communication — contract terms are the truth, and contract changes go through formal amendment. Topics include make-or-buy analysis, contract types (FP, CR, T&M), source selection criteria, statement of work (SOW), bidder conference, contract administration, and procurement closeout. The Process domain is 50% of the exam; procurement threads into risk, scope, schedule, and integration. In real projects, procurement is given to procurement team and forgotten; on the exam, PMI rewards PM ownership of vendor performance.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about deciding what to buy, matching contract type to scope certainty, selecting vendors fairly, and managing performance through the contract.

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Procurement Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Formal SOW; competitive bidding; FP common; full administration |
| **Agile** | T&M / ranged-scope contracts; vendor as team member; iteration acceptance |
| **Hybrid** | Mix of contract types per work characteristic |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **FP for clear scope. T&M (capped) for evolving. CR for unique.**
2. **Make-or-buy is decision, not reflex.**
3. **Contract is the truth.** Verbal commitments don't bind.
4. **PM owns vendor performance.** Not procurement team.
5. **Closeout procurement properly.** Acceptance + payment + lessons.

### Ethics Angle

- **Honesty:** Procurement representations accurate.
- **Fairness:** Source selection unbiased.
- **Responsibility:** Vendor performance is PM's.
- **Respect:** Vendor as partner, not adversary.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Make-or-Buy Analysis** | Decision: produce internally vs purchase | Trap: skipping |
| **SOW (Statement of Work)** | Procurement scope document | Trap: ambiguity |
| **RFP / RFQ / RFI** | Request for Proposal / Quotation / Information | Trap: confusing |
| **Bidder Conference** | Pre-bid meeting; all vendors equal information | Trap: selective info |
| **Source Selection Criteria** | Predefined evaluation factors | Trap: ad-hoc |
| **FP (Fixed Price)** | Seller risk high, buyer low; needs clear scope | Trap: FP for unclear |
| **FPIF (Fixed Price Incentive Fee)** | FP + incentive | Trap: complex |
| **FPEPA (Fixed Price Economic Price Adjustment)** | FP + inflation adjustment | Trap: long-term contracts |
| **CR (Cost Reimbursable)** | Buyer risk high; seller risk low | Trap: open-ended without controls |
| **CPFF (Cost Plus Fixed Fee)** | Cost + fixed fee | Trap: no incentive |
| **CPIF (Cost Plus Incentive Fee)** | Cost + incentive for performance | Trap: incentive design |
| **CPAF (Cost Plus Award Fee)** | Cost + subjective award | Trap: dispute |
| **T&M (Time and Materials)** | Hourly + materials | Trap: open-ended without cap |
| **PoP (Point of Total Assumption)** | FPIF: cost where seller assumes 100% | Trap: ignoring |
| **Contract Change Control** | Amendment process | Trap: verbal mods |
| **SLA** | Service Level Agreement | Trap: confusing with contract |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| **Make-or-buy** decision needed | Analyze cost, capability, risk, strategic | Decision not reflex |
| Vendor proposes **FP** for unclear scope | Negotiate scope clarity or change to T&M with cap | Match contract to scope |
| **Bidder conference** | All vendors equal information | Fairness |
| Source selection **subjective** | Define objective criteria upfront | Audit trail |
| Vendor **below spec** | Document; engage through contract; require correction | Same standard |
| Vendor **above spec** | Acknowledge; reward through incentive if applicable | Recognize |
| Contract change | Formal amendment; not verbal | Contract change control |
| Vendor **breached** contract | Document; engage; legal if needed; escalate | Contract |
| Procurement **closeout** | Acceptance + final payment + lessons + archive | Proper closure |
| Vendor as team member (agile) | Integrate into ceremonies + agreement | Partnership |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Vendor breach affects critical delivery | Brief sponsor + procurement + legal with breach evidence, attempts, options |
| Vendor demands contract change above PM authority | Through procurement governance per change mgmt plan |

## 6. Visual Memory Aid

### Contract Type Decision Tree

```text
WORK CHARACTERISTIC
        │
        ▼
Scope clearly defined + stable?
   YES → FIXED PRICE family (FP, FPIF, FPEPA)
   NO  ↓
        ▼
Scope unique / unknown? Cannot define upfront?
   YES → COST REIMBURSABLE family (CPFF, CPIF, CPAF)
   NO  ↓
        ▼
Need flexibility + cap? Short-term work?
   YES → TIME AND MATERIALS (with cap + max hours)
   NO  ↓
        ▼
Reassess work characteristics
```

> **S**OW + RFP
> **C**ontract type selection
> **P**erformance management
> **C**loseout

Memory hook: **"Make, SOW, Contract, Perform, Close."**

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Formal competitive bidding
- FP common
- Full administration

### Agile / Hybrid

- T&M / ranged-scope contracts
- Vendor as team member

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** vendor contracts heavily regulated
- BSEC / Bangladesh Bank: procurement records retained
- **Public-sector:** RFP-driven; tight procurement rules

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Sponsor instructs you to award contract to vendor without competitive bidding because "they're trusted." Procurement policy requires competitive process. What should you do FIRST?

A. Award as instructed.
B. Refuse; explain procurement policy; propose competitive process or formal sole-source justification with documented rationale.
C. Award and document sponsor instruction.
D. Escalate to legal.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

Vendor account manager defends slow vendor team-member delivery. Team frustrated. PO wants team-member removed. What should you do FIRST?

A. Demand removal.
B. Meet with vendor team-member to understand cause; coordinate with vendor account manager; if pattern, escalate per contract.
C. Escalate to vendor leadership.
D. Tolerate.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

Vendor proposes FP for evolving scope. Vendor account manager pushes for quick signature. What should you do FIRST?

A. Sign.
B. Negotiate scope clarity before FP or change to T&M with cap; engage procurement.
C. Sign and renegotiate later.
D. Reject.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

Vendor consistently below spec. Two engagements done. Promises improvement; defects continue. Contract hard to terminate. Release in 6 weeks. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Brief sponsor + procurement with defect data, attempts, contract risk, recommended contractual escalation.
B. Cancel contract immediately.
C. Continue engaging.
D. Absorb defects internally.
E. Activate contract penalties / replacement clauses per terms.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Predictive)**

Procurement need identified. Arrange:

1. Issue RFP to qualified vendors.
2. Conduct bidder conference.
3. Make-or-buy analysis and SOW preparation.
4. Evaluate proposals against source selection criteria; award.

A. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
B. 3 → 1 → 2 → 4
C. 2 → 3 → 1 → 4
D. 4 → 3 → 2 → 1

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which is NOT a standard contract type?

A. Fixed Price (FP)
B. Time and Materials (T&M)
C. Cost Plus Fixed Fee (CPFF)
D. Cost Plus Incentive Fee (CPIF)
E. Fixed Cost Reimbursable Award (FCRA)

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

Vendor's senior negotiator misrepresents material fact during negotiation that would benefit your project if uncorrected. What do you do?

A. Stay silent and benefit.
B. Address directly during negotiation; ask for clarification; if confirmed misrepresentation, factor into agreement and consider escalation to vendor leadership.
C. Use as leverage.
D. End negotiation.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

Vendor delivers system that internal users will operate post-go-live. Vendor's standard training is minimal. Adoption risk high. What is the BEST approach?

A. Accept minimal training.
B. Negotiate expanded user training with vendor as part of acceptance; or plan internal training; embed training in DoD for handover.
C. Skip user training.
D. Escalate to procurement.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

Vendor breach: failure to deliver per SLA for 3 consecutive months. Vendor cites force majeure that you believe is overstated. Critical path impact. What is the BEST next step?

A. Continue current arrangement.
B. Document breach evidence with SLA references; engage vendor with cure period notice per contract; brief sponsor + procurement + legal with options (penalty, replacement, contract change); decide path.
C. Sue vendor immediately.
D. Absorb impact.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

Procurement governance audit reveals: multiple contracts signed without competitive bidding, verbal amendments common, vendor performance not tracked. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Investigate root causes of governance gaps.
B. Cancel all current contracts.
C. Restore competitive bidding discipline per policy.
D. Mandate all contracts formal amendment only.
E. Replace procurement staff.
F. Implement vendor performance tracking and reporting.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Fairness + procurement policy.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sponsor pressure does not waive procurement policy.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Diagnose + coordinate + escalate per contract.

**Mindset Lesson:** Vendor team-member issues = diagnose + coordinate with vendor.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** FP needs clear scope.

**Mindset Lesson:** Contract type matches scope.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and E**

**PMI Justification:** Sponsor brief + contractual recourse.

**Mindset Lesson:** Vendor breach pattern = procurement governance + contract terms.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: B (3 → 1 → 2 → 4)**

**PMI Justification:** Make-buy/SOW → RFP → bidder conference → evaluate/award.

**Mindset Lesson:** Foundation before solicitation.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** FCRA not standard.

**Mindset Lesson:** Memorize standard contract types.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty principle.

**Mindset Lesson:** Misrepresentation addressed, not exploited.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Training in DoD for handover.

**Mindset Lesson:** Adoption is project responsibility.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Documented breach + cure period per contract + governance.

**Mindset Lesson:** Vendor breach handled through contract mechanism.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, F**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Diagnose root causes.
- C: Restore bidding discipline.
- F: Performance tracking.
- B: Cancel all disproportionate.
- D: Mandate without coaching insufficient.
- E: Replace before investigation.

**Mindset Lesson:** Procurement governance gaps = diagnose + restore discipline + tracking.

- MSCPC: Make-buy · SOW · Contract · Perform · Close.
- FP for clear scope. T&M (capped) for evolving. CR for unique.
- Bidder conference for fairness.
- Source selection criteria objective.
- PM owns vendor performance, not procurement team.
- Contract is the truth. Verbal mods don't bind.

- Vendor breach via cure period + escalation.
