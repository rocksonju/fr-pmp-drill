# PMP Exam Master Brief: Define Team Ground Rules (People · Task 12)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Defining team ground rules is the co-creation of explicit behavioral expectations and decision-making norms that bind the team's daily working life — covering communication, conflict handling, decision authority, working hours, ceremonies, and accountability. PMI treats ground rules as team-owned, not PM-imposed, and as enforceable through team self-correction rather than PM policing. Topics include team charter, working agreement, ground rules creation, norm enforcement, and updating rules through retrospectives. The People domain is 42% of the exam; this task threads heavily into team-building, conflict, and shared understanding. In real teams, ground rules are written once and forgotten; on the exam, PMI rewards the option that treats rules as living artifacts maintained by the team and amended when violated.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about co-creating, enforcing, and amending team behavioral norms — owned by the team, maintained through retrospectives.

---

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Ground Rules Look Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Team charter signed at kickoff; rules formalized; updated less frequently |
| **Agile** | Working agreement co-created; revisited each retrospective; enforced by team |
| **Hybrid** | Team charter + working agreement; both maintained through ceremonies |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Team-owned > PM-imposed.** PM-authored rules lack enforcement.
2. **Enforcement is by team, not PM policing.** Team holds itself accountable.
3. **Violation → retrospective discussion → rule amendment if needed.** Rules evolve.
4. **Cover behavior, decision-making, ceremonies, and accountability explicitly.** Implicit ≠ shared.
5. **Make rules visible.** Hidden rules don't shape behavior.

### Ethics Angle

- **Respect:** All team voices shape the rules.
- **Fairness:** Rules apply equally; no carve-outs by seniority.
- **Honesty:** Violations named openly; not suppressed.
- **Responsibility:** Team accountability for rule maintenance, not PM-only.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Team Charter** | Co-created document of team purpose, values, norms | Trap: PM-imposed |
| **Working Agreement** | Agile version covering ceremonies, response times, conflict handling | Trap: skipping co-creation |
| **Ground Rules** | Specific behavioral expectations | Trap: vague rules |
| **Norm** | Implicit or explicit standard | Trap: leaving implicit |
| **Enforcement** | Team self-correction through naming behavior | Trap: PM policing |
| **Retrospective Amendment** | Updating rules based on team learning | Trap: static rules |
| **Decision-Making Norm** | How team makes decisions (consensus, consent, vote, delegation) | Trap: leaving ambiguous |
| **Response-Time Norm** | Expected response window for chat / email | Trap: leaving implicit |
| **Conflict Norm** | How team handles disagreement | Trap: defaulting to avoidance |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| **New team** forming | Facilitate working agreement co-creation at kickoff | Foundation |
| Ground rule **violated** | Surface in retrospective; team discusses; amend rule if needed | Team-owned enforcement |
| Senior member **ignores** ground rule | Team coaches; if pattern, PM coaches privately | Apply equally |
| Working agreement **outdated** | Retrospective revisit; team amends | Living document |
| Team operates on **implicit norms** that conflict | Surface; co-create explicit rules | Implicit ≠ shared |
| New member **unaware** of ground rules | Onboard with rules + context | Onboarding includes rules |
| Sponsor imposes **rule** team disagrees with | Engage sponsor; surface team perspective; negotiate | Imposed rules don't stick |
| Team has **no decision-making rule** | Co-create decision-making norm (consensus, consent, etc.) | Decision-making clarity |
| Ground rules **enforced inconsistently** | Audit; reset; team commits to consistent enforcement | Inconsistency erodes trust |
| Team violates rule **routinely** | Likely rule is wrong; amend through retro | Rule fit-for-purpose |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Sponsor imposes ground rule that conflicts with PMI ethics | Refuse with rationale; engage sponsor; escalate to governance if persisting |
| Persistent ground-rule violation despite team coaching by a member | Joint conversation with functional manager / HR after documented pattern |

## 6. Visual Memory Aid

### Ground Rules Lifecycle

```text
TEAM FORMS
        │
        ▼
Co-create working agreement / ground rules
   (behavior, decisions, ceremonies, response, conflict)
        │
        ▼
Make visible (team space, wiki, kanban side panel)
        │
        ▼
Daily / weekly: team applies; PM facilitates
        │
        ▼
Violation? → Team surfaces in retro
        │
        ▼
Retrospective review: amend, reinforce, or replace
        │
        ▼
Repeat continuously
```

### Ground Rules Mnemonic: **C-V-A-R**

> **C**o-create with team
> **V**isible to all
> **A**pply equally
> **R**eview / amend in retrospective

Memory hook: **"CVAR — Co-create, Visible, Apply equally, Review."**

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Team charter formalized at kickoff
- Update at phase gates if needed

### Agile / Hybrid

- Working agreement co-created
- Retrospective is amendment mechanism
- Often shorter and more behavioral

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** hierarchical violations of ground rules common; team enforcement counter-cultural
- **Multinational:** ground rules must account for cultural communication norms
- **Vendor mixed teams:** include vendor staff in ground rules creation

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "PM authors ground rules for consistency" | Faster | Lacks ownership; ignored |
| "Rules are general; no need to write" | Pragmatism | Implicit norms conflict |
| "PM polices all violations" | Authority | Team must self-correct |
| "Apply rules selectively for senior members" | Pragmatism | Inconsistency erodes trust |
| "Set rules and never revisit" | Stability | Outdated rules ignored |
| "Skip ground rules; team will form naturally" | Empowerment | Storming lengthens |
| "Hidden rules implied by behavior" | Cultural shorthand | New members don't know |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Your sponsor instructs you to impose a ground rule "no one challenges the sponsor publicly" because past meetings have included pushback. You believe this would suppress honest feedback. What should you do FIRST?

A. Impose as instructed.
B. Refuse; engage sponsor on the principle (challenge is healthy); propose team-owned norms for respectful challenge.
C. Impose and document.
D. Escalate to HR.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

In retrospective, team identifies that response times in chat have become inconsistent — some respond in 5 minutes, others not for two days. What is the BEST action?

A. Mandate 5-minute response times.
B. Facilitate team agreement on response-time norm differentiated by urgency (urgent vs normal); document in working agreement.
C. Let the team figure it out.
D. Move all communication to email.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

A senior team member consistently interrupts junior members in meetings. The behavior violates team ground rules on respectful listening. What should you do FIRST?

A. Confront publicly in next meeting.
B. Coach the team to name the dynamic in the moment (rule-based); have private conversation with senior member surfacing impact; reinforce rule application equally.
C. Apply the rule only to junior members.
D. Reassign senior member.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

A new team is forming. The PM wants to set strong working norms. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Facilitate co-creation of working agreement covering behavior, decisions, ceremonies, response.
B. Author and distribute ground rules.
C. Make working agreement visible in team space.
D. Wait until first conflict to define rules.
E. Adopt another team's ground rules verbatim.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

Team finds an outdated ground rule. Arrange:

1. Make amended rule visible to team.
2. Surface the outdated rule in retrospective.
3. Co-create amendment with team.
4. Apply the amended rule consistently going forward.

A. 2 → 3 → 1 → 4
B. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
C. 3 → 2 → 1 → 4
D. 4 → 2 → 3 → 1

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following is NOT a typical category covered in team ground rules?

A. Decision-making norms
B. Communication response times
C. Conflict handling
D. Ceremony cadence
E. Executive compensation structure

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

You realize a ground rule has been quietly waived for the sponsor's preferred team member who has been missing standups. The team is aware. What do you do?

A. Continue the waiver to preserve relationship.
B. Surface the inconsistency openly in retrospective; restore equal application; address with the affected member privately; engage sponsor if pattern recurs.
C. Hide the waiver.
D. Remove standups for everyone.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

Team uses Slack heavily. New member from a culture preferring formal email is struggling. What is the BEST approach?

A. Force new member to use Slack.
B. Coach team on communication norms diversity; co-create explicit response and channel norms; pair new member with mentor for chat acclimatization.
C. Switch entire team to email.
D. Reassign new member.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

A team member has violated working agreement (missed standups, late responses, missed deadlines) consistently for three weeks. Team has surfaced in retros; coaching done. No improvement. What is the BEST next step?

A. Continue private coaching.
B. Document violations; brief functional manager / HR with the working agreement, the team-level surfacing, coaching record, and impact; request joint action.
C. Remove from team.
D. Apply harsher rules to others to "normalize."

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

The working agreement is one year old and rarely referenced. Team is operating on implicit norms that occasionally conflict. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Facilitate working agreement review and amendment in retrospective.
B. Replace working agreement with PM-authored rules.
C. Make amended working agreement visible in team space.
D. Discard working agreement entirely.
E. Onboard new members with working agreement context.
F. Mandate weekly reading of working agreement.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty + Respect; healthy challenge required.

**Mindset Lesson:** Rules suppressing honest feedback refused with alternative.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Team agreement + differentiated norms + documented.

**Mindset Lesson:** Response-time norms co-created with differentiation, not mandated.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Team self-correction + private coaching + equal application.

**Mindset Lesson:** Ground rules apply equally; team-led enforcement first.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Co-creation + visibility = ownership + reference.

**Mindset Lesson:** Foundations: co-create + visible.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: A (2 → 3 → 1 → 4)**

**PMI Justification:** Surface → amend → visible → apply.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sequence keeps rules living.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** Executive compensation is HR domain, not team ground rules.

**Mindset Lesson:** Ground rules cover team behavior, not org-wide policy.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Fairness + open surfacing + private discussion + sponsor engagement.

**Mindset Lesson:** Rule inconsistency surfaced openly; restoration includes sponsor if pattern.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Coach diversity + co-create + mentor pairing.

**Mindset Lesson:** Cultural communication differences accommodated through norms + mentor.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Documented pattern + joint action with FM/HR.

**Mindset Lesson:** Persistent rule violations escalated jointly after documented pattern.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Review and amend.
- C: Visibility restored.
- E: Onboard new members.
- B: PM-authored replacement loses ownership.
- D: Discard removes foundation.
- F: Mandatory reading is policing.

**Mindset Lesson:** Outdated working agreement = retro amendment + visibility + onboarding. Never PM-author, discard, or police.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**DEFINE TEAM GROUND RULES — Exam Morning Card**

- CVAR: Co-create · Visible · Apply equally · Review/amend.
- Team-owned, not PM-imposed.
- Team self-corrects; PM facilitates.
- Cover behavior, decisions, ceremonies, response times, conflict explicitly.
- Rules visible in team space.
- Retrospective is the amendment forum.
- Apply equally — no carve-outs by seniority.
- Routine violation suggests rule is wrong; amend.
- New members onboarded with rules + context.
- Implicit norms drift; surface and explicit.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates rule violations with documented pattern, team-level surfacing, and coaching record — never absorbs, never selectively applies, never lets PM police what team should self-correct.*
