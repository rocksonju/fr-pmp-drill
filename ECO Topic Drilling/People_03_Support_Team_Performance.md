# PMP Exam Master Brief: Support Team Performance (People · Task 3)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Supporting team performance is the continuous practice of measuring how the team is doing, giving timely feedback, recognizing contribution fairly, and removing barriers to higher performance — without micromanaging or substituting metrics for judgment. PMI treats this as the PM's standing responsibility, not a quarterly review event. Topics include performance assessment (team and individual), feedback delivery, recognition systems, retrospectives, and connection between performance and team health. The People domain is 42% of the exam; this task threads into team-building, training, and leadership questions. In real teams, performance is monitored after problems appear; on the exam, PMI selects the option that supports performance proactively and treats decline as a system signal first.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about continuous, fair performance support — measure, feedback, recognize, remove barriers — without micromanagement.

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Performance Support Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Scheduled performance reviews, milestone-tied recognition, formal training plans |
| **Agile** | Retrospectives as continuous performance loop, peer recognition, team-owned improvement |
| **Hybrid** | Formal review + retrospective; performance assessed at iteration + milestone level |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Performance decline is a system signal first.** Process, scope, or pace before individual.
2. **Feedback is timely or it is decorative.** Save-it-for-review feedback misses the moment.
3. **Recognition is leadership, not budget.** Public, timely, contribution-based often outperforms monetary.
4. **Velocity is not performance.** Planning input only.
5. **Burnout is process, not weakness.** PM owns the conditions.

### Ethics Angle

- **Honesty:** Performance data shared with stakeholders must be accurate.
- **Fairness:** Assessment criteria documented and applied consistently.
- **Respect:** Honest feedback delivered with care.
- **Responsibility:** Burnout indicators surfaced and addressed.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Team Performance Assessment** | Periodic evaluation of team health and output | Trap: confusing with individual performance review |
| **Individual Performance Review** | Assessment of individual contribution | Trap: skipping when busy |
| **Feedback Loop** | Mechanism to deliver and receive performance input | Trap: feedback only at review time |
| **Recognition System** | Documented basis for recognition; formal + informal | Trap: visibility-based recognition |
| **Retrospective** | Agile process improvement ceremony | Trap: holding without committing to improvement |
| **One-on-One** | Regular private conversation between PM and team member | Trap: skipping during crunch |
| **360-Degree Feedback** | Feedback from peers, subordinates, supervisors | Trap: assuming universal applicability |
| **Velocity** | Story points completed per sprint | Trap: using as performance metric |
| **Cycle Time / Lead Time / Throughput** | Kanban flow metrics | Trap: confusing the three |
| **Burnout Indicators** | Exhaustion, cynicism, reduced efficacy | Trap: treating as individual weakness |
| **Psychological Safety** | Team belief that interpersonal risk is safe | Trap: confusing with comfort |
| **Continuous Improvement (Kaizen)** | Small, ongoing performance improvements | Trap: assuming improvement requires major change |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Team performance is **declining** | Investigate system causes (scope, pace, clarity, leadership) before individual | System first |
| Individual is **underperforming** | Private 1:1 to diagnose cause | Diagnose before discipline |
| Team is **silent in ceremonies** | Check psychological safety; 1:1 surface | Silence ≠ agreement |
| Recognition feels **inconsistent** | Audit criteria; reset with team input | Fairness is structural |
| **Burnout indicators** appear | Assess pace + scope + clarity; renegotiate if structural | Burnout is process problem |
| Velocity declining | Investigate cause in retrospective | Do not punish for the metric |
| Sprint review feedback **ignored** | Coach team to surface dismissed feedback in retro | Process improvement opportunity |
| New team member is **struggling** | Pair with mentor; provide directive support | Situational style |
| Team requests **more autonomy** | Assess readiness; gradual delegation; situational style | Match autonomy to readiness |
| Sponsor demands **immediate ranking** of team members | Push back; provide aggregated team performance; coach context | Rankings often distort behavior |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Individual performance issue persists after documented coaching | Joint conversation with functional manager / HR with documented attempts |
| Team-wide burnout persists after PM-level pace action | Escalate to sponsor with pace data, retro evidence, recommended scope/timeline renegotiation |

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Scheduled performance reviews at milestones
- Recognition tied to deliverables
- Training plans budgeted at planning

### Agile / Hybrid

- Retrospectives as continuous performance loop
- Peer recognition; team-owned DoD
- Velocity for planning only

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** hierarchical culture discourages safety; build deliberately
- BSEC / Bangladesh Bank deadlines tempt overtime — surface as sustainable-pace risk
- **Multinational:** recognition norms vary; calibrate

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Use velocity as performance metric" | Measurable | Gaming follows pressure |
| "Recognize the most visible contributor" | Visible work earns visible reward | Fairness violation |
| "Ask team to push through burnout" | Senior PMs deliver | Sustainable pace breach |
| "Replace underperforming members" | Quick fix | Coaching + documentation first |
| "Skip 1:1s during crunch" | Crunch needs focus | 1:1s are when issues surface |
| "Hold annual performance reviews only" | Less administrative | Timely feedback > delayed review |
| "Add bonuses for zero-defect sprints" | Incentive aligns | Often produces defect hiding |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Your sponsor wants you to rank-order all team members by performance for a layoff decision. You have not done individual reviews this cycle and the team has been performing well as a unit. What should you do FIRST?

A. Provide a ranking based on your impression.
B. Refuse to provide a ranking without documented assessment; offer aggregated team performance and explain the cost of forced ranking.
C. Ask team members to rank themselves.
D. Provide a ranking and accept the consequences.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

Sprint velocity has declined four sprints. The PO is pressuring the team. Team members have begun inflating story points. What is the BEST action?

A. Allow the inflation.
B. Facilitate a retrospective on velocity pressure; coach PO on planning vs performance distinction; reset estimation norms with team agreement.
C. Confront the team publicly.
D. Replace story points with hours.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

Two team members complain that a third member is consistently coasting. You have not observed the pattern directly. What should you do FIRST?

A. Confront the third member based on the report.
B. Observe directly; review work output; hold private 1:1 with the third member to surface workload, clarity, and motivation.
C. Reassign the third member.
D. Escalate to functional manager.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

Team morale is dropping. Team members declining vacation. Sprint reviews engagement low. Sponsor milestone in 5 weeks. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Hold one-on-ones to assess individual workload.
B. Push through the milestone.
C. Convene focused retrospective on pace and workload patterns.
D. Mandate vacation immediately.
E. Replace burned-out members.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

A team member's performance has declined over three sprints. Arrange:

1. Joint conversation with functional manager / HR if not improving.
2. Private 1:1 to understand cause.
3. Document coaching attempts and pattern.
4. Coach with specific, time-bound improvement actions.

A. 1 → 2 → 4 → 3
B. 2 → 4 → 3 → 1
C. 4 → 2 → 3 → 1
D. 3 → 2 → 4 → 1

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following is NOT a typical input to a team performance assessment?

A. Observed team behavior in ceremonies
B. Output quality and quantity over time
C. Team member feedback through 1:1s
D. Team agreement adherence
E. The PM's gut impression of individual personalities

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

A team member with consistently strong output has been experiencing a personal crisis for two weeks. Output dropped. Sponsor asks for an honest performance assessment. What do you do?

A. Highlight the drop to ensure accuracy.
B. Provide honest aggregated team picture, acknowledge dip is recent and being addressed privately, decline to share personal circumstance without consent.
C. Hide the drop entirely.
D. Share the personal circumstance to explain.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

Hybrid project. Team has co-located and remote members. In-person ceremonies hold engagement; remote members report feeling left out. What is the BEST approach?

A. Require all members co-located.
B. Move ceremonies to fully virtual format.
C. Send written summaries to remote members.
D. Continue current format.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

Team member has missed sprint commitments three times. Two private coaching conversations. No improvement. Team morale dropping. You have documentation. What is the BEST next step?

A. Continue private coaching.
B. Brief functional manager / HR with coaching record, team impact, request joint action.
C. Remove immediately.
D. Publicly call out in retrospective.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

Team performance has been declining. Engagement low. Defects rising. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Investigate root causes through 1:1s and focused retrospective.
B. Increase pressure on team.
C. Surface pace, scope, psychological safety conditions to team; adjust agreements.
D. Replace lowest-output members.
E. Renegotiate scope or timeline with PO/sponsor based on team-health data.
F. Add monetary bonuses for defect-free delivery.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Fairness requires documented assessment; ranking without basis is unfair. Offering aggregated data + explaining cost is professional pushback.

**Mindset Lesson:** Forced ranking without documentation is refused with alternative.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Metric gaming is system signal. Retrospective surfaces pressure; PO coaching addresses cause; team reset restores integrity.

**Mindset Lesson:** Fix pressure, not metric.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Diagnose before action — observe, review, 1:1.

**Mindset Lesson:** Second-hand performance reports require validation before action.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** 1:1s + focused retro cover individual + team levels. Push through, mandate, replace are wrong.

**Mindset Lesson:** Burnout = diagnose at both levels, never push through.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: B (2 → 4 → 3 → 1)**

**PMI Justification:** Diagnose → coach → document → joint escalation.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sequence prevents premature escalation.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** PM gut impression is bias, not assessment input.

**Mindset Lesson:** Performance assessment is evidence-based.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty + Respect — aggregate truthfully, protect privacy.

**Mindset Lesson:** Honesty does not require disclosing private circumstance.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Equal-disadvantage virtual format equalizes participation.

**Mindset Lesson:** Hybrid asymmetry creates class systems.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Two coaching attempts + team impact → joint FM/HR action.

**Mindset Lesson:** Performance escalation is partnership with FM/HR, not unilateral removal.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:** Diagnose + adjust conditions + renegotiate. Pressure, replacement, defect bonuses all wrong.

**Mindset Lesson:** Team-health decline is diagnosed + adjusted + renegotiated.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**SUPPORT TEAM PERFORMANCE — Exam Morning Card**

- Decline is system signal first. Process, scope, pace before individual.
- Feedback timely or decorative. Save-for-review misses moment.
- Recognition fair, timely, contribution-based, often public.
- Velocity is planning input. Never performance metric.
- Burnout is process problem. Renegotiate, never push through.
- 1:1s non-negotiable during crunch.
- Performance management sequence: diagnose → coach → document → escalate jointly.
- Forced ranking without documentation refused with alternative.
- Aggregate honestly. Protect individual privacy.
- Virtual ceremonies: equal-disadvantage beats partial co-location.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates performance issues with documented diagnosis and coaching — never reflexively, never as unilateral removal, never as public shaming.*
