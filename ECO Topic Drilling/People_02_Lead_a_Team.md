# PMP Exam Master Brief: Lead a Team (People · Task 2)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Leading a team is the practice of setting vision, inspiring commitment, building capability, and making team-level decisions in service of project outcomes — without relying on positional authority. PMI distinguishes leadership (influence) from management (process). The PM and Scrum Master are the primary leaders of the project even when team members report elsewhere. The People domain is 42% of the exam; "lead a team" questions appear repeatedly and bleed into stakeholder engagement, conflict, and team performance topics. Leadership styles include servant, transformational, situational, transactional, charismatic, and laissez-faire — PMI's default is servant for agile/hybrid and situational across contexts. In real teams, leadership defaults to command-and-control; on the exam, PMI almost always selects the servant or situational option that develops capability.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about influencing without authority — setting vision, choosing the right style for the context, and growing capability.

---

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Leadership Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Directive at start; delegative as team matures; formal authority more visible |
| **Agile** | Servant leadership default; remove impediments, coach, protect team |
| **Hybrid** | Servant at team level; directive at program/governance level when needed |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Servant leadership is default for agile/hybrid.** Almost always the right answer when style is in question.
2. **Situational leadership beats single-style purism.** Adapt to follower readiness.
3. **Leadership without authority is the PM's reality.** Influence > position.
4. **Vision drives commitment.** A team without vision delivers compliance, not engagement.
5. **Develop the team, do not deplete it.** Leadership creates capability that outlasts the project.

### Ethics Angle

- **Respect:** Honest feedback delivered with care; sugar-coating is disrespect.
- **Fairness:** Recognition and opportunity distributed by contribution.
- **Honesty:** Lead with truthful status, not optimism theater.
- **Responsibility:** Develop the team — abdication ≠ empowerment.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Servant Leadership** | Leader serves team — removes impediments, develops people | Trap: confusing with passive leadership |
| **Transformational Leadership** | Vision + intellectual stimulation + individualized consideration | Trap: assuming it is only charisma |
| **Transactional Leadership** | Reward/punishment for performance | Trap: assuming always wrong; right for routine work |
| **Situational Leadership** | Style adapts to follower readiness (directing → coaching → supporting → delegating) | Trap: defaulting to one style |
| **Charismatic Leadership** | Influence through personality and conviction | Trap: confusing with transformational |
| **Laissez-faire** | Hands-off; team decides all | Trap: confusing with servant — servant is active |
| **Autocratic / Directive** | Leader decides; team executes | Trap: assuming always wrong; right for crisis |
| **Democratic / Participative** | Input-based; leader decides with input | Trap: confusing with consensus |
| **Vision** | Clear future state that inspires commitment | Trap: assuming vision is in charter; must be communicated |
| **Influence** | Producing desired effects without direct authority | Trap: confusing with power |
| **Power Types** | Legitimate, reward, expert, referent, coercive | Trap: assuming legitimate is strongest; expert + referent often stronger |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Team is **new / forming** | Provide directive leadership; share vision; define roles | New teams need direction |
| Team is **performing** | Delegate; servant-leadership mode | Performing teams thrive on autonomy |
| **Storming** phase | Coach through; reinforce ground rules | Storming is necessary |
| Team **lacks vision** | Articulate vision; connect work to outcomes | Vision drives commitment |
| Team **disengaged** in ceremonies | Investigate cause; rebuild trust and purpose | Disengagement is leadership signal |
| Team member shows **strong leadership** of peers | Recognize; develop into formal leader role | Leadership development is PM's responsibility |
| New team member is **struggling** | Pair with mentor; provide directive support; build to coaching | Situational style: new → direct |
| Sponsor demands **autocratic** style | Push back if context does not warrant; explain cost | Style mismatch damages team |
| Team **regressed** under stress | Adapt style to current Tuckman state | Tuckman regression is real |
| Leadership decision **requires unpopular trade-off** | Communicate openly; acknowledge cost; explain rationale | Transparency preserves trust |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Leadership conflict between PM and functional manager over team direction | Engage FM directly first; escalate to sponsor with documented attempts if unresolved |
| Sponsor demands leadership style that breaches ethics (e.g., public shaming, retaliation) | Refuse; document; escalate to next governance level if sponsor persists |

## 6. Visual Memory Aid

### Situational Leadership Style Selection

```text
TEAM STATE
        │
        ▼
Forming / novice / unclear ──→ DIRECTING (high task, low relationship)
        │
        ▼
Storming / building skill ──→ COACHING (high task, high relationship)
        │
        ▼
Norming / competent ──→ SUPPORTING (low task, high relationship)
        │
        ▼
Performing / expert ──→ DELEGATING (low task, low relationship)
        │
        ▼
SERVANT LEADERSHIP underlies all stages — remove impediments, develop people
```

### Leadership Mnemonic: **V-I-C-E**

> **V**ision (set direction)
> **I**nfluence (without authority)
> **C**oaching (develop capability)
> **E**xample (model behavior)

Memory hook: **"Lead by VICE — vision, influence, coaching, example."**

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Directive leadership common in execution under deadline pressure
- Formal authority more visible; sponsor relationships heavy
- Team charter formalized; recognition tied to milestones

### Agile / Hybrid

- Servant leadership default
- Coaching > directing
- Team self-organizes; PM/SM removes impediments
- Hybrid: situational shift across audiences

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** hierarchical culture; servant leadership requires counter-cultural effort
- BSEC / Bangladesh Bank deadlines tempt directive default; use sparingly
- **Cross-cultural teams:** leadership style expectations vary; calibrate
- **Public-sector:** formal authority embedded; navigate without bypass

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Direct the team in detail to ensure quality" | Directive shows ownership | Over-directing breaks self-organization |
| "Let the team handle everything since they are smart" | Empowerment is good | Laissez-faire ≠ servant; abdication |
| "Use positional authority to enforce decisions" | Authority is faster | Damages trust; expert/referent power outlasts |
| "Stay with the same leadership style throughout" | Consistency is leadership | Situational adaptation outperforms purism |
| "Avoid difficult conversations to preserve morale" | Reduces friction | Avoidance is leadership failure |
| "Take credit for team success to motivate yourself" | Recognition matters | Visible leaders credit teams; private credit motivates leaders |
| "Replace team members who challenge decisions" | Reduces friction | Challenges often surface valuable input |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

You manage a USD 10M banking transformation. Your sponsor instructs you to "publicly call out underperformers in steering meetings to motivate the team." You have documented coaching plans with the affected members. What should you do FIRST?

A. Comply with the sponsor's instruction.
B. Refuse; explain the cost of public shaming on trust; propose recognizing progress in coaching plans privately and team performance publicly.
C. Comply privately to one member as a test.
D. Escalate the instruction to HR.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

You are a new Scrum Master joining a high-performing agile team. After two weeks, you have started directing task assignments to "show leadership." Velocity has dropped 30%. What is the BEST action?

A. Continue directing to establish authority.
B. Stop directing; transition to servant leadership; transparently acknowledge the impact in retrospective.
C. Escalate the velocity drop to the sponsor.
D. Restructure the team.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

You are leading a hybrid project. A senior team member with strong domain expertise consistently dismisses input from junior members. Junior members have begun withdrawing in ceremonies. The senior member is critical to delivery. What should you do FIRST?

A. Reassign the senior member.
B. Hold private one-on-ones with the senior member to surface the impact and coach behavior; reinforce ground rules.
C. Publicly correct the senior in next ceremony.
D. Ignore the dynamic since the senior is critical.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

You have taken over a project from a heavily directive prior PM. The team appears compliant but disengaged. Sponsor wants engagement restored. Which TWO actions should you take FIRST?

A. Hold one-on-ones with each team member to learn strengths and concerns.
B. Introduce new processes to assert your leadership.
C. Acknowledge the transition openly; invite feedback; signal style shift.
D. Replace key team members.
E. Report the disengagement to the sponsor immediately.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

A new agile team has been formed. The PM/Scrum Master wants to set leadership tone. Arrange the following actions:

1. Co-create team agreement and ground rules.
2. Hold one-on-ones to learn strengths and goals.
3. Hold team kickoff covering vision, principles, and roles.
4. Begin sprint planning.

A. 4 → 2 → 1 → 3
B. 3 → 2 → 1 → 4
C. 1 → 3 → 2 → 4
D. 2 → 1 → 3 → 4

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following is NOT typically considered a leadership style category?

A. Servant
B. Transformational
C. Transactional
D. Charismatic
E. Procedural

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

A team member with consistently strong output asks you privately for credit and a promotion recommendation. The team member's contributions have been significant but no more than two other quieter team members. The sponsor will follow your recommendation. What do you do?

A. Recommend only the visible contributor.
B. Recommend all three contributors based on documented contribution; transparently explain the recommendation criteria to the sponsor.
C. Make no recommendation.
D. Recommend the visible one and verbally acknowledge the others privately.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

A team finds your leadership style confusing — some members say "too hands-off," others say "too directive." You realize you have been applying the same style across team members at different Tuckman stages. What is the BEST approach?

A. Adopt a single style for consistency.
B. Apply situational leadership: directive to newer members, coaching to developing members, delegating to performing members; communicate the rationale openly.
C. Ask the team to vote on a preferred style.
D. Continue current style and let the team adapt.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

A functional manager has consistently overridden your team-leadership decisions on a shared resource. You have engaged the FM twice. The resource is now disengaged. What is the BEST next step?

A. Continue absorbing the overrides.
B. Brief the sponsor with the leadership impact, the engagement attempts, the resource state, and request governance-level resolution.
C. Escalate to HR.
D. Direct the resource to ignore the FM.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

You are leading a team that has performed well but recently shifted from co-located to fully virtual. Engagement has dropped. Which THREE actions are MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Investigate the engagement drop through one-on-ones and a focused retrospective.
B. Mandate cameras on for all ceremonies.
C. Rebuild team rituals for virtual context (kickoff energy, structured check-ins, social touchpoints).
D. Replace virtual ceremonies with written status only.
E. Adapt leadership style to virtual context — more frequent, shorter touchpoints.
F. Require all members to relocate to the same office.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Code of Ethics (Respect) prohibits public shaming. PM refuses, explains, proposes private coaching + public team recognition. Option A complies with ethics breach. Option C partial-complies. Option D escalates without first engaging sponsor.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sponsor pressure does not waive Respect — refuse + propose alternative, not silence or partial compliance.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Scrum Master should serve, not direct. Transparent retrospective acknowledgment restores trust. Option A continues the harm. Option C escalates a self-caused problem. Option D punishes the team.

**Mindset Lesson:** Style miscalibration is corrected transparently — self-awareness is leadership.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Private coaching surfaces impact + reinforces ground rules. Option A loses critical capability. Option C is public shaming. Option D abandons junior members.

**Mindset Lesson:** Critical members are coached on team-corrosive behavior — not protected, not shamed, not reassigned.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** 1:1s (A) and open acknowledgment (C) build trust and signal style shift. Option B asserts before earning. Option D punishes. Option E externalizes leadership work.

**Mindset Lesson:** New PM transition starts with listening + acknowledgment — never asserting before understanding.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: B (3 → 2 → 1 → 4)**

**PMI Justification:** Kickoff for vision/principles/roles (3). 1:1s for personal context (2). Team agreement co-created (1). Sprint planning (4). Reversing order weakens foundation.

**Mindset Lesson:** Vision → relationships → agreement → commitment.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** Procedural is not a recognized leadership style category. Servant, Transformational, Transactional, Charismatic, Situational, Laissez-faire, Autocratic, Democratic are standard.

**Mindset Lesson:** Memorize leadership categories; novel-sounding names are common distractors.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Fairness requires recognition by documented contribution. Transparent criteria protect against favoritism. Option A omits equally-contributing peers. Option C abdicates. Option D maintains visibility bias.

**Mindset Lesson:** Recognition fairness is structural and transparent — never gut-driven, never visibility-driven.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Situational leadership adapts to readiness. Transparent rationale prevents confusion. Option A forces single style. Option C abdicates. Option D continues miscalibration.

**Mindset Lesson:** Situational style + transparent rationale beats stylistic consistency.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Two PM-level attempts failed; resource impact is structural. Brief sponsor with data + attempts + recommendation. Option A absorbs structural harm. Option C is wrong forum. Option D directs insubordination.

**Mindset Lesson:** FM override patterns are escalated to sponsor with data and recommendation — not absorbed, not bypassed.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Diagnose before intervention.
- C: Rebuild rituals for new context.
- E: Adapt style — frequent, shorter touchpoints work in virtual.
- B: Mandates do not produce engagement.
- D: Removes the ceremony entirely.
- F: Impractical and punitive.

**Mindset Lesson:** Virtual leadership shift = diagnose + ritual rebuild + style adaptation — never mandate, removal, or relocation.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**LEAD A TEAM — Exam Morning Card**

- Servant leadership default (agile/hybrid). Active service, not passive.
- Situational style: directing → coaching → supporting → delegating.
- Tuckman: Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing → Adjourning.
- Influence > authority. Expert + referent power often beats positional.
- Vision drives commitment. Articulate it.
- Recognition fair, transparent, contribution-based.
- Public shaming = ethics breach. Refuse even under sponsor pressure.
- Style mismatch costs engagement. Self-awareness is leadership.
- New PM transition = listen + acknowledge + adjust. Not assert.
- Virtual leadership = more frequent + shorter touchpoints + ritual rebuild.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates leadership conflicts with data and attempts to the sponsor — never absorbs structural overrides, never bypasses sponsor, never complies with ethics-breaching style demands.*
