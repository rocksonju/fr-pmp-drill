# PMP Exam Master Brief: Ensure Team Members and Stakeholders are Adequately Trained (People · Task 5)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Training is the deliberate investment in capability — for team members to deliver and for stakeholders to engage productively. PMI treats training as an ongoing PM responsibility, not a startup checklist or closure activity. Topics include training needs assessment, training plan development, just-in-time training, certification requirements, cross-training for resilience, and stakeholder training for adoption. The People domain is 42% of the exam; this task threads into resource management, knowledge transfer, and organizational change. In real projects, training is the first cost cut under budget pressure; on the exam, PMI selects the option that protects training because capability deficits become risk events.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about continuously investing in team and stakeholder capability — not deferring training to closure or cutting it under cost pressure.

---

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Training Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Formal training plan, budgeted, scheduled around milestones, vendor-delivered |
| **Agile** | Continuous capability building, spikes for learning, pair programming, mob programming, peer teaching |
| **Hybrid** | Formal training plan + continuous in-flight learning; certification + JIT training |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Training is investment, not cost.** Capability gaps become risk events.
2. **Just-in-time training beats just-in-case.** Skills used quickly stick.
3. **Cross-training is resilience.** Single points of failure are project risks.
4. **Stakeholder training enables adoption.** Delivering a product nobody can use is failure.
5. **Skill gaps surface in retrospectives.** Listen to the team about capability.

### Ethics Angle

- **Responsibility:** Capability deficits require active intervention.
- **Fairness:** Training opportunity distributed by need and growth potential, not favoritism.
- **Respect:** Training plans honor individual development goals.
- **Honesty:** Capability gaps reported honestly to sponsor with impact and mitigation.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Training Needs Assessment** | Identification of capability gaps against project requirements | Trap: skipping; assuming current capability is sufficient |
| **Training Plan** | Documented plan covering audience, content, schedule, delivery, budget | Trap: leaving informal |
| **Just-In-Time (JIT) Training** | Training delivered just before skill is needed | Trap: defaulting to upfront training |
| **Cross-Training** | Multiple team members trained in same skill for resilience | Trap: relying on single specialist |
| **Certification Gap** | Required certification not yet held by team member | Trap: discovering at audit/regulator inspection |
| **Knowledge Transfer** | Structured handover of project/product knowledge | Trap: assuming documentation alone transfers knowledge |
| **Stakeholder Training** | Training to enable stakeholder adoption of project outputs | Trap: skipping; treating as customer's problem |
| **Spike (Agile)** | Time-boxed learning investigation | Trap: treating spike output as deliverable |
| **Pair / Mob Programming** | Two or more developers, one keyboard | Trap: dismissing as half-productivity |
| **Communities of Practice** | Cross-team peer learning groups | Trap: assuming individual training suffices |
| **Coaching vs Training** | Coaching = personal development through questions; Training = skill transfer | Trap: using interchangeably |
| **Training ROI** | Capability gain × applied use / training cost | Trap: assuming ROI is hard to measure |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Skill gap identified in retrospective | Add to training plan; JIT training before skill needed | Listen to team |
| New technology required mid-project | Spike + targeted training; plan capability ramp-up | Plan capability transition |
| Sponsor cuts training budget | Surface capability risk; document; propose phased plan | Protect training as investment |
| Team member requests training | Evaluate against project needs + individual development | Training is fair, structured |
| Single specialist becomes risk | Cross-train; document knowledge | Single point of failure = risk |
| Stakeholder adoption is failing | Provide stakeholder training; redesign rollout | Adoption is project responsibility |
| Certification gap discovered | Document; plan certification path; surface compliance risk | Audit/regulator exposure |
| Training delivered but skill not used | Investigate; just-in-time may be needed | Just-in-case training fades |
| Sponsor demands trained-up team before project starts | Negotiate phased approach; JIT for late-need skills | Front-loading wastes investment |
| Vendor delivers solution requiring user training | Plan training as part of delivery; include in DoD | Training enables adoption |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Sponsor cuts training budget creating capability risk | Document capability risk with project impact; propose phased plan; escalate to next governance level if cut sustained |
| Required certification not held; regulator exposure | Escalate immediately; certification timing is rarely PM-resolvable |

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Formal training plan; budgeted; vendor-delivered
- Scheduled around milestones
- Certification gaps identified at planning

### Agile / Hybrid

- Continuous capability building
- Spikes for learning, pair programming, mob programming
- Retrospective surfaces capability needs

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** BSEC certification often mandatory for certain roles — verify at planning
- Compliance training (AML, KYC) recurring; embed in onboarding
- **Cross-cultural teams:** language and cultural training increases effectiveness
- **Vendor-heavy projects:** vendor training of internal staff for handover is critical

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Cut training to meet budget" | Quick budget recovery | Capability gaps become risk events |
| "Train everyone upfront on all expected skills" | Front-loaded preparation | Just-in-case training fades; JIT sticks |
| "Skip stakeholder training; that is customer's job" | Project scope is delivery | Adoption is project responsibility |
| "Rely on single specialist for critical skill" | Specialists are productive | Single point of failure = risk |
| "Add training to project closure" | Lessons learned + training | Training is continuous |
| "Document the skill instead of training" | Documentation transfers knowledge | Documentation alone rarely transfers |
| "Replace team members who lack the skill" | Faster than training | Training builds long-term capability |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Your sponsor instructs you to skip mandatory AML training for two new team members because "they will pick it up on the job." Their roles require AML knowledge for compliance. What should you do FIRST?

A. Skip training as instructed.
B. Refuse; document the compliance risk; propose JIT mandatory training before AML-touching work.
C. Skip training and document the instruction.
D. Escalate to HR immediately.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

A retrospective surfaces that team is repeatedly slow on a specific technical area. The team identifies need for training. The PO wants to keep velocity up. What is the BEST action?

A. Defer training; push for velocity.
B. Add a training spike to the next sprint; coach PO on capability investment vs short-term velocity.
C. Hire a specialist.
D. Escalate to sponsor for training budget.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

A senior team member refuses to mentor a junior colleague during a busy delivery. The junior needs help to deliver their stories. What should you do FIRST?

A. Reassign mentoring to another senior.
B. Engage the senior to understand workload pressure; explore options including reducing senior's delivery commitment to protect mentoring; surface trade-off.
C. Escalate to functional manager.
D. End mentoring and require junior to self-learn.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

You discover that critical knowledge is concentrated in one team member. Project risk has risen. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Plan cross-training across at least two other team members.
B. Hire backup specialist.
C. Document the specialist's knowledge through structured handover sessions.
D. Wait until specialist is ready to train.
E. Escalate to sponsor.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

A new technology adoption requires team training. Arrange:

1. Apply skill on first stories with pair programming.
2. Identify capability gap through team conversation.
3. Adjust training plan based on retrospective input.
4. Run targeted training session(s).

A. 2 → 4 → 1 → 3
B. 4 → 2 → 1 → 3
C. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
D. 2 → 1 → 4 → 3

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following is NOT a typical input to a training needs assessment?

A. Skill gap analysis against project requirements
B. Stakeholder adoption requirements
C. Compliance / certification requirements
D. Team member career-development goals
E. Historical project closure dates

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

A regulator audit is in three weeks. You discover a team member's mandatory certification expired six months ago and they have been working on regulated activities. What do you do?

A. Hide the lapse and rush re-certification.
B. Immediately remove the team member from regulated activities; brief sponsor and compliance officer; plan re-certification; document past activities for assessment.
C. Backdate the certification.
D. Ignore since the audit may not find it.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

A vendor delivers a major system that internal users will operate post-go-live. The vendor's standard offering includes minimal training. Internal user adoption risk is high. What is the BEST approach?

A. Accept the vendor's minimal training as scoped.
B. Negotiate expanded user training with the vendor as part of acceptance; or plan internal training program; embed training in Definition of Done for system handover.
C. Skip user training; deliver and move on.
D. Escalate to procurement.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

You discover three months before delivery that the team lacks a critical compliance skill. Building the skill requires 6 weeks of training. Sponsor demands the milestone. What is the BEST next step?

A. Push the team to learn on the job.
B. Brief sponsor with capability gap, training time, risk of skipping; propose phased scope or timeline adjustment; engage compliance partner if available.
C. Hide the gap.
D. Hire a contractor without training the team.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

Team has just adopted a new framework. Engagement and quality have dropped. Members report feeling out of depth. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Investigate capability gaps and learning preferences.
B. Roll back to old framework.
C. Pair team members with experienced practitioners; consider mob programming for early features.
D. Mandate self-study after hours.
E. Build a community of practice with other teams using the framework.
F. Replace team members.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Compliance training is non-negotiable. Refuse + document + propose JIT.

**Mindset Lesson:** Compliance training is not waived by sponsor authority.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Spike + PO coaching on investment vs velocity.

**Mindset Lesson:** Capability gap addressed with spike, not by deferring or hiring.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Engage + diagnose + adjust workload to protect mentoring.

**Mindset Lesson:** Mentoring is asset — adjust workload to protect, not reassign.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Cross-training + structured handover documentation address SPOF.

**Mindset Lesson:** SPOF resilience = training + documentation, not waiting or hiring.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: A (2 → 4 → 1 → 3)**

**PMI Justification:** Identify → train → apply with pair → adjust plan.

**Mindset Lesson:** Identify before training; apply quickly; iterate.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** Historical closure dates are not training needs inputs.

**Mindset Lesson:** Training needs inputs are about capability, not timeline.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty + Responsibility — remove from activities, brief, plan, document.

**Mindset Lesson:** Certification lapse with regulator exposure is escalated immediately, never hidden.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Negotiate expanded training or plan internal; embed in DoD.

**Mindset Lesson:** User training is project responsibility for adoption.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Capability gap + impact + proposed adjustment to sponsor.

**Mindset Lesson:** Capability gap discovered late is escalated with options, not absorbed or hidden.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Diagnose.
- C: Pair / mob for early features.
- E: Community of practice.
- B: Roll-back loses investment.
- D: After-hours mandates breach sustainable pace.
- F: Replacement is punitive.

**Mindset Lesson:** Capability transition = diagnose + pair + community — never mandate after hours, never replace, never roll back without analysis.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**ENSURE ADEQUATE TRAINING — Exam Morning Card**

- Training is investment, not cost.
- JIT > upfront. Skills used quickly stick.
- Cross-train for resilience. Single points of failure are risks.
- Skill gaps surface in retrospectives. Listen.
- Stakeholder training enables adoption. Project responsibility.
- Spike = time-boxed learning. Output is reduced uncertainty.
- Pair / mob programming = continuous capability transfer.
- Certification gaps escalated immediately. Regulator exposure.
- Documentation alone rarely transfers knowledge.
- Training cut under budget pressure = capability risk; document.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates training/certification gaps with impact and options to sponsor — never absorbs, never hides, never relies on documentation alone for transfer.*
