# PMP Exam Master Brief: Determine Appropriate Project Methodology / Methods and Practices (Process · Task 13)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Determining appropriate methodology is the discipline of selecting predictive, agile, or hybrid approaches — and tailoring within them — based on project characteristics, stakeholder needs, organizational culture, and regulatory environment. PMI treats methodology as a tailoring decision, not a religion. Topics include selection criteria (uncertainty, scope clarity, stakeholder collaboration capability, regulatory constraints), tailoring within methodology, and switching costs. The Process domain is 50% of the exam; methodology choice underlies every other process. In real organizations, methodology defaults to "what we always do"; on the exam, PMI rewards deliberate tailoring to project context.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about deliberately choosing and tailoring predictive, agile, or hybrid based on project characteristics — not defaulting to organizational habit.

---

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Methodology Selection Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Stable scope, clear requirements, regulatory environment, large independent contracts |
| **Agile** | Evolving requirements, stakeholder collaboration available, value via iteration |
| **Hybrid** | Mixed conditions: some areas stable, others evolving; common for transformation |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Method follows characteristics, not preference.**
2. **Tailoring within methodology > switching.**
3. **Hybrid is legitimate, not compromise.**
4. **No one-size-fits-all.**
5. **Switching costs are real.** Don't switch midstream without analysis.

### Ethics Angle

- **Honesty:** Methodology choice transparent.
- **Responsibility:** PM owns tailoring.
- **Fairness:** Stakeholders consulted on choice.
- **Respect:** Cultural fit considered.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Predictive (Waterfall)** | Sequential planning + execution | Trap: assuming outdated |
| **Agile** | Iterative + adaptive | Trap: assuming only Scrum |
| **Hybrid** | Mix per project area | Trap: assuming compromise |
| **Stacey Matrix** | Complexity tool: simple/complicated/complex | Trap: not used by PMI directly |
| **Cynefin Framework** | Decision-making by complexity | Trap: similar but distinct |
| **Tailoring** | Adapt method to project | Trap: defaulting |
| **Lifecycle** | Phased structure of project | Trap: confusing methodology vs lifecycle |
| **Scrum** | Specific agile framework | Trap: agile = Scrum only |
| **Kanban** | Pull-based flow | Trap: confusing with Scrum |
| **SAFe / LeSS / Nexus** | Scaled agile frameworks | Trap: assuming any large = SAFe |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| New project + uncertain scope | Consider agile or hybrid | Iteration discovers |
| New project + stable scope + heavy regulation | Predictive or hybrid | Compliance |
| Sponsor demands "do agile" | Assess fit; tailor; don't force | Fit-for-purpose |
| Org culture predictive but project needs agile | Hybrid; sponsor support | Cultural reality |
| Switching methodology midstream | Analyze cost; only with clear benefit | Switching costs |
| Vendor uses different methodology | Integration points; both methods coexist | Integration |
| Regulatory **requires documentation** | Embed in DoD (agile) or use predictive | Embed, don't bypass |
| Stakeholder unavailable continuously | Agile may be impractical; consider hybrid | Collaboration requirement |
| Multiple teams + complex dependencies | Consider scaled agile (SAFe, LeSS, Nexus) | Scaling |
| Short timeline + clear scope + small team | Lightweight agile or predictive | Match |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Sponsor mandates methodology that doesn't fit characteristics | Brief sponsor with characteristics, fit analysis, alternative; if persisting, escalate to governance |
| Methodology switching midstream proposed without analysis | Refuse; do analysis; brief sponsor |

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Sequential phases
- Heavy upfront planning
- Change control formal

### Agile / Hybrid

- Iterative; continuous refinement
- Sprint or kanban cadence
- Backlog-driven

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** hybrid common; predictive for regulatory artifacts
- BSEC / Bangladesh Bank: documentation requirements influence
- **Public-sector:** RFP often dictates predictive; tailor inside contract

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Sponsor instructs you to "use Scrum" for a regulatory compliance project with fixed scope, fixed date, and required formal documentation. Scrum doesn't fit. What should you do FIRST?

A. Force-fit Scrum.
B. Engage sponsor on characteristics; propose hybrid (Scrum at delivery + predictive governance for regulatory documentation); fit-for-purpose.
C. Decline project.
D. Use Scrum but generate documentation parallel.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

Team has been running Scrum but the team is small (3 people) and feedback cycles take 4 weeks due to external dependencies. Velocity erratic. What is the BEST action?

A. Continue Scrum.
B. Assess fit; consider Kanban or longer iterations; tailor based on actual characteristics; co-create change with team.
C. Add more team members to maintain Scrum.
D. Skip ceremonies.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

Vendor team uses predictive methodology; your team uses agile. Integration points unclear. Cross-team confusion. What should you do FIRST?

A. Force vendor to switch.
B. Engage vendor; agree on integration cadence and artifacts; document shared interfaces in plan.
C. Accept vendor's approach entirely.
D. Escalate to procurement.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

You are starting a new project: bank-wide CRM rollout. Scope partially evolving (UI, workflows) and partially fixed (regulatory compliance, integration with legacy systems). Stakeholder collaboration available. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Adopt hybrid: agile for UI/workflows; predictive for compliance/integration.
B. Adopt pure Scrum.
C. Tailor each area's methodology to characteristics.
D. Wait for sponsor preference.
E. Default to predictive.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Predictive)**

Selecting methodology. Arrange:

1. Recommend methodology with rationale; obtain sponsor agreement.
2. Identify project characteristics (scope clarity, collab, regulatory, culture).
3. Identify tailoring options within methodology.
4. Implement and adjust through retrospectives.

A. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
B. 2 → 3 → 1 → 4
C. 3 → 2 → 1 → 4
D. 4 → 3 → 2 → 1

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which is NOT a recognized scaled agile framework?

A. SAFe
B. LeSS
C. Nexus
D. Disciplined Agile
E. WaterScrum

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

You discover org has been using "agile" label on projects that operate predictive with longer phases. Sponsor wants you to use the agile label for regulatory exemption purposes. What do you do?

A. Use the label.
B. Refuse; explain that methodology label must match practice; propose using actual methodology name with appropriate regulatory communication.
C. Use the label with documentation.
D. Defer.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

You are running hybrid project. Stakeholders confused about "agile vs waterfall" labels. What is the BEST approach?

A. Pick one label.
B. Explain hybrid: agile at delivery, predictive at governance; document in PM plan; communicate as "hybrid with these specific practices."
C. Avoid labels.
D. Discontinue communication.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

Sponsor mandates switching from Scrum to waterfall midstream because "I want more documentation." Team operating well in Scrum. Mid-project switching cost would be high. What is the BEST next step?

A. Switch immediately.
B. Brief sponsor with switching cost analysis, current performance data, options (tailoring within Scrum to add documentation), and recommendation; engage at governance level if sponsor refuses analysis.
C. Refuse outright.
D. Pretend to switch.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

Org has multiple projects with varying characteristics. Currently all use Scrum regardless. Several struggling. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Assess fit per project; tailor or switch where appropriate.
B. Mandate all switch to predictive.
C. Build methodology selection guidance based on project characteristics.
D. Replace PMs to enforce Scrum.
E. Coach PMs on tailoring within methodology.
F. Discontinue methodology choice altogether.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Fit-for-purpose tailoring.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sponsor methodology preference assessed against fit.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Assess + Kanban consideration + team co-creation.

**Mindset Lesson:** Methodology assessment includes team input.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Integration cadence + shared artifacts.

**Mindset Lesson:** Cross-methodology integration through agreed interfaces.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Hybrid + per-area tailoring.

**Mindset Lesson:** Hybrid by characteristics, not compromise.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: B (2 → 3 → 1 → 4)**

**PMI Justification:** Characteristics → tailoring → recommend → implement+adjust.

**Mindset Lesson:** Diagnosis before recommendation.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** WaterScrum isn't standard.

**Mindset Lesson:** Memorize standard scaled agile frameworks.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty principle.

**Mindset Lesson:** Methodology label must match practice.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Hybrid explicit + documented practices.

**Mindset Lesson:** Stakeholder confusion about labels = explicit practice explanation.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Switching cost analysis + alternative + governance escalation.

**Mindset Lesson:** Mid-project methodology switch requires analysis.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Per-project fit assessment.
- C: Selection guidance.
- E: Coach tailoring.
- B: Mandate predictive same problem.
- D: Replacement doesn't fix methodology fit.
- F: Discontinuing choice removes the work.

**Mindset Lesson:** Methodology mismatch = assess + guidance + coach.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**DETERMINE APPROPRIATE METHODOLOGY — Exam Morning Card**

- CSSRC: Characteristics · Scope · Stakeholder collab · Regulatory · Culture.
- Method follows characteristics, not preference.
- Tailoring within > switching.
- Hybrid is legitimate.
- Switching cost real. Analyze before midstream switch.
- Predictive for stable scope + regulatory. Agile for evolving + collab.
- Hybrid for mixed conditions.
- Scaled agile: SAFe, LeSS, Nexus for multiple teams.
- Label must match practice.
- Sponsor preference assessed against fit, not forced.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates methodology choice with characteristics analysis, fit recommendation, and switching cost where applicable — never absorbs mismatched methodology, never mislabels practice.*
