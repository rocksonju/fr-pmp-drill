# PMP Exam Master Brief: Build Shared Understanding (People · Task 10)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Building shared understanding is the deliberate practice of ensuring the team and stakeholders interpret scope, terminology, decisions, and priorities the same way — so work is not redone because two parties thought they agreed. PMI treats ambiguity as a risk to surface, not a "we'll figure it out" condition. Topics include common vision, definition of done (DoD), definition of ready (DoR), glossaries, decision logs, assumption logs, and active alignment techniques. The People domain is 42% of the exam; this task threads into collaboration, ground rules, scope, and quality. In real teams, ambiguity is often discovered at integration or release; on the exam, PMI rewards the option that surfaces and resolves misunderstanding proactively, not retroactively.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about deliberately aligning interpretation of scope, terms, and decisions — so rework happens because of new information, not because of misalignment.

---

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Shared Understanding Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Requirements documentation, glossary, assumption log, decision log |
| **Agile** | DoD and DoR co-created, story examples, walking skeleton, sprint reviews |
| **Hybrid** | Formal glossary + co-created DoD; release-level shared understanding + iteration-level confirmation |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Ambiguity is a risk to surface, not tolerate.** Assumption logs exist for a reason.
2. **Confirm understanding actively.** Paraphrasing closes the gap; nodding does not.
3. **DoD and DoR are shared understanding made explicit.** They are alignment artifacts.
4. **Decision logs prevent re-litigation.** Anchored decisions stay anchored.
5. **Walking skeleton beats document.** Real working product confirms understanding faster than spec.

### Ethics Angle

- **Honesty:** Admit when understanding is incomplete.
- **Respect:** Confirm understanding without making others feel ignorant.
- **Responsibility:** Misunderstanding cost is the PM's to surface and prevent.
- **Fairness:** All voices contribute to shared understanding, not just senior ones.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Shared Understanding** | Common interpretation of scope, terms, decisions across team + stakeholders | Trap: assuming everyone agrees |
| **Definition of Done (DoD)** | Team's shared completion criteria | Trap: PM-imposed |
| **Definition of Ready (DoR)** | Criteria a backlog item meets before sprint planning | Trap: confusing with DoD |
| **Assumption Log** | Document of project assumptions to validate | Trap: leaving in PM's head |
| **Decision Log** | Document of decisions, rationale, owner, date | Trap: re-litigating decisions |
| **Glossary** | Shared definitions of project terms | Trap: assuming common vocabulary |
| **Active Listening** | Paraphrasing + confirming | Trap: hearing ≠ listening |
| **Walking Skeleton** | Minimal end-to-end implementation that confirms architecture and shared understanding | Trap: detailed-spec-instead |
| **Spike** | Time-boxed learning to reduce ambiguity | Trap: unconstrained investigation |
| **Information Radiator** | Visible, low-friction status display | Trap: behind-login dashboard |
| **Specification by Example** | Examples that anchor shared understanding of behavior | Trap: prose-only specs |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Team disagrees on what "done" means | Co-create DoD with team + PO | Shared understanding |
| Stakeholder and team use **same word** with **different meanings** | Add to glossary; confirm with both parties | Terminology drift causes rework |
| Decision is being **re-litigated** weeks later | Refer to decision log; surface why re-litigation arose | Decision log is authoritative |
| Team makes **assumption** without validation | Log; validate; communicate | Assumption logs surface risk |
| Stakeholder approves **PRD** without questioning details | Confirm understanding actively; ask specific questions | Approval ≠ understanding |
| Story requirements are **ambiguous** | Use specification-by-example; walking skeleton; DoR | Concrete examples > prose |
| Team **misinterpreted** scope | Surface, realign, update DoD; do not blame | Misinterpretation is design failure |
| New team member is **confused** about decisions | Walk through decision log; pair with mentor | Onboarding includes context |
| Stakeholder says they were **"not informed"** | Acknowledge; review communication plan; surface gaps | Engagement design |
| Sprint review reveals **misunderstanding** | Treat as feedback; align; adjust DoD if pattern | Reviews surface misalignment |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Stakeholders fundamentally disagree on scope interpretation despite PM-level alignment | Brief sponsor with the disagreement, evidence, attempts, and recommended interpretation |
| Decision being re-opened by senior stakeholder unilaterally | Engage stakeholder with decision log + rationale; escalate to sponsor if pattern recurs |

## 6. Visual Memory Aid

### Shared Understanding Flow

```text
ALIGNMENT NEED IDENTIFIED (story, decision, term, assumption)
        │
        ▼
Surface the gap: where do interpretations diverge?
        │
        ▼
Use the right tool:
   - Term gap        → Glossary
   - "Done" gap      → Definition of Done
   - Pre-sprint gap  → Definition of Ready
   - Decision gap    → Decision Log
   - Assumption gap  → Assumption Log
   - Behavior gap    → Specification by Example / walking skeleton
        │
        ▼
Confirm understanding actively (paraphrase, ask examples)
        │
        ▼
Document; communicate; revisit if conditions change
```

### Shared Understanding Mnemonic: **G-D-D-D-A**

> **G**lossary (terms)
> **D**efinition of Done (completion)
> **D**efinition of Ready (start)
> **D**ecision log (anchors)
> **A**ssumption log (surfaces ambiguity)

Memory hook: **"GDDDA — five artifacts that hold shared understanding."**

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Requirements documents; assumption log; glossary
- Decision log formalized

### Agile / Hybrid

- DoD + DoR co-created
- Specification by example; walking skeleton
- Decision log light-weight but maintained

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** terminology drift across business + tech + regulator vocabularies; glossary essential
- BSEC / Bangladesh Bank often use specific terms that mean different things in different contexts
- **Cross-cultural:** language and idiom drift; active confirmation essential
- **Public-sector:** decision logs especially valuable due to long timelines and personnel turnover

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Send detailed spec document for sign-off" | Documentation discipline | Approval ≠ understanding |
| "Trust the team to align verbally" | Empowerment | Verbal alignment drifts |
| "Decide ambiguities yourself and announce" | Decision velocity | Ownership lost; resentment grows |
| "Defer alignment until details emerge" | Just-in-time | Misalignment compounds |
| "Skip glossary; everyone speaks the same language" | Pragmatism | Terminology drift universal |
| "Re-litigate decisions when stakeholders disagree" | Responsiveness | Decision log prevents re-litigation |
| "Treat assumption as confirmed if no one objects" | Pragmatism | Silent assumptions become risks |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

You discover that a key stakeholder signed off on a PRD without understanding a critical assumption that, if wrong, would materially affect cost. The stakeholder is busy and unlikely to revisit unprompted. What should you do FIRST?

A. Proceed since the PRD is signed.
B. Proactively engage the stakeholder, walk through the assumption explicitly, and confirm understanding; document the conversation.
C. Wait until the assumption proves wrong.
D. Add a footnote and continue.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

During sprint review, a stakeholder says the feature "is not what we wanted." The team believes they delivered the user story exactly as written. What is the BEST action?

A. Reject the feedback.
B. Surface the gap; use the demo to walk through interpretation; if pattern, strengthen DoR with examples and acceptance criteria.
C. Implement whatever the stakeholder now wants.
D. Escalate to sponsor.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

A senior stakeholder reopens a decision made 6 sprints ago. The decision is documented in the decision log. The stakeholder insists the original decision was wrong. What should you do FIRST?

A. Reopen and re-decide.
B. Engage stakeholder with the decision log + rationale; ask what new information warrants revisit; if genuine, follow change process.
C. Refuse to discuss.
D. Escalate to sponsor.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

Multiple stakeholders use "customer" to mean different things. Confusion has caused two rework cycles. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Add "customer" to a glossary with disambiguated definitions per context.
B. Pick one definition and impose.
C. Engage stakeholders to agree on disambiguation; communicate.
D. Defer until next planning cycle.
E. Have legal define "customer."

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

A new user story is being written. Arrange:

1. Apply Definition of Ready check before sprint planning.
2. Capture acceptance criteria with examples.
3. Discuss story with PO and team in backlog refinement.
4. Confirm shared understanding through walkthrough.

A. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
B. 3 → 2 → 4 → 1
C. 2 → 3 → 1 → 4
D. 4 → 1 → 2 → 3

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following is NOT a typical tool for building shared understanding?

A. Glossary
B. Definition of Done
C. Decision log
D. Assumption log
E. Earned Value Management report

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

You realize the sponsor and the regulatory stakeholder have different understandings of a critical compliance scope. Sponsor believes scope X; regulator believes scope X+. Sponsor is presenting to the board next week. What do you do?

A. Let sponsor present as-is.
B. Immediately surface the gap to sponsor; co-engage with the regulator to confirm; align before board presentation; document agreed scope.
C. Send written summary to regulator only.
D. Update PRD silently.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

A new team member joins. They are asking many questions about past decisions and assumptions. The team is busy. What is the BEST approach?

A. Tell them to figure it out from documentation.
B. Pair with a mentor; walk through decision log + assumption log + glossary; structured onboarding includes context, not just code.
C. Escalate to functional manager.
D. Defer onboarding until less busy.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

A senior stakeholder unilaterally reinterprets a documented decision and instructs vendors directly. You have engaged the stakeholder twice. What is the BEST next step?

A. Continue absorbing.
B. Brief sponsor with the decision log, engagement attempts, the impact of reinterpretation, and recommended governance action.
C. Escalate to HR.
D. Direct vendors to ignore the stakeholder.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

The team has had three consecutive sprints with stories that "passed acceptance" but later required rework due to misunderstanding. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Investigate why acceptance is missing real understanding.
B. Increase number of approvals required before acceptance.
C. Strengthen Definition of Ready with examples and acceptance criteria.
D. Add formal handoff documentation.
E. Use specification by example or walking skeleton to anchor understanding.
F. Mandate longer story discussions.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty + Responsibility — proactive confirmation; documented.

**Mindset Lesson:** Stakeholder approval without understanding is not safe; confirm actively.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Surface gap; use demo to align; strengthen upstream artifacts.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sprint review surfacing misunderstanding = feedback + upstream fix.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Decision log + rationale; check for new information; change process if warranted.

**Mindset Lesson:** Decision re-litigation handled with log + change process, not refused.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Glossary + stakeholder agreement covers artifact + alignment.

**Mindset Lesson:** Terminology drift fixed by glossary + agreement, not imposition.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: B (3 → 2 → 4 → 1)**

**PMI Justification:** Discuss → criteria → walkthrough → DoR check.

**Mindset Lesson:** Refinement before planning; confirm before checking DoR.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** EVM is performance measurement, not shared-understanding tool.

**Mindset Lesson:** Distinguish performance tools from alignment tools.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty + Responsibility — surface, co-engage, align before board.

**Mindset Lesson:** Misalignment between sponsor and regulator escalated immediately.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Structured onboarding includes context; mentor + logs.

**Mindset Lesson:** New member onboarding includes decision context, not just code.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Sponsor briefing with decision log, attempts, impact, recommendation.

**Mindset Lesson:** Stakeholder reinterpretation pattern escalated with data.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Diagnose missing understanding.
- C: Strengthen DoR with examples.
- E: Specification by example / walking skeleton.
- B: More approvals don't add understanding.
- D: Formal handoff replaces conversation.
- F: Longer doesn't equal aligned.

**Mindset Lesson:** Repeated misunderstanding = diagnose + strengthen DoR + concrete examples. Never more approvals, formal handoff, or longer meetings.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**BUILD SHARED UNDERSTANDING — Exam Morning Card**

- Ambiguity is a risk to surface, not tolerate.
- GDDDA: Glossary · Definition of Done · Definition of Ready · Decision log · Assumption log.
- Confirm understanding actively. Paraphrase, ask examples.
- Approval ≠ understanding.
- Walking skeleton + spec by example > prose specs.
- Decision log prevents re-litigation. Senior reopen → engage with log + rationale.
- Same word, different meanings = glossary.
- New member onboarding includes decision context.
- Misalignment between sponsor and regulator surfaced immediately.
- Sprint review misunderstanding = feedback + upstream artifact strengthening.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates shared-understanding breakdowns with decision log, engagement attempts, and impact — never absorbs, never re-litigates anchored decisions reflexively, never treats approval as understanding.*
