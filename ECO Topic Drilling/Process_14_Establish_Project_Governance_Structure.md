# PMP Exam Master Brief: Establish Project Governance Structure (Process · Task 14)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Establishing project governance is the discipline of defining decision rights, escalation paths, accountability, oversight, and reporting cadence so the project operates within an authority framework. PMI treats governance as enabling, not bureaucratic — it makes decision-making faster by clarifying who decides what. Topics include RACI matrix, decision rights, steering committee, CCB, escalation paths, and project-management-office (PMO) integration. The Process domain is 50% of the exam; governance threads into integration, change, and stakeholder. In real organizations, governance is implicit; on the exam, PMI rewards explicit governance documentation.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about explicitly defining decision authority, escalation paths, and accountability — so decisions happen faster, not slower.

---

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Governance Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Steering committee; CCB; formal RACI; structured cadence |
| **Agile** | Lightweight; PO + sponsor + team; backlog-driven; ceremonies as governance |
| **Hybrid** | Steering + agile ceremonies; CCB for baselines; team for HOW |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Governance enables, not blocks.** Clear authority speeds decisions.
2. **One Accountable per decision.** Multiple R's, single A.
3. **Escalation paths documented.**
4. **PMO leveraged, not bypassed.**
5. **Steering committee for strategic; PM for tactical.**

### Ethics Angle

- **Honesty:** Governance reports truthful.
- **Responsibility:** Accountability honored.
- **Fairness:** Decisions on criteria.
- **Respect:** Authority levels respected.

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Project chartered; **governance** undefined | Engage sponsor; establish steering, CCB, RACI | Foundation |
| **Decision** required above PM authority | Route to correct authority per governance | Authority |
| **RACI** has multiple A | Clarify; only one A | RACI rule |
| Steering committee **dormant** | Re-engage; reset cadence | Governance discipline |
| PMO **policies** ignored | Align; integrate; explain context | Integration |
| Sponsor demands **PM decide above authority** | Decline; offer to facilitate | Authority |
| Decision rights **unclear** | Document; obtain sponsor approval | Clarity |
| **Stakeholder bypasses** governance | Re-engage; restore process; brief sponsor | Discipline |
| **Cross-program** decision | Engage program governance | Multi-project |
| New PMO **mandate** | Integrate; tailor | PMO leverage |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Decision required above PM authority | Per governance to CCB / sponsor / steering |
| Governance breakdown (dormant committee, ignored cadence) | Brief sponsor with impact and reset proposal |

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Formal steering + CCB
- Detailed RACI
- Structured cadence

### Agile / Hybrid

- Lighter governance
- PO + team for tactical
- Sponsor + steering for strategic

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** governance hierarchical; multiple oversight layers
- BSEC / Bangladesh Bank: regulatory bodies in governance
- **Public-sector:** governance often political

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Sponsor instructs you to dissolve the project steering committee because "they slow things down." Committee has legitimate oversight role per org policy. What should you do FIRST?

A. Dissolve.
B. Refuse; engage sponsor on the value of steering oversight; propose tailoring (shorter meetings, focused agenda) rather than dissolution.
C. Dissolve with documentation.
D. Escalate to PMO.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

PO and team operating without sponsor visibility. Sponsor recently complained about being uninformed on strategic decisions. What is the BEST action?

A. Continue.
B. Establish lightweight sponsor governance (monthly steering or sprint review participation); document decision rights; communicate to team.
C. Send sponsor daily reports.
D. Have sponsor join every standup.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

PMO mandates new templates that don't fit agile delivery cadence. Team complains. PMO insists. What should you do FIRST?

A. Force compliance.
B. Engage PMO; explain delivery context; propose tailored templates that meet PMO intent + delivery needs.
C. Ignore PMO.
D. Escalate to sponsor.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

New project. Sponsor wants you to establish governance. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Define decision rights, RACI, escalation paths.
B. Replicate another project's governance verbatim.
C. Establish steering committee composition and cadence.
D. Implicit governance to start.
E. Wait for first decision.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Predictive)**

Establishing governance. Arrange:

1. Document governance plan; communicate.
2. Define decision rights and escalation paths.
3. Establish steering committee + CCB composition + cadence.
4. Identify project stakeholders and their authority levels.

A. 4 → 2 → 3 → 1
B. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
C. 3 → 4 → 2 → 1
D. 2 → 4 → 3 → 1

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

In RACI, which of the following is INCORRECT?

A. R = Responsible (does the work)
B. A = Accountable (signs off)
C. C = Consulted (input)
D. I = Informed (notified)
E. Multiple A per task is the norm

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

Sponsor instructs you to make a decision above your documented authority threshold to "speed things up." Decision affects baseline. What do you do?

A. Make the decision.
B. Refuse; explain authority threshold; offer to facilitate quick decision with proper authority (emergency CCB, urgent sponsor decision); document.
C. Make decision quietly.
D. Defer.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

Multiple governance bodies (steering, CCB, PMO, sponsor) want different report formats. What is the BEST approach?

A. Send all same report.
B. Tailor reports per body's decision rights and information needs; share source of truth (dashboard or registry).
C. Discontinue reporting.
D. Long combined report to all.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

Steering committee dormant for 3 months. Decisions deferred. Project at risk. You have raised once. What is the BEST next step?

A. Continue waiting.
B. Brief sponsor with the governance gap, impact, recommendation to reset cadence; if sponsor doesn't act, escalate to PMO/governance level.
C. Decide unilaterally.
D. Cancel steering committee.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

Decisions routinely take too long. Multiple parties consulted; accountability unclear. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Investigate decision-making bottlenecks.
B. Skip consultation to speed.
C. Clarify RACI: one Accountable per decision.
D. Eliminate steering committee.
E. Document decision rights and authority thresholds.
F. Bypass governance for "small" decisions.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Tailoring > dissolution; oversight value.

**Mindset Lesson:** Governance pace fixed by tailoring, not dissolution.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Lightweight sponsor governance.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sponsor governance scaled to need.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Engage PMO; tailor.

**Mindset Lesson:** PMO friction = engage + tailor, not force or bypass.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Decision rights + steering composition.

**Mindset Lesson:** Governance foundation = decision rights + bodies.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: A (4 → 2 → 3 → 1)**

**PMI Justification:** Stakeholders → rights → bodies → document.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sequence builds on stakeholder map.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** Multiple A breaks RACI.

**Mindset Lesson:** One A per task.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Refuse + offer proper-authority route.

**Mindset Lesson:** Above-authority decisions not absorbed by PM.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Tailored per body + single source.

**Mindset Lesson:** Governance reporting tailored.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Sponsor brief + escalate if no action.

**Mindset Lesson:** Dormant governance escalated proactively.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Diagnose bottlenecks.
- C: Clarify A.
- E: Document rights + thresholds.
- B: Skipping consultation removes value.
- D: Eliminate steering removes strategic oversight.
- F: Bypass undermines.

**Mindset Lesson:** Decision-making slowness = diagnose + RACI + authority thresholds.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**ESTABLISH PROJECT GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE — Exam Morning Card**

- RDSCP: RACI · Decision rights · Steering · CCB · PMO.
- Governance enables, not blocks.
- One A per RACI. Multiple R OK.
- Decision rights documented; authority thresholds explicit.
- Escalation paths documented.
- Steering for strategic; PM for tactical.
- PMO leveraged through integration.
- Stakeholder bypass restored, not normalized.
- Dormant governance reset proactively.
- Above-authority decisions routed, not absorbed.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates above-authority decisions to proper governance — never absorbs, never dissolves oversight for speed, never normalizes bypass.*
