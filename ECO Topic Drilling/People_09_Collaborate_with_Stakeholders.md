# PMP Exam Master Brief: Collaborate with Stakeholders (People · Task 9)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Collaborating with stakeholders is the practice of partnering with people who have an interest in or influence over the project — not just informing them, not just managing them — to co-create outcomes. PMI distinguishes engagement (active two-way work) from communication (one-way information transfer). Topics include stakeholder identification, engagement assessment matrix, power/interest grid, salience model, joint planning, and continuous relationship investment. The People domain is 42% of the exam; collaboration threads heavily into stakeholder engagement (Process Task 4), communications, and conflict. In real organizations, stakeholders are treated as audience; on the exam, PMI rewards the option that treats them as co-owners and surfaces resistance early through engagement, not announcement.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about co-creating outcomes with stakeholders as partners — not informing them as audience.

---

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Stakeholder Collaboration Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Stakeholder register, engagement assessment matrix, scheduled engagement at phase gates |
| **Agile** | Continuous engagement; sprint review as collaboration; PO as stakeholder proxy |
| **Hybrid** | Register + iteration-level collaboration; release-level joint planning |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Collaborate, do not inform.** Information transfer is not engagement.
2. **Resistance is data.** Resistant stakeholders surface real issues; invest more, not less.
3. **Continuous > scheduled.** Engagement is daily, not milestone-only.
4. **Salience matters.** Power + Legitimacy + Urgency = definitive stakeholders.
5. **Tailor to audience.** Same information, different format and depth per stakeholder.

### Ethics Angle

- **Respect:** All stakeholders engaged equally regardless of stance.
- **Honesty:** Stakeholder communication accurate; selective disclosure breaches.
- **Fairness:** Engagement effort matched to legitimate interest, not political alliance.
- **Responsibility:** PM owns stakeholder collaboration; cannot fully delegate.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Stakeholder** | Anyone affected by or with influence over the project | Trap: limiting to formal roles |
| **Stakeholder Register** | Document of stakeholders + classification + engagement | Trap: one-time artifact |
| **Power/Interest Grid** | 2×2 matrix for engagement strategy | Trap: assuming "manage closely" = all high-power |
| **Salience Model** | Power + Legitimacy + Urgency → identifies definitive stakeholders | Trap: ignoring legitimacy + urgency |
| **Engagement Assessment Matrix** | Maps current vs desired engagement: Unaware → Resistant → Neutral → Supportive → Leading | Trap: confusing current with desired |
| **Active Listening** | Paraphrasing, confirming understanding | Trap: hearing ≠ listening |
| **Joint Planning** | Stakeholders participate in planning decisions | Trap: planning to stakeholders, not with them |
| **Co-creation** | Solution shaped together with stakeholders | Trap: PM-authored + stakeholder approval |
| **Tailoring** | Adapting engagement to audience needs | Trap: one-size-fits-all |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| **New stakeholder** identified mid-project | Add to register; assess engagement; engage early | Late identification ≠ reduced engagement need |
| Stakeholder is **resistant** | Meet to understand root cause; do not exclude | Resistance is data |
| Stakeholder is **unaware** | Targeted information + invitation to engage | Cannot collaborate with unaware |
| Sponsor **dismisses** a stakeholder concern | Surface impact data; advocate for inclusion | PM owns stakeholder collaboration |
| Two stakeholders **conflict** | Facilitate joint session; surface shared interests | Joint > parallel |
| Stakeholder **stopped responding** | Proactive reach out; investigate barriers | Silence ≠ disinterest |
| Stakeholder demands **more frequent updates** | Accommodate; update communication plan | Tailoring is responsiveness |
| Stakeholder **bypasses** PM to team | Re-engage; restore channel; understand the bypass | Bypass signals communication design failure |
| Stakeholder **engagement dropped** | Investigate; do not assume project is fine just because delivery is | Engagement decline is leading indicator |
| New regulatory stakeholder identified | Register; salience analysis; engage immediately | Regulator ≈ definitive stakeholder |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Stakeholder collaboration breakdown after PM-level attempts | Brief sponsor with engagement history, attempts, impact, and recommended governance action |
| Sponsor instructs PM to exclude a legitimate stakeholder | Refuse with ethics + impact rationale; engage stakeholder; escalate to governance if sponsor persists |

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Stakeholder register formal; engagement at phase gates
- Communication plan structured

### Agile / Hybrid

- Continuous engagement via sprint review + ongoing PO conversations
- Lightweight register; engagement evolves through iterations

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** BSEC, Bangladesh Bank are definitive stakeholders; treat with salience model
- Regulatory communication formal; embed in DoD
- **Public-sector:** political stakeholders with high power but variable legitimacy
- **Multinational:** cultural communication norms vary; tailor

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Exclude difficult stakeholder" | Reduces friction | Engagement of all legitimate stakeholders required |
| "Send status report to resistant stakeholder" | Communication = engagement | Reports are push; resistance needs dialogue |
| "Inform stakeholder only when bad news confirmed" | Avoid false alarms | Proactive communication required |
| "Delegate stakeholder engagement to team" | Distributed effort | PM owns collaboration |
| "Treat all stakeholders the same" | Fairness | Tailoring required |
| "Update communication plan at closure" | Lessons capture | Living document |
| "Engagement only at milestones" | Structured | Continuous engagement is principle |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Your sponsor instructs you to exclude a regulatory stakeholder from status meetings because "they slow everything." The stakeholder has approval authority. Three weeks later, the stakeholder contacts you frustrated. What should you do FIRST?

A. Follow sponsor's instruction.
B. Meet privately with the regulator; understand concerns; re-engage and update communication; brief sponsor on impact of exclusion.
C. Escalate immediately to steering committee.
D. Send the regulator the missed status reports.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

Sprint review attendance has dropped. Stakeholders rarely give feedback. PO worried about value drift. What is the BEST action?

A. Cancel sprint reviews; replace with formal UAT at end.
B. Investigate why stakeholders are not attending; work with PO to increase relevance and accessibility of sprint reviews.
C. Allow team to make product decisions since stakeholders implicitly delegated.
D. Escalate to senior management for mandatory attendance.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

A senior stakeholder initially supportive has become resistant after learning the agile approach won't produce a formal requirements document. They are now lobbying others against the project. What should you do FIRST?

A. Update register to mark stakeholder as project threat.
B. Meet with stakeholder to understand specific concerns; explore tailored artifact options.
C. Ask sponsor to override the stakeholder.
D. Document the resistance in risk register and continue.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

Two key stakeholders received conflicting information about project scope — one from your team, one from a vendor. Both made decisions on different assumptions. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Convene a meeting with both stakeholders and vendor to establish single source of truth.
B. Issue formal scope clarification memo to all stakeholders immediately.
C. Escalate vendor communication to legal.
D. Review and update communications plan to prevent recurrence.
E. Suspend all stakeholder communication until resolved internally.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

A new stakeholder is identified mid-project. Arrange:

1. Engage stakeholder; understand interests and concerns.
2. Add to stakeholder register with classification.
3. Tailor communication and engagement plan.
4. Assess current engagement vs desired.

A. 2 → 4 → 1 → 3
B. 1 → 2 → 4 → 3
C. 2 → 1 → 4 → 3
D. 4 → 2 → 1 → 3

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following is NOT a valid engagement level in the stakeholder engagement assessment matrix?

A. Unaware
B. Resistant
C. Neutral
D. Supportive
E. Critical

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

A stakeholder asks you whether a project is on track. You know there is a 6-week delay, but your sponsor wants the board meeting in 2 weeks to go smoothly first. What do you do?

A. Say "on track" until after the board meeting.
B. Honestly state the project is currently delayed by 6 weeks; share the recovery plan; offer to brief stakeholder fully.
C. Decline to answer.
D. Say "substantially on track."

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

Regulators require formal monthly status reports. Business stakeholders prefer real-time visibility. The team uses a digital kanban accessible to all. What is the BEST communication approach?

A. Standardize on monthly reports for all.
B. Discontinue kanban.
C. Tailor: kanban live for business stakeholders; monthly formal reports for regulators; sponsor-tailored summary.
D. Ask regulators to use kanban instead.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

A conflict between two senior business stakeholders has persisted through two facilitated sessions. Both report to your sponsor. One threatens to escalate to sponsor directly. What is the BEST next step?

A. Allow the escalation.
B. Proactively brief sponsor with the conflict, facilitation attempts, impact, and request involvement.
C. Escalate to steering committee.
D. Run a third facilitation.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

Stakeholder engagement scores have declined over three sprints. Attendance dropped; feedback weakening. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Investigate the engagement decline cause.
B. Shorten sprint reviews to reduce stakeholder time commitment.
C. Work with PO to make reviews more relevant and outcome-focused.
D. Replace sprint reviews with monthly status report.
E. Add standing retrospective item on stakeholder engagement.
F. Escalate to sponsor for mandatory attendance.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Regulator has approval authority; ethical and practical engagement required.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sponsor exclusion instructions overridden by stakeholder engagement principle for legitimate authorities.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Declining attendance is engagement design problem; PM + PO investigate and adapt.

**Mindset Lesson:** Engagement decline = process problem first.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Understanding before action; tailored artifacts may address concern.

**Mindset Lesson:** Resistance is data — diagnose before prescribing.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and D**

**PMI Justification:** Joint meeting + plan update covers tactical + structural.

**Mindset Lesson:** Conflicting information requires single source of truth + plan update.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: C (2 → 1 → 4 → 3)**

**PMI Justification:** Register → engage → assess gap → tailor plan.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sequence captures stakeholder before tailoring.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** Levels are Unaware, Resistant, Neutral, Supportive, Leading. "Critical" is not a level.

**Mindset Lesson:** Memorize five engagement levels exactly.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty principle absolute; transparency with recovery plan.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sponsor calendar does not waive Honesty in stakeholder communication.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: C**

**PMI Justification:** Tailoring across audiences.

**Mindset Lesson:** Tailored communication serves all audiences without abandoning any.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Proactive sponsor briefing after exhausted attempts.

**Mindset Lesson:** Escalation is briefed proactively, not let happen.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Diagnose decline.
- C: Make reviews more relevant.
- E: Embed engagement in retrospective.
- B: Shortening reduces value.
- D: Replacing agile ceremony with waterfall report.
- F: Mandatory attendance is coercive.

**Mindset Lesson:** Engagement decline = diagnose + relevance + retro embed. Never coerce, shorten, or replace.

- Resistance is data; invest more, not less.
- Continuous > scheduled. Daily, not milestone-only.
- Salience model: Power + Legitimacy + Urgency.
