# PMP Exam Master Brief: Support Organizational Change (Business Environment · Task 4)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Supporting organizational change is the discipline of preparing, equipping, and supporting people through the people-side of project-driven change — so that the technical delivery actually produces business value. PMI distinguishes change management (technical project change) from organizational change management (OCM, people-side). Topics include OCM models (ADKAR, Kotter 8-step, Lewin), stakeholder readiness, resistance management, communication, training, and sustainment. The Business Environment domain is 8% of the exam; OCM is tested with situational scenarios. In real organizations, technical delivery happens but adoption fails; on the exam, PMI rewards the option that integrates OCM with delivery — not bolt-on training at the end.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about preparing and supporting people through change so technical delivery actually produces business value — OCM integrated with delivery, not bolted on.

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | OCM Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | OCM plan; change champions; structured stakeholder communication |
| **Agile** | Continuous engagement; iterative adoption; feedback loops |
| **Hybrid** | OCM plan + iteration-level engagement |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Technical delivery ≠ adoption.**
2. **OCM begins at project initiation.**
3. **Resistance is data, not obstacle.**
4. **Sponsor visibility critical.**
5. **Sustainment beyond go-live.**

### Ethics Angle

- **Honesty:** Change impact communicated truthfully.
- **Responsibility:** PM owns OCM integration.
- **Fairness:** Impact considered across affected groups.
- **Respect:** Adopters' concerns heard.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **OCM (Organizational Change Management)** | People-side of change | Trap: confusing with technical change |
| **ADKAR Model** | Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement | Trap: skipping stages |
| **Kotter 8-Step** | Urgency, Coalition, Vision, Communicate, Empower, Wins, Build, Anchor | Trap: order |
| **Lewin Model** | Unfreeze, Change, Refreeze | Trap: simple but powerful |
| **Stakeholder Readiness** | State of preparation for change | Trap: assumed |
| **Resistance Management** | Engaging resistance constructively | Trap: dismissing |
| **Change Champion / Sponsor** | Visible OCM advocate | Trap: low-profile |
| **Communication Plan (OCM)** | Audience-specific messaging | Trap: one-size-fits-all |
| **Training Plan (OCM)** | Capability building for adoption | Trap: skipping |
| **Sustainment** | Maintaining change long term | Trap: stopping at go-live |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| **Project initiated** with change implications | Develop OCM plan with sponsor + change manager | Initiate OCM |
| **Adoption** failing at go-live | Investigate cause (awareness, desire, knowledge, ability); coach team + sponsor | Diagnose |
| **Resistance** appearing | Engage; understand root cause; constructively address | Resistance = data |
| **Sponsor low-profile** | Engage sponsor on visibility importance; coach | Sponsor critical |
| **Training plan** missing | Build training plan; JIT delivery | Capability |
| **Stakeholder unreadiness** | Assess readiness; targeted engagement | Readiness |
| **Communication plan** ad-hoc | Audience-specific messaging plan | Discipline |
| **Sustainment plan** missing | Plan post-go-live reinforcement + measurement | Beyond go-live |
| **Multiple change initiatives** competing | Coordinate; brief sponsor on capacity | Change saturation |
| **Cultural fit** of change questionable | Surface; engage sponsor on cultural risk | Realistic |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Adoption likely to fail without OCM investment | Brief sponsor with readiness assessment, options, recommendation |
| Sponsor unwilling to be visible | Coach; if persistent, brief next governance level on adoption risk |

## 6. Visual Memory Aid

### OCM Integration with Project

```text
PROJECT INITIATED
        │
        ▼
OCM PLAN with sponsor + change manager
        │
        ▼
Engage stakeholders (impacted parties)
        │
        ▼
Build awareness + desire (ADKAR)
        │
        ▼
Build knowledge + ability (training, communication)
        │
        ▼
Reinforce (ADKAR-R): change champion network
        │
        ▼
GO-LIVE
        │
        ▼
Sustainment: measure adoption, reinforce, support
        │
        ▼
Sponsor visible throughout
```

### OCM Mnemonic — ADKAR

> **A**wareness (of need)
> **D**esire (to participate)
> **K**nowledge (how to change)
> **A**bility (to demonstrate)
> **R**einforcement (to sustain)

Memory hook: **"ADKAR — Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement."**

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Sponsor instructs you to hide expected job impact of automation project from affected employees until after go-live. What should you do FIRST?

A. Hide.
B. Refuse; engage sponsor on the ethics + adoption risk of hiding; propose honest communication with appropriate timing and support; brief HR.
C. Hide and document.
D. Disclose to affected directly bypassing sponsor.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

System being released has change implications. Team built features. Adoption likely low without OCM. PO doesn't see OCM as their job. What is the BEST action?

A. Skip OCM.
B. Engage PO + sponsor on adoption risk; identify change manager or accountable owner; embed OCM activities in release plan.
C. Hire OCM consultants without PO awareness.
D. Push features without OCM.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

Resistance emerging from affected user group. They feel imposed-upon. Sponsor wants to "push through." What should you do FIRST?

A. Push through.
B. Engage user group; understand concerns; co-create adoption approach; brief sponsor with resistance data and recommended engagement.
C. Replace users.
D. Cancel project.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

Go-live in 6 weeks. Affected stakeholder readiness assessment hasn't been done. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Conduct readiness assessment now; identify gaps.
B. Skip readiness; go-live anyway.
C. Plan accelerated training + communication targeting identified gaps.
D. Delay go-live indefinitely.
E. Skip OCM.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Predictive)**

OCM for new system. Arrange ADKAR:

1. Reinforcement (sustainment)
2. Desire (motivation)
3. Knowledge (training)
4. Awareness (communication)
5. Ability (practice)

A. 4 → 2 → 3 → 5 → 1
B. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5
C. 2 → 4 → 3 → 5 → 1
D. 4 → 3 → 2 → 1 → 5

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which is NOT a step in Kotter's 8-Step model?

A. Create urgency
B. Form a powerful coalition
C. Create and communicate vision
D. Empower action
E. Maintain status quo

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

Project automation will eliminate 50 roles. Sponsor wants to claim "no impact" in stakeholder communications. What do you do?

A. Comply.
B. Refuse; explain ethics + organizational consequences of concealment; propose honest communication with timing + support + redeployment plan; brief HR + sponsor.
C. Comply with caveat.
D. Disclose directly.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

Multiple affected groups with different concerns. What is the BEST OCM communication approach?

A. Same message to all.
B. Tailor message per affected group; consistent core message; multiple channels; sponsor-visible.
C. Stop communicating.
D. Daily detail.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

OCM activities chronically under-resourced. Adoption risk high. Sponsor unaware of OCM cost. You have raised once. What is the BEST next step?

A. Continue under-resourced.
B. Brief sponsor with adoption risk quantified, OCM resource gap, options (resource allocation, scope adjustment), and recommendation.
C. Hide.
D. Pivot unilaterally.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

Adoption failing at go-live. Affected users not aware of change purpose. Resistance growing. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Diagnose ADKAR stage where failure occurred.
B. Push users to comply.
C. Engage users; understand resistance root cause; address constructively.
D. Replace affected users.
E. Build sustainment + reinforcement plan; engage sponsor for visibility.
F. Cancel project.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty + adoption risk + HR.

**Mindset Lesson:** Material impact disclosed appropriately with support.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** OCM ownership identified + integrated.

**Mindset Lesson:** OCM not optional; identify owner + integrate.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Engage + understand + co-create + brief.

**Mindset Lesson:** Resistance engaged constructively.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Readiness assessment + targeted action.

**Mindset Lesson:** Readiness assessed + addressed before go-live.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: A (4 → 2 → 3 → 5 → 1)**

**PMI Justification:** Awareness → Desire → Knowledge → Ability → Reinforcement.

**Mindset Lesson:** ADKAR sequential.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** "Maintain status quo" is opposite of Kotter.

**Mindset Lesson:** Memorize Kotter 8 steps.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty + organizational ethics + HR.

**Mindset Lesson:** Workforce impact disclosed honestly with support.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Tailored + consistent core + multi-channel.

**Mindset Lesson:** OCM communication tailored per audience.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Adoption risk + resource gap + options.

**Mindset Lesson:** OCM under-resourcing escalated with data.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Diagnose ADKAR.
- C: Engage resistance.
- E: Sustainment + sponsor visibility.
- B: Push compliance.
- D: Replace users.
- F: Cancel.

**Mindset Lesson:** Adoption failure = diagnose + engage + sustainment.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**SUPPORT ORGANIZATIONAL CHANGE — Exam Morning Card**

- OCM = people-side of change. Distinct from project change.
- ADKAR: Awareness · Desire · Knowledge · Ability · Reinforcement.
- Kotter 8-step: Urgency · Coalition · Vision · Communicate · Empower · Wins · Build · Anchor.
- Lewin: Unfreeze · Change · Refreeze.
- Technical delivery ≠ adoption.
- OCM begins at initiation, not go-live.
- Sponsor visibility critical.
- Resistance is data, engaged constructively.
- Sustainment beyond go-live.
- Workforce impact disclosed honestly with support.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates OCM gaps with adoption risk and resource analysis — never hides workforce impact, never pushes users through resistance, never treats technical delivery as adoption.*
