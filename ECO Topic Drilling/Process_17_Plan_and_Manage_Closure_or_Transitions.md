# PMP Exam Master Brief: Plan and Manage Project / Phase Closure or Transitions (Process · Task 17)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Planning and managing project/phase closure is the discipline of completing all work properly — formal acceptance, deliverable transition, financial reconciliation, contract closeout, lessons capture, archive, and team release — so the project ends cleanly with traceability and continuity. PMI treats closure as a deliberate phase, not an afterthought. Topics include closure criteria, formal acceptance, transition to operations, contract closeout, lessons learned final, archive, and team release. The Process domain is 50% of the exam; closure threads into integration, procurement, and knowledge. In real projects, closure is skipped under pressure to start next project; on the exam, PMI rewards proper closure discipline.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about formally closing projects and phases through acceptance, transition, contract closeout, lessons capture, and archive — not skipping to the next thing.

---

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Closure Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Formal closeout phase; acceptance + lessons + archive + contract close |
| **Agile** | Release transitions; product handover continuous; final lessons + retrospective |
| **Hybrid** | Phase closures + final closeout |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Closure is a phase, not an afterthought.**
2. **Formal acceptance required.**
3. **Lessons + archive non-negotiable.**
4. **Contract closeout requires audit-trail.**
5. **Team release respectful.**

### Ethics Angle

- **Honesty:** Closure status truthful.
- **Responsibility:** PM owns closure.
- **Fairness:** Final compensation/recognition fair.
- **Respect:** Team adjourning celebrated.

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Approaching **closure** | Verify closure criteria met; plan closeout | Criteria-driven |
| **Acceptance** not obtained | Validate Scope with stakeholders; ICC if change | Formal acceptance |
| **Transition** to operations | Plan handover; documentation; training | Continuity |
| **Contract** with vendor | Closeout per contract terms; financial reconciliation | Procurement |
| **Lessons** not captured | Capture now; review with team; archive | Final lesson |
| **Archive** undefined | Per records retention | Compliance |
| **Team release** | Performance assessment + recognition + transition support | Respect |
| **Project terminated** early | Same closure discipline; lessons + archive | Discipline |
| Phase **complete** | Phase closeout; gate review; next phase start | Sequential |
| **Punch list** items remain | Address through transition plan; track to closure | Open items |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Sponsor refuses to invest in proper closure | Brief sponsor with cost of skipping (lessons loss, audit risk, team morale) and propose minimum closure |
| Stakeholder withholds acceptance unreasonably | Engage; understand; ICC if change warranted; escalate to governance if blocked |

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Formal closeout phase
- Acceptance + lessons + archive + contract close

### Agile / Hybrid

- Release transitions continuous
- Final lessons in last retrospective
- Closeout activities lighter but present

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** records retention 7+ years
- BSEC / Bangladesh Bank: closure documentation audited
- **Public-sector:** closure often subject to audit

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Sponsor wants you to close the project immediately to free your team for next initiative — skipping acceptance, lessons, and archive. Closure criteria not yet met. What should you do FIRST?

A. Skip and close.
B. Refuse; explain cost of skipping (audit risk, lessons loss); propose accelerated proper closeout with minimum activities.
C. Skip and document.
D. Defer.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

Product team is moving to next release. Current release artifacts in various states. Some lessons not captured. What is the BEST action?

A. Move to next release.
B. Final retrospective with lessons capture; transition deliverables to operations; archive artifacts; then move.
C. Lessons later.
D. Discard artifacts.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

Stakeholder withholds formal acceptance citing concerns not in original criteria. Project delivered per scope. What should you do FIRST?

A. Force acceptance.
B. Engage stakeholder; understand concerns; assess if legitimate (validate against criteria); if change warranted, route through ICC; if not, document declines and escalate.
C. Bypass stakeholder.
D. Cancel project.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

Project being terminated early due to strategic shift. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Capture lessons learned with team.
B. Disband team immediately.
C. Plan structured closeout (transition + contract closeout + archive).
D. Discard incomplete artifacts.
E. Skip transition.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Predictive)**

Closing project. Arrange:

1. Team release with performance assessment + recognition.
2. Final lessons learned + archive.
3. Formal acceptance from sponsor + transition to operations.
4. Contract closeout with vendors.

A. 3 → 4 → 2 → 1
B. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
C. 2 → 3 → 4 → 1
D. 4 → 3 → 2 → 1

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which is NOT typically part of project closeout?

A. Formal acceptance
B. Lessons learned
C. Archive
D. Contract closeout
E. Stakeholder hiring decisions

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

You discover at closeout that final report omits material lessons that would help future projects. Sponsor preferred omission. What do you do?

A. Submit as-is.
B. Surface lessons honestly in final report; brief sponsor on lessons value; propose framing constructively while keeping content.
C. Omit and document.
D. Defer.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

Closeout communications: sponsor wants summary; vendor wants closeout letter; team wants celebration. What is the BEST approach?

A. Same closure email to all.
B. Tailor: closeout report to sponsor; formal closeout letter + financial reconciliation to vendor; team celebration with lessons capture + recognition.
C. Skip communications.
D. Long combined to all.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

Vendor refuses to sign closeout despite delivering per contract. Vendor wants additional fees. What is the BEST next step?

A. Pay additional fees.
B. Engage vendor with contract evidence; if no resolution, brief sponsor + procurement + legal with vendor position, contract terms, and recommended action.
C. Skip closeout.
D. Accept vendor's demand.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

Project approaching closure with multiple incomplete items: some pending acceptance, some vendor closeout, lessons partial. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Build closeout checklist; track to completion.
B. Skip incomplete items.
C. Engage stakeholders for acceptance; engage vendors for closeout; capture lessons before disbanding.
D. Disband team to focus on next project.
E. Communicate closure status transparently to all parties.
F. Discard incomplete artifacts.

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**PLAN AND MANAGE CLOSURE OR TRANSITIONS — Exam Morning Card**

- ATCLAT: Acceptance · Transition · Contract closeout · Lessons · Archive · Team release.
- Closure is a phase, not afterthought.
- Formal acceptance required; verbal insufficient.
- Lessons captured before disbanding.
- Archive per records retention.
- Contract closeout: performance + reconciliation + sign-off.
- Team release respectful: performance + recognition + transition support.
- Early termination = same closure discipline.
- Phase closure includes gate review.
- Closure status communicated tailored to audience.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates closure refusal with cost of skipping (lessons, audit, team morale) and proposes minimum proper closeout — never absorbs skipping, never accepts verbal acceptance, never disbands before closure.*
