# PMP Exam Master Brief: Mentor Relevant Stakeholders (People · Task 13)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Mentoring is the relationship-based practice of sharing experience and perspective to develop another person's career, judgment, and capability — distinct from coaching (which develops skill through questions) and training (which transfers specific competence). PMI expects PMs to mentor relevant stakeholders (junior PMs, team members aspiring to leadership, even peer PMs in some contexts) and to identify mentors for themselves and team members. Topics include mentoring vs coaching vs training distinction, succession planning, knowledge transfer, and mentor matching. The People domain is 42% of the exam; mentoring threads into team performance, training, leadership, and team building. In real organizations, mentoring is ad-hoc and personal; on the exam, PMI rewards the option that treats mentoring as structured investment with clear objectives and matched relationships.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about deliberate, relationship-based capability development through experience sharing — distinct from coaching and training.

---

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Mentoring Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Structured mentoring programs; cross-project mentor matching |
| **Agile** | Peer pairing; community of practice; mentorship within team and across teams |
| **Hybrid** | Mix of formal mentorship + peer learning + cross-team mentor relationships |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Mentoring ≠ coaching ≠ training.** Use the right tool for the need.
2. **Mentoring is about career and judgment, not task.** Long horizon.
3. **Match mentor and mentee deliberately.** Random pairing rarely works.
4. **Mentoring builds succession.** Critical roles need pipeline.
5. **PMs mentor and are mentored.** Lifelong learning.

### Ethics Angle

- **Respect:** Mentoring is voluntary engagement of mentor's experience.
- **Honesty:** Honest feedback delivered with care.
- **Fairness:** Mentoring opportunity distributed; not just inner circle.
- **Responsibility:** Mentoring relationships honored; not abandoned mid-stream.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Mentoring** | Long-horizon relationship for career and judgment development | Trap: confusing with coaching or training |
| **Coaching** | Skill / performance development through questions | Trap: defaulting to "tell" mode |
| **Training** | Specific competence transfer | Trap: assuming mentoring replaces training |
| **Sponsor (Career)** | Senior advocate who opens opportunities for mentee | Trap: confusing with project sponsor |
| **Reverse Mentoring** | Junior mentors senior on new domain | Trap: assuming mentor must be older |
| **Peer Mentoring** | Colleagues mentor each other | Trap: assuming hierarchical only |
| **Mentor Matching** | Deliberate pairing based on goals + style | Trap: random pairing |
| **Succession Planning** | Pipeline for critical roles | Trap: ignoring; SPOF risk |
| **Community of Practice** | Cross-team peer learning groups | Trap: assuming 1:1 mentoring suffices |
| **Mentoring Compact** | Explicit agreement on goals, cadence, confidentiality | Trap: leaving implicit |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Team member expresses **career interest** | Listen; explore goals; consider mentor matching | Career-level development |
| Junior PM joins org | Match with experienced mentor; set compact | Structured onboarding |
| Critical role has **no successor** | Identify candidate; mentor-match; build pipeline | Succession |
| Senior member can mentor but **doesn't volunteer** | Engage; offer; discuss workload protection | Mentoring is asset |
| PM **lacks mentor** | Identify; engage; build relationship | PMs need mentors too |
| Mentoring relationship **stalled** | 1:1 with both parties; assess compact alignment; rematch if needed | Relationships drift |
| Sponsor demands mentor be **PM only** | Offer self if appropriate; suggest peer or external mentor option | Capacity limits |
| Mentee wants **specific skill** | Likely needs training/coaching, not mentoring | Right tool |
| Community of practice **emerging** | Support; provide forum; participate | Peer learning multiplies |
| Mentoring **opportunity given to inner circle only** | Audit fairness; broaden access | Fairness in development |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Mentor commits ethics breach during mentoring relationship | Escalate to HR; mentee protected; pattern documented |
| Critical succession gap cannot be resolved through internal mentoring | Brief sponsor with gap, options (hire, external mentor, contract-to-hire), recommendation |

## 6. Visual Memory Aid

### Mentoring vs Coaching vs Training

```text
              MENTORING       COACHING        TRAINING
Horizon:      Long (career)   Mid (perf)      Short (skill)
Method:       Share experience Ask questions  Transfer competence
Relationship: 1:1 ongoing     1:1 episodic    Group or 1:1 event
Focus:        Judgment +      Skill +         Specific
              career          performance     competence
Outcome:      Wisdom +        Improved        Capability
              opportunities   performance     gain
```

### Mentoring Mnemonic: **M-M-C-S**

> **M**atch mentor and mentee deliberately
> **M**entoring compact (goals, cadence, confidentiality)
> **C**adence sustained
> **S**uccession built through mentoring

Memory hook: **"MMCS — match, compact, cadence, succession."**

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Mentoring = coaching = training" | Convenient | Each is different |
| "PM mentors everyone personally" | Hands-on | Capacity limits; PM also needs mentor |
| "Mentor by giving advice unprompted" | Sharing experience | Imposing; not invited |
| "Random mentor pairings" | Fair distribution | Mismatched pairs fail |
| "Mentor only when junior asks" | Respect autonomy | Many never ask; pipeline failures |
| "Mentoring at year-end review" | Structured | Long-horizon needs cadence |
| "Inner-circle mentoring" | Trusted | Fairness violation |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Your sponsor asks you to be the formal mentor for the sponsor's nephew, who has just joined as a junior PM. You have no prior relationship with the nephew. What should you do FIRST?

A. Decline outright.
B. Engage sponsor on the matching principle; offer alternative mentor options that fit nephew's goals; if appropriate, agree to one introductory conversation to assess fit.
C. Accept without conditions.
D. Escalate to HR.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

A junior team member wants to grow into a tech-lead role over the next 12 months. The team operates with strong pair programming culture. What is the BEST action?

A. Defer to next performance review.
B. Match with experienced tech lead for mentoring relationship; co-create mentoring compact (goals, cadence); leverage pair programming for continuous skill transfer.
C. Send junior to formal training only.
D. Promote immediately.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

A mentor and mentee report that their relationship has "stalled." Neither wants to continue. What should you do FIRST?

A. Force continuation.
B. Hold separate 1:1s to understand; respect both parties' autonomy; rematch mentee with another mentor if interest remains.
C. Mandate weekly check-ins.
D. Escalate to functional manager.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

You identify that a critical role has no successor. The current incumbent will likely retire in 24 months. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Identify candidate(s) and match with incumbent for mentoring.
B. Wait until 6 months before retirement to start succession.
C. Document knowledge in formal handover artifacts in parallel with mentoring.
D. Hire external replacement now.
E. Cancel the role.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

Setting up a new mentoring relationship. Arrange:

1. Establish mentoring compact (goals, cadence, confidentiality).
2. Identify mentee's career goals and gaps.
3. Match with mentor based on goals + style.
4. Sustain cadence; review compact periodically.

A. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
B. 2 → 3 → 1 → 4
C. 3 → 1 → 2 → 4
D. 4 → 2 → 3 → 1

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following BEST describes mentoring vs coaching vs training?

A. Mentoring is long-horizon career and judgment development.
B. Coaching uses questions to develop performance and skill.
C. Training transfers specific competence.
D. All three terms are interchangeable.
E. Mentoring is relationship-based and sustained.

(Select the statement that is INCORRECT.)

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

You discover the senior mentor in a high-profile mentoring relationship has been claiming credit for the mentee's project work in steering meetings. What do you do?

A. Stay silent to protect the mentor relationship.
B. Engage the mentor privately about the impact; ensure credit is corrected going forward; if pattern persists, escalate to functional manager and HR; protect mentee.
C. Reassign the mentee.
D. Continue without intervention.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

Your team has talented juniors who would benefit from cross-team mentor relationships. The org has no formal mentoring program. What is the BEST approach?

A. Wait for org to build a program.
B. Identify potential mentors across teams; propose lightweight matching; start with two volunteer pairs; document outcomes for org-wide expansion.
C. Hire external mentors.
D. Mentor all juniors yourself.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

You are mentoring a junior PM. After 6 months, the junior PM reveals that a senior colleague has been taking inappropriate credit for the junior's work for several months. The junior has documented evidence. What is the BEST next step?

A. Tell the junior to handle it directly.
B. Together with the junior, plan response: gather evidence, escalate to functional manager / HR with documentation; support the junior through the process.
C. Confront the senior yourself.
D. Reassign the junior.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

You want to build a mentoring culture across multiple teams. Currently, mentoring is ad-hoc. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Identify volunteer mentors and mentees; deliberate matching.
B. Mandate all senior staff be mentors.
C. Establish mentoring compacts (goals, cadence, confidentiality).
D. Replace mentoring with formal training programs.
E. Build community of practice for peer learning.
F. Audit mentoring opportunity distribution for fairness.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Matching principle + alternatives + introductory conversation respects nephew, sponsor, and fairness.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sponsor-imposed mentoring matched on principle and fit, not relationship.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Match + compact + leverage existing pair culture.

**Mindset Lesson:** Career-development paths combine mentor + continuous skill transfer.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Diagnose + respect autonomy + rematch if needed.

**Mindset Lesson:** Mentor relationships honored; not forced.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Mentor matching + parallel documentation covers people and artifact.

**Mindset Lesson:** Succession is mentoring + documentation, not one or the other.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: B (2 → 3 → 1 → 4)**

**PMI Justification:** Goals → match → compact → sustain.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sequence ensures fit before commitment.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: D (incorrect statement)**

**PMI Justification:** Mentoring, coaching, training are distinct tools.

**Mindset Lesson:** Distinguish three tools; use the right one.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Private engagement + correction + escalation if pattern; protect mentee.

**Mindset Lesson:** Mentor misconduct addressed privately first; escalate with pattern.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Lightweight bottom-up matching builds culture; document for scaling.

**Mindset Lesson:** Mentoring culture built grassroots when formal program absent.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Joint planning + escalate to FM/HR with documentation + support mentee.

**Mindset Lesson:** Mentor supports mentee through escalation; does not handle for them.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Volunteer matching.
- C: Compacts.
- E: Community of practice.
- B: Mandate kills voluntariness.
- D: Training replaces mentoring.
- F: Audit good but not foundational.

**Mindset Lesson:** Mentoring culture = voluntary matching + compacts + community. Not mandate, not training replacement.
