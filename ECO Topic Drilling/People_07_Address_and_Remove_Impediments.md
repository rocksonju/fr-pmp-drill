# PMP Exam Master Brief: Address and Remove Impediments, Obstacles, and Blockers (People · Task 7)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Addressing and removing impediments is the servant-leader's core operating practice — identifying what is slowing or blocking the team and clearing it at the lowest effective level, escalating only when the impediment requires authority beyond the PM/Scrum Master. PMI treats impediment removal as proactive, not reactive: PM scans for blockers, captures them in an impediment log, and clears them with urgency. Topics include impediment identification (standups, retrospectives, 1:1s), root cause analysis, impediment log, and escalation paths. The People domain is 42% of the exam; this task threads into leadership, team performance, and process domain stakeholder/risk topics. In real teams, impediments accumulate silently because team members assume "that's just how it is"; on the exam, PMI rewards the option that surfaces, names, and clears the blocker — and treats absorbed impediments as leadership failure.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about active, urgent removal of anything slowing the team — surface, root-cause, clear at lowest level, escalate when authority demands.

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Impediment Removal Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Issue log, escalation paths documented, PM-driven removal |
| **Agile** | Impediment log/board, surface in daily standup, SM removes or escalates |
| **Hybrid** | Impediment surfacing through ceremonies + documented escalation paths |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Impediment removal is the PM/SM's primary job.** Not status reporting, not coordination — clearing blockers.
2. **Root cause before action.** Surface symptom is rarely the actual impediment.
3. **Escalate when authority demands.** Not because the impediment is hard.
4. **Impediment log is visible.** Hidden impediments don't get removed.
5. **Absorbed impediments train team helplessness.** "We worked around it" is a failure signal.

### Ethics Angle

- **Responsibility:** Impediments owned by PM until cleared or escalated.
- **Honesty:** Surface impediments openly; do not hide to protect status colors.
- **Respect:** Team members raising impediments are heard, not dismissed.
- **Fairness:** Impediment priority by impact, not by who raised it.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Impediment** | Anything slowing the team's ability to deliver value | Trap: confusing with risk; impediment is current, risk is future |
| **Obstacle / Blocker** | Synonyms for impediment | Trap: treating differently; same concept |
| **Impediment Log / Board** | Visible list of impediments with owner and status | Trap: keeping invisible |
| **Daily Standup / Scrum** | Daily team event surfacing impediments | Trap: turning into status report |
| **Root Cause Analysis** | Identify underlying cause, not just symptom | Trap: clearing surface symptom |
| **5 Whys** | Sequential "why" questions to find root cause | Trap: stopping at first plausible answer |
| **Ishikawa / Fishbone** | Categorized cause-effect diagram | Trap: confusing with Pareto |
| **Escalation Path** | Documented route for impediments beyond PM authority | Trap: undocumented; reflex-driven |
| **Servant Leadership** | Active removal of impediments + team support | Trap: passive |
| **Impediment Aging** | Time impediment has been open | Trap: ignoring; aging signals priority |
| **Cycle Time Impact** | How much an impediment delays flow | Trap: not measuring |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Team member raises **impediment** in standup | Log it; assign owner (often PM); clear or escalate | Visible + owned + actioned |
| **Impediment recurs** | Root cause analysis (5 Whys); fix the cause | Symptom clearance fails |
| Impediment requires **another team's input** | Engage other team's PM/SM; coordinate | Cross-team impediments common |
| Impediment **exceeds PM authority** | Escalate to sponsor with impact and recommendation | Authority threshold |
| Team has **worked around** an impediment silently | Surface it; log; assess if cause still exists | Workarounds hide impediments |
| **Vendor delay** is blocking the team | Engage vendor PM through contract; escalate procurement if needed | Vendor impediment goes through contract |
| **Tooling** impediment slows team | Procure or fix; do not let team improvise indefinitely | Tooling cost < team productivity loss |
| Team **stops raising** impediments | Investigate; check psychological safety | Silence = often suppression |
| Impediment log is **aging** | Review priorities; clear or escalate | Aging signals neglect |
| Sponsor **dismisses** impediment as "team's job to figure out" | Push back with impact data; restore PM's impediment-removal role | Servant-leader role not negotiable |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Impediment exceeds PM authority (cross-team, vendor contract, organizational policy) | Escalate to sponsor with impediment, impact, attempts, and recommended action |
| Impediment recurs despite escalation — sponsor not acting | Re-engage sponsor with aging data; escalate to next governance level if pattern recurs |

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Issue log; escalation paths in governance
- PM-driven removal with team input

### Agile / Hybrid

- Impediment board visible in team space
- Daily standup surfaces impediments
- SM owns clearance + escalation
- Retrospective identifies recurring patterns

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** approval bottlenecks structural; negotiate pre-approved categories
- BSEC / Bangladesh Bank regulatory holds frequent — embed in impediment process
- **Vendor-heavy:** vendor impediments through contract escalation
- **Multinational:** time-zone impediments need design, not absorption

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Let the team figure out their own impediments" | Self-organization | Servant leader removes impediments |
| "Document and revisit in retrospective" | Discipline | Documenting ≠ removing; aging hurts |
| "Escalate every impediment to sponsor for visibility" | Transparency | Sponsor not the right level for most impediments |
| "Tell team to work around the impediment" | Pragmatic delivery | Workaround masks impediment |
| "Solve the surface symptom" | Quick win | Symptom recurs; root cause remains |
| "Treat impediment as risk in risk register" | Risk discipline | Impediment is current, not future |
| "Wait for impediment to resolve naturally" | Patience | Aging impediments cost compounding |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

You identify that a recurring impediment is caused by a senior stakeholder's pattern of late approvals. The stakeholder is the sponsor's peer. Escalating may damage relationship. What should you do FIRST?

A. Continue absorbing.
B. Engage the stakeholder directly with impact data; if unresolved, brief sponsor with the pattern, attempts, and impact.
C. Escalate to sponsor immediately.
D. Have the team work around late approvals.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

During daily standup, team members consistently report blockers but the SM does not log or follow up. Velocity dropping. What is the BEST action as the new PM/SM?

A. Continue current standup format.
B. Reset standup format to ensure impediment capture; introduce visible impediment board; review aging weekly; coach team that surfacing is expected.
C. Cancel standups.
D. Have team escalate to sponsor directly.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

A vendor's slow response is blocking three stories. Vendor manager defends current SLA. PO wants the stories removed from the sprint. What should you do FIRST?

A. Remove stories from sprint.
B. Engage vendor with impact data; explore options (escalate within vendor, prioritization, contract negotiation); brief PO + procurement.
C. Escalate to procurement.
D. Tolerate slow response.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

Impediment log shows 12 open impediments aged 30+ days. Team morale low. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Prioritize impediments by impact and clear top 3.
B. Hide the impediment log to reduce stress.
C. Brief sponsor on aged impediments needing governance support; request unblocking.
D. Close impediments as "won't fix" to clean the log.
E. Add more impediments to ensure visibility.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

A team member raises a new impediment in daily standup. Arrange:

1. Engage relevant party (team, vendor, FM, sponsor) for action.
2. Log impediment with owner, impact, aging.
3. Verify clearance with team + capture lesson if pattern.
4. Perform root cause analysis if recurring.

A. 2 → 1 → 3 → 4
B. 1 → 2 → 4 → 3
C. 2 → 4 → 1 → 3
D. 4 → 2 → 1 → 3

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following is NOT typically a source of impediments?

A. Cross-team dependencies
B. Approval bottlenecks
C. Tooling/environment issues
D. Vendor delays
E. Project closure activities

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

You discover the team has been silently working around a security-tooling impediment by manually disabling a control. This may be a compliance breach. The team has been doing this for weeks. What do you do?

A. Tell the team to continue but document.
B. Stop the workaround immediately; brief sponsor and security/compliance; engage to clear the underlying impediment; document past activities for assessment.
C. Quietly fix the impediment without disclosing.
D. Reprimand the team for the workaround.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

The team uses a digital impediment board accessible to all stakeholders. The sponsor complains it shows "too many problems." Sponsor wants a clean dashboard. What is the BEST approach?

A. Hide the impediment board.
B. Keep the impediment board live for team and PMO transparency; produce a sponsor-tailored summary highlighting aged + escalated items + clearance velocity; coach sponsor on impediment visibility as a leading indicator.
C. Stop logging impediments.
D. Move impediments to a private list.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

An impediment is caused by an organizational policy outside your authority. You have engaged the policy owner twice without movement. Cycle time impact is significant. What is the BEST next step?

A. Continue absorbing.
B. Brief sponsor with impediment, impact data, engagement attempts, and recommended governance action.
C. Direct team to violate the policy.
D. Wait for next policy review.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

Team has been delivering steadily but impediments are accumulating in the log. Some are aging 60+ days. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Prioritize by impact and aging; clear top items.
B. Close aged impediments without resolution to clean log.
C. Brief sponsor on aged impediments requiring governance support.
D. Mandate team to stop raising new impediments.
E. Run a focused retrospective on impediment patterns and structural causes.
F. Add monetary bonuses for impediment clearance.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Engage directly first; escalate with documentation if unresolved.

**Mindset Lesson:** Impediment escalation follows direct engagement.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Reset format + visible board + aging review + coaching restores servant-leader role.

**Mindset Lesson:** Impediment surfacing is structural; rebuild the structure.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Engage + explore + brief covers vendor mechanics.

**Mindset Lesson:** Vendor impediment is through engagement + procurement coordination.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** Clear top + escalate aged combines tactical + governance moves.

**Mindset Lesson:** Aged impediment log = prioritize + escalate, never hide or fake-close.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: A (2 → 1 → 3 → 4)**

**PMI Justification:** Log → engage → verify → root cause if pattern.

**Mindset Lesson:** Root cause comes after pattern detection.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** Closure activities are project work, not impediments.

**Mindset Lesson:** Distinguish impediment sources from project activities.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty + Responsibility — stop, brief, engage, document.

**Mindset Lesson:** Compliance-breaching workaround escalated immediately.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Keep transparency + audience-tailored summary + coach sponsor on indicator meaning.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sponsor discomfort with impediment visibility = coaching opportunity, not hiding.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Two engagement attempts failed; sponsor briefing with data and recommendation.

**Mindset Lesson:** Structural impediments need governance — escalate with data.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Prioritize and clear.
- C: Escalate aged for governance.
- E: Focused retro on patterns.
- B: Fake-closing is dishonest.
- D: Mandates breaks surfacing.
- F: Bonuses produce gaming.

**Mindset Lesson:** Aged impediment log = prioritize + escalate + pattern retro. Never hide, fake-close, mandate, or bonus.
