# PMP Exam Master Brief: Negotiate Project Agreements (People · Task 8)

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Negotiating project agreements is the practice of reaching mutually acceptable arrangements with stakeholders, sponsors, vendors, and team members about scope, schedule, resources, contracts, and trade-offs. PMI treats negotiation as principled problem-solving, not positional bargaining — find shared interests, expand the pie before splitting it, and never sacrifice integrity for short-term concession. Topics include negotiation strategies, BATNA/ZOPA, contract types, win-win outcomes, and integrative vs distributive negotiation. The People domain is 42% of the exam; negotiation threads into procurement, stakeholder, scope, and schedule. In real organizations, negotiation defaults to "who has more leverage"; on the exam, PMI rewards principled negotiation that produces durable agreements.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about principled, interest-based negotiation that produces durable, win-win agreements — not leverage-driven bargaining.

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Negotiation Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Formal contracts; scope/schedule/cost negotiated at planning; change requests negotiated through ICC |
| **Agile** | Continuous trade-off conversations within team agreement; PO negotiates priority with stakeholders; contracts tailored (T&M, ranged scope) |
| **Hybrid** | Formal contract framework + iterative scope/priority negotiation within sprints |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **Interest-based > position-based.** Behind every position is an interest; address the interest.
2. **Know your BATNA.** Without BATNA, you have no walk-away power.
3. **Integrative > distributive.** Expand the pie before splitting it.
4. **Never trade ethics for concessions.** Integrity is not negotiable.
5. **Document agreements.** Verbal agreements drift; written agreements anchor.

### Ethics Angle

- **Honesty:** Represent the project's position truthfully; do not bluff.
- **Fairness:** Both parties' interests considered, even with leverage asymmetry.
- **Respect:** Counterparty's needs heard, not dismissed.
- **Responsibility:** Negotiated agreements honored; renegotiate openly if circumstances change.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **BATNA** | Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement | Trap: ignoring; BATNA defines walk-away |
| **ZOPA** | Zone of Possible Agreement (overlap of reservation prices) | Trap: assuming ZOPA exists; sometimes does not |
| **Reservation Price** | Walk-away point | Trap: revealing |
| **Integrative Negotiation** | Win-win; expand value | Trap: defaulting to distributive |
| **Distributive Negotiation** | Win-lose; split fixed pie | Trap: assuming all negotiations are zero-sum |
| **Principled Negotiation** | Separate people from problem; focus on interests; invent options; objective criteria | Trap: positional bargaining |
| **Anchoring** | First number/position shapes range | Trap: letting other side anchor |
| **Contract Types** | Fixed Price (FP), Cost Plus (CR), Time & Materials (T&M) | Trap: matching wrong type to context |
| **Fixed Price (FP)** | Buyer risk low if scope clear; seller risk high | Trap: using FP with unclear scope |
| **Cost Reimbursable (CR)** | Buyer risk high; seller risk low | Trap: no buyer incentive control |
| **Cost Plus Fixed Fee (CPFF)** | Cost reimbursed + fixed fee | Trap: assuming all CR types are same |
| **Cost Plus Incentive Fee (CPIF)** | Cost reimbursed + incentive based on performance | Trap: complex incentive design |
| **Cost Plus Award Fee (CPAF)** | Cost reimbursed + subjective award | Trap: subjectivity disputes |
| **Time & Materials (T&M)** | Hourly + materials | Trap: open-ended without cap |
| **MOU / SLA / Contract** | Memorandum of Understanding, Service Level Agreement, formal contract | Trap: confusing legal weight |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Stakeholder pushes back on **scope** | Understand the interest behind the position; explore options | Position-only response misses interest |
| Vendor proposes **fixed price** for unclear scope | Negotiate scope clarity first; or change to T&M | FP requires clear scope |
| Sponsor demands **lower cost** | Explore scope/schedule/quality trade-offs; do not absorb silently | Trade-offs are explicit |
| Two stakeholders have **conflicting** demands | Facilitate joint session; find shared interest | Joint > parallel |
| You have **no BATNA** | Develop one before negotiating critical agreement | No BATNA = no leverage |
| Counterparty makes **first offer** | Anchor or counter-anchor; do not accept silently | Anchoring shapes range |
| Agreement **drifts** from documented | Re-anchor through written confirmation | Verbal drift is common |
| Counterparty **misrepresents** facts | Address directly; do not retaliate; document | Integrity not optional |
| Sponsor demands **bluff** | Refuse; propose principled position | Bluffing breaches Honesty |
| Change request **negotiable** with PO | Quantify impact; explore alternatives | Impact-based negotiation |

**Escalation-Specific Scenarios:**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Vendor negotiation breakdown threatens contract | Brief sponsor + procurement with positions, BATNA, recommendation |
| Counterparty breaches negotiated agreement | Document; engage directly; escalate to governance if pattern recurs |

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project

- Formal contracts; legal review heavy
- Negotiation rounds structured

### Agile / Hybrid

- Continuous trade-off conversation within team agreement
- PO negotiates priority with stakeholders
- Contracts tailored (T&M, ranged scope)

### Industry / Regulatory Factors

- **Banking / Capital Markets:** vendor contracts heavily regulated; legal review required
- BSEC / Bangladesh Bank counterparty agreements may have regulator-imposed terms
- **Public-sector:** RFP-driven; principled negotiation within procurement rules
- **Cross-cultural:** negotiation norms vary; calibrate

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Take the first offer to be agreeable" | Quick close | Without anchoring/exploration, leaves value on table |
| "Use leverage to force concessions" | Faster | Damages durability of agreement |
| "Bluff the BATNA" | Strengthens position | Breaches Honesty |
| "Negotiate position-by-position" | Structured | Misses underlying interests |
| "Sign agreement without documenting" | Trust-based | Drift; disputes |
| "Accept fixed price for unclear scope" | Apparent budget certainty | Sets up disputes; vendor exits or quality drops |
| "Refuse to walk away" | Commitment | Without BATNA = bad agreements |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

Your sponsor instructs you to "tell the vendor we have another supplier lined up at half the price" to pressure concessions. You have no such alternative. What should you do FIRST?

A. Bluff as instructed.
B. Refuse to bluff; develop genuine BATNA (alternative quotes); propose principled negotiation; document the refusal.
C. Bluff but document.
D. Escalate to procurement.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

The PO and a senior stakeholder are negotiating priority of a new compliance feature. PO position: "next sprint." Stakeholder position: "now." What is the BEST facilitation?

A. Pick a side.
B. Surface interests behind positions (PO interest: protect sprint goal; stakeholder interest: regulatory deadline); explore options (split, time-box, parallel); use objective criteria (deadline date, regulatory risk).
C. Defer decision.
D. Escalate to sponsor.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

A vendor proposes Fixed Price for a scope that is still evolving. The vendor account manager pushes hard for quick signature. What should you do FIRST?

A. Sign to preserve vendor relationship.
B. Negotiate either scope clarity before FP, or change contract type to T&M with cap; engage procurement.
C. Sign and renegotiate later.
D. Reject the vendor.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

You are entering a critical vendor negotiation. The vendor's proposed terms are higher than your target. Which TWO actions FIRST?

A. Identify your BATNA and reservation price.
B. Accept the proposed terms to avoid risk.
C. Identify the vendor's interests behind their position.
D. Threaten to terminate the relationship.
E. Sign and address terms in next renewal.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Predictive)**

Negotiating a contract amendment. Arrange:

1. Anchor with principled opening based on objective criteria.
2. Identify shared interests and invent options for mutual gain.
3. Prepare BATNA and reservation price.
4. Document agreement.

A. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
B. 3 → 2 → 1 → 4
C. 2 → 1 → 3 → 4
D. 4 → 3 → 2 → 1

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following is NOT a typical contract type?

A. Fixed Price (FP)
B. Cost Plus Fixed Fee (CPFF)
C. Time and Materials (T&M)
D. Cost Plus Incentive Fee (CPIF)
E. Fixed Cost Reimbursable Award (FCRA)

---

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

A vendor's senior negotiator misrepresents a material fact about delivery capacity during a negotiation. You notice. The misrepresentation, if uncorrected, would benefit your project. What do you do?

A. Stay silent and benefit from the misrepresentation.
B. Address the misrepresentation directly during the negotiation; ask for clarification; if confirmed misrepresentation, factor into agreement and consider escalation to vendor leadership.
C. Use the misrepresentation as leverage.
D. End the negotiation.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

You are negotiating with a stakeholder over scope. The stakeholder communicates via long, position-heavy emails. You have responded with similar emails. Progress is stalled. What is the BEST approach?

A. Continue email exchange.
B. Move to a synchronous conversation focused on underlying interests; capture agreed points in writing afterward.
C. Escalate to sponsor.
D. Accept stakeholder's position to unstick.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

Vendor negotiation has reached impasse. Vendor's reservation price is above your reservation price. No ZOPA exists. Walking would significantly impact delivery. What is the BEST next step?

A. Sign at vendor's price.
B. Brief sponsor with both positions, BATNA analysis, impact of walk vs sign, and recommendation; request decision.
C. Refuse without sponsor consultation.
D. Bluff additional budget.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

You are preparing for a critical multi-party negotiation with vendor, sponsor, and business stakeholders. Which THREE actions MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Identify each party's interests and likely BATNA.
B. Plan to use leverage over the weakest party.
C. Identify objective criteria all parties can accept.
D. Prepare aggressive opening positions.
E. Prepare options that create value for multiple parties.
F. Bluff your BATNA to strengthen leverage.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Honesty principle; refuse to bluff; develop genuine BATNA.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sponsor pressure does not waive Honesty in negotiation.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Surface interests; options; objective criteria — textbook principled negotiation.

**Mindset Lesson:** Priority conflict resolved through interests + options + criteria.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** FP needs scope clarity; otherwise T&M with cap.

**Mindset Lesson:** Contract type matches scope certainty.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** BATNA + counterparty interests are textbook preparation.

**Mindset Lesson:** Negotiation prep = own BATNA + counterparty interests.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: B (3 → 2 → 1 → 4)**

**PMI Justification:** Prepare BATNA → identify interests → anchor principled → document.

**Mindset Lesson:** BATNA before anchoring; document after agreement.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** FCRA is not a standard type.

**Mindset Lesson:** Memorize standard contract types.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Address misrepresentation; do not exploit.

**Mindset Lesson:** Counterparty misrepresentation addressed, not exploited.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Synchronous conversation focused on interests; capture afterward.

**Mindset Lesson:** Position-stalled email = move to interests in real-time.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Sponsor briefing with positions + BATNA + impact + recommendation.

**Mindset Lesson:** No ZOPA = sponsor governance decision with options.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**

- A: Interests + BATNAs.
- C: Objective criteria.
- E: Value-creating options.
- B: Leverage over weakest is unprincipled.
- D: Aggressive opening damages relationship.
- F: Bluffing breaches Honesty.

**Mindset Lesson:** Multi-party negotiation prep = interests + criteria + value-creating options.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**NEGOTIATE PROJECT AGREEMENTS — Exam Morning Card**

- BIOC: BATNA · Interests · Options · Criteria.
- Interests > positions. Behind every position is an interest.
- Integrative > distributive. Expand the pie before splitting.
- Know your BATNA. Without it, no walk-away power.
- Anchor or counter-anchor; don't accept first offer silently.
- FP for clear scope. T&M (capped) for evolving scope. CR types for unique work.
- Never bluff BATNA. Never trade ethics for concessions.
- Verbal drifts. Document agreements.
- Misrepresentation addressed, not exploited.
- Position-stalled email = move to synchronous interest conversation.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates negotiation impasse to sponsor with positions, BATNA, impact, and recommendation — never bluffs, never absorbs unprincipled instructions, never exploits counterparty misrepresentation.*
