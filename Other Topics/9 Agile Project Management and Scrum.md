# PMP Exam Master Brief: Agile Project Management and Scrum
### Weak Spots Targeted: Escalation · Communication

---

## 1. Topic Snapshot — Plain English

Agile project management is the practice of delivering value iteratively, incrementally, and adaptively using empirical process control — inspect, adapt, repeat. Scrum is the most-tested agile framework on the exam: three roles (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Developers), five events (Sprint, Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective), and three artifacts (Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment), each with a commitment (Product Goal, Sprint Goal, Definition of Done). Beyond Scrum, the exam covers Kanban, XP practices, and scaling patterns (SAFe, LeSS, Nexus) at a lighter level. Approximately 50% of the current exam is agile or hybrid; expect Scrum-specific role/event/artifact recognition questions plus situational behavior questions decided by agile principles. In real teams, "doing Scrum" without internalizing the values produces zombie ceremonies; on the exam, mixing the Scrum Master's role with the Product Owner's role, or treating velocity as a productivity KPI, kills your score.

**In one sentence:** This topic is about delivering value iteratively through empiricism, with Scrum as the most-tested framework — three roles, five events, three artifacts, three commitments.

## 2. The PMI Mindset on This Topic

### Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid Lens

| Lens | Agile / Scrum Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Predictive** | Not Scrum; but agile principles (iterative review, stakeholder engagement, lessons) can be borrowed at phase gates |
| **Agile** | Scrum / Kanban / XP / hybrid; team self-organizes; PO owns backlog; SM serves team; empirical control via events |
| **Hybrid** | Scrum at delivery layer + predictive governance for contracts, regulators, dependencies; release plan as integration backbone |

### Top 5 PMI-isms on This Topic

1. **The Scrum Master serves the team. Not directs.** Any answer where SM assigns work, decides scope, or overrides team self-organization is wrong.
2. **The Product Owner owns WHAT and WHY. The Developers own HOW.** Mixing these roles is the most common exam trap.
3. **Sprints are time-boxed and never extended.** If the team cannot complete the sprint goal, the sprint still ends; unfinished work returns to the backlog.
4. **The Sprint Goal is the commitment, not the Sprint Backlog.** The team can adapt the backlog to protect the goal.
5. **Definition of Done is a team commitment that lives across sprints.** It is not a sprint-level artifact; it is the standing quality bar.

### Ethics Angle
- **Respect:** Self-organization requires respect for the team's capacity to decide HOW.
- **Honesty:** Sprint review must show real working product; demos of mock-ups or unfinished work pretending to be done is dishonest.
- **Responsibility:** Scrum Master is responsible for upholding Scrum even when stakeholders/leaders pressure for deviations.
- **Fairness:** Backlog prioritization by PO must be transparent; favoritism toward loud stakeholders breaches Fairness.

## 4. Key Terms, Formulas & Acronyms

| Term / Formula | Definition / Calculation | Exam Trap to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Scrum** | Lightweight framework for solving complex adaptive problems with empirical process control | Trap: confusing Scrum with agile as a whole; Scrum is one framework |
| **Empiricism** | Knowledge from experience; decisions based on observation (transparency, inspection, adaptation) | Trap: planning more to feel certain; empiricism shortens the loop |
| **Scrum Values** | Commitment, Focus, Openness, Respect, Courage | Trap: confusing with Manifesto values; Scrum has its own five |
| **Product Owner (PO)** | Accountable for value of product; manages backlog, defines Product Goal | Trap: assuming PO writes all stories or directs the team |
| **Scrum Master (SM)** | Accountable for Scrum process effectiveness; serves team, PO, and organization | Trap: assuming SM is a PM or assigns tasks |
| **Developers** | Cross-functional team members accountable for the Increment | Trap: assuming Developers are only programmers; all delivery roles |
| **Sprint** | Time-box of ≤1 month (typically 2–4 weeks) where Increment is created | Trap: extending sprint to finish work; sprints end on time |
| **Sprint Planning** | Event where Scrum team plans the work of the Sprint, agrees Sprint Goal | Trap: PM/SM imposing the Sprint Goal |
| **Daily Scrum** | 15-minute event for Developers to inspect progress toward Sprint Goal | Trap: SM running it; Developers own it |
| **Sprint Review** | Event to inspect the Increment and adapt the backlog with stakeholders | Trap: confusing with retrospective; review = product, retro = process |
| **Sprint Retrospective** | Event for team to inspect itself and create plan for improvements | Trap: holding it without committing to at least one improvement |
| **Product Backlog** | Ordered list of what is needed in the product; PO accountable | Trap: assuming PM/SM manages it |
| **Sprint Backlog** | Sprint Goal + selected backlog items + plan for delivering them | Trap: confused with product backlog; Developers own sprint backlog |
| **Increment** | Concrete stepping stone toward Product Goal; meets Definition of Done | Trap: shipping below DoD and calling it an Increment |
| **Product Goal** | Long-term objective; commitment of the Product Backlog | Trap: confusing with Sprint Goal |
| **Sprint Goal** | Single objective for the Sprint; commitment of the Sprint Backlog | Trap: treating sprint backlog as the commitment instead |
| **Definition of Done (DoD)** | Quality commitment of the Increment | Trap: per-sprint redefinition; DoD spans sprints |
| **Definition of Ready (DoR)** | Criteria a backlog item meets before sprint planning | Trap: confusing with DoD; not a Scrum-Guide concept but commonly used |
| **Velocity** | Story points completed per sprint (team-specific planning metric) | Trap: using velocity to compare teams or as a productivity KPI |
| **Story Points** | Relative effort estimation unit | Trap: converting story points to hours |
| **User Story (INVEST)** | Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable | Trap: writing stories that fail INVEST and calling them user stories |
| **Burndown / Burnup Chart** | Visualizations of progress against scope/time | Trap: assuming burndown alone tells the story; burnup shows scope changes |
| **Kanban** | Pull-based flow system; WIP limits; continuous delivery | Trap: confusing Kanban with Scrum-with-no-sprints |
| **WIP Limit (Work-in-Progress)** | Maximum items in a workflow stage at once | Trap: ignoring WIP limits; they enforce flow discipline |
| **XP (Extreme Programming)** | Engineering practices: TDD, pair programming, CI, refactoring | Trap: confusing XP with Scrum; XP is engineering, Scrum is framework |
| **SAFe / LeSS / Nexus** | Scaling frameworks for multiple teams | Trap: assuming any large agile = SAFe; each scaling framework has distinct mechanics |

## 5. PM Behavior Cheat Sheet — FIRST / NEXT / BEST / EXCEPT

| Stem Signal | Scrum Master / PM Should FIRST Do | Because |
|---|---|---|
| Stakeholder demands a **scope addition mid-sprint** | Engage PO to assess vs Sprint Goal; if impact significant, defer to next sprint via backlog | Sprint Goal is protected; in-sprint scope changes are discouraged |
| **Team cannot finish** committed sprint backlog | End the sprint on time; unfinished work returns to the backlog; do not extend | Sprints are time-boxed; extension defeats empiricism |
| Sprint review shows **stakeholders are disengaged** | Investigate; restructure review to deliver more value; coach PO on inviting feedback | Sprint review is the feedback engine; disengagement = process failure |
| **Daily Scrum** is being run by the SM and is becoming status reporting | Coach the team to own the Daily Scrum; SM facilitates, does not direct | Daily Scrum is the Developers' event |
| PO writes user stories **without team input** | Coach the PO on collaboration; team should help shape stories | Backlog refinement is a team activity, not a PO solo activity |
| PO is **unavailable** during sprint | Surface as impediment; do not proceed without PO if value decisions arise | Customer collaboration is foundational; absence is impediment |
| Velocity is being used to **evaluate team performance** | Coach leadership on the planning-vs-performance distinction; reset measurement | Velocity gaming follows pressure |
| Team member from **outside Scrum team** wants daily individual status | Redirect to information radiators and sprint review | Pulling individuals breaks self-organization |
| **Definition of Done is loose** and varies by sprint | Co-create a stable DoD with team and PO that lives across sprints | DoD is a standing quality bar, not a sprint-level artifact |
| Sprint retrospective produces **no improvements** for multiple sprints | Coach the team to commit to one specific experiment per retro | Reflection without adaptation fails Principle 12 |

**Two Escalation-Specific Scenarios (your weak spot):**

| Stem Signal | Correct Escalation Logic |
|---|---|
| Stakeholder bypasses PO and adds work directly to the team | Re-engage stakeholder, restore PO ownership; escalate to sponsor if the bypass recurs, with the impact on PO authority |
| Leadership mandates extension of sprint or skipping retrospective to "hit a date" | Refuse; coach leadership on Scrum rules and the cost of breaking them; escalate to sponsor if leadership persists |

## 7. Tailoring Considerations

### Predictive Project
- Pure Scrum not used; agile principles borrowed for stakeholder engagement and continuous improvement
- Phase-gate retrospectives possible; iterative review checkpoints between gates
- Definition of Done concept can apply to predictive deliverables as acceptance criteria

### Agile / Hybrid
- Scrum used straight for product teams; Kanban for support/maintenance flows
- Hybrid: Scrum at delivery layer + predictive contracts, milestones, governance; release plan + product backlog as integration
- Scaling (SAFe, LeSS, Nexus) when multiple teams contribute to one product
- XP practices (TDD, pair, CI) embedded for engineering quality

### Industry / Regulatory Factors
- **Banking / Capital Markets (your context):** BSEC and Bangladesh Bank may require formal release sign-offs and audit trails; embed in DoD and release process, not bypass
- Regulator-facing scope often non-negotiable; PO must respect compliance backlog items as fixed-priority
- **Public-sector / RFP-driven:** Scrum within fixed-price contracts requires tailored procurement (T&M, ranged scope, governance check-ins)
- **Multi-team programs:** scaling frameworks adopted with care; many fail because they ignore principles

## 8. Common Wrong Answers & Why PMI Rejects Them

| Tempting Answer | Why Experienced PMs Pick It | Why PMI Marks It Wrong |
|---|---|---|
| "Extend the sprint by one week to finish the work" | The work matters; extending is pragmatic | Sprint time-box is sacred; unfinished work returns to backlog |
| "Have the Scrum Master decide the Sprint Goal" | SM has process knowledge | Scrum Team owns Sprint Goal; SM facilitates, does not impose |
| "Assign user stories to specific team members in sprint planning" | Skill-matched assignment is efficient | Developers self-organize during the sprint; assignment violates the principle |
| "Cancel the retrospective when the team is busy" | Crunch needs delivery focus | Retrospective is the adapt step; cancelling breaks empiricism |
| "Use velocity to compare teams" | Velocity is measurable | Velocity is team-specific; comparison is meaningless and produces gaming |
| "Have the PO accept stories that don't meet DoD if they are close" | Pragmatic acceptance keeps flow | DoD is the quality bar; below-DoD work is not done |
| "Skip the Daily Scrum on light sprints" | Less ceremony in light sprints | Daily Scrum is the daily inspect-adapt; removing it weakens empiricism |
| "Have the SM update the burndown chart for the team" | SM is responsible for the framework | Developers update the sprint progress; SM facilitates |
| "Adopt SAFe immediately because the team grew" | Scaling is needed for multi-team | Scaling frameworks need principles foundation; rushing adoption produces ceremony theater |

## 9. 10 Hardest Scenario-Based Questions

---

**Q1 — Ethics Dilemma (Predictive)**

You are the Scrum Master on a USD 8M digital-onboarding platform for a bank. Your sponsor instructs you to "skip the next two retrospectives" because retrospectives are "soft and slow us down." The team has just flagged an emerging quality issue that retrospectives would address. What should you do FIRST?

A. Skip the retrospectives as instructed since the sponsor has authority.
B. Refuse, document the role of retrospectives in addressing the quality issue, and meet with the sponsor to discuss the cost of skipping.
C. Hold the retrospectives anyway without telling the sponsor.
D. Reduce retrospectives to 15 minutes to satisfy both demands.

---

**Q2 — Agile Ceremony / Engagement**

You are a Scrum Master on a trading-platform product. Sprint Planning has been running for 6 hours per sprint and team members are exhausted. The Product Owner brings stories that are not refined; the team spends most of the time clarifying. What is the BEST action?

A. Reduce Sprint Planning to 2 hours regardless of refinement state.
B. Coach the PO and team on backlog refinement as a separate ongoing activity; ensure stories meet a Definition of Ready before Sprint Planning.
C. Have the Scrum Master refine the stories before Sprint Planning.
D. Skip backlog refinement and accept long planning sessions as normal.

---

**Q3 — FIRST Action, Conflict (Hybrid)**

You are leading a hybrid project. A senior stakeholder has bypassed the Product Owner and added three stories directly to the team's sprint backlog mid-sprint. The team is now overcommitted. The PO is upset. The senior stakeholder is the sponsor's peer. What should you do FIRST?

A. Reject the added stories and inform the senior stakeholder of the breach.
B. Meet with the senior stakeholder to understand the urgency, restore PO ownership of backlog decisions, and route the requests through the PO for prioritization.
C. Escalate to the sponsor immediately.
D. Accept the added stories and adjust sprint commitments.

---

**Q4 — Two Correct, Pick Best (Predictive)**

A predictive bank-IT project leader is considering introducing agile practices to improve responsiveness. The contract is fixed-scope and fixed-price. Stakeholder engagement is weak. Which TWO actions should you take FIRST?

A. Introduce iterative stakeholder review checkpoints between phase gates without changing the contract.
B. Convert the contract to time-and-materials.
C. Run team retrospectives at end of each phase to apply inspect-and-adapt.
D. Replace the project plan with a product backlog.
E. Adopt full Scrum overnight.

---

**Q5 — Ordered Sequence (Agile)**

A new Scrum team is starting on a payments product. The Scrum Master is setting up the team for the first sprint. Arrange the following actions in the correct order:

1. Run Sprint Planning to commit to the first Sprint Goal.
2. Hold team kickoff to align on Scrum values, roles, and how the team will work.
3. Co-create the Definition of Done with the team and Product Owner.
4. Initialize the Product Backlog with the PO and capture top items.

A. 1 → 2 → 3 → 4
B. 2 → 4 → 3 → 1
C. 4 → 3 → 2 → 1
D. 3 → 2 → 4 → 1

---

**Q6 — EXCEPT Question (Predictive)**

Which of the following is NOT one of the five events in Scrum?

A. Sprint Planning
B. Daily Scrum
C. Sprint Review
D. Sprint Retrospective
E. Backlog Grooming

**Q7 — Ethics / Honesty Hardline**

You are a Scrum Master on a regulatory-reporting product. The team has not met the Definition of Done for a story; the test suite is incomplete. The PO wants to demo the story at Sprint Review tomorrow with regulator stakeholders present. The PO suggests showing it as "complete" since "it almost is." What do you do?

A. Demo the story as complete since the PO has authority over the review.
B. Demo the story honestly as work in progress, explain what is and is not meeting DoD, and align on the path forward.
C. Skip demoing the story at Sprint Review.
D. Demo the story as complete and disclose the DoD gap only if asked.

---

**Q8 — Communication Strategy (Hybrid)**

You are managing a hybrid project. The Scrum team uses a digital task board accessible to all stakeholders. Regulators require formal monthly reporting. The sponsor wants brief weekly updates. Business stakeholders prefer the live board. What is the BEST approach?

A. Send the full task board state to all stakeholders weekly.
B. Tailor: live board for business stakeholders; weekly one-page summary for sponsor; formal monthly report for regulators — single source of truth, multiple output formats.
C. Discontinue formal reporting in favor of the board alone.
D. Replace the live board with formal monthly reports for all.

---

**Q9 — Escalation Judgment (Hybrid)**

You are a Scrum Master. A senior executive has directed the team to skip the Daily Scrum during a delivery crunch because "we need every minute coding." You have explained the role of the Daily Scrum once; the executive insists. Quality has begun slipping. What is the BEST next step?

A. Skip the Daily Scrum and document the executive's instruction.
B. Brief the sponsor with the role of the Daily Scrum, the quality data showing slippage, the executive's instruction, and a recommendation to restore the ceremony.
C. Continue holding the Daily Scrum secretly.
D. Cancel all ceremonies during the crunch.

---

**Q10 — Multiple Response (Agile/Hybrid)**

A Scrum team's Sprint Reviews have become poorly attended by stakeholders. Feedback is minimal. The team is making product decisions on its own. The Product Owner is concerned about value drift. Which THREE actions are MOST appropriate? (Select three.)

A. Investigate why stakeholders are not attending or engaging.
B. Cancel Sprint Reviews and replace them with a written monthly report.
C. Restructure Sprint Reviews to be more outcome-focused, demoing real working product against the Sprint Goal.
D. Allow the team to continue making product decisions since stakeholders have implicitly delegated.
E. Engage the PO to actively invite stakeholders and curate feedback questions.
F. Make Sprint Review attendance mandatory by sponsor mandate.

## 10. Answer Key with Full Justification

---

**Q1 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Retrospectives are the team's adapt step and Scrum non-negotiable. Skipping them under sponsor instruction breaks empiricism. The Scrum Master refuses, documents, and engages the sponsor on the cost. Option A complies with an instruction that breaks Scrum. Option C lies to the sponsor. Option D dilutes the ceremony to placate.

**Mindset Lesson:** Scrum Master defends the framework when leadership presses against it — with documented reasoning, not compliance or stealth.

---

**Q2 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Backlog refinement is an ongoing team activity that prepares stories for Sprint Planning. A Definition of Ready ensures stories arrive at planning in usable shape. Option A constrains the symptom. Option C centralizes refinement on the SM, breaking team ownership. Option D normalizes broken planning.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sprint Planning length is downstream of refinement quality — fix the upstream activity, not the time-box.

---

**Q3 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** Restoring PO ownership of backlog is the first move; engaging the stakeholder to understand urgency keeps the relationship intact while restoring the process. Option A rejects without engaging. Option C escalates before engaging. Option D accepts the bypass.

**Mindset Lesson:** Stakeholder bypass of PO is fixed by re-engagement + process restoration, not by reflexive rejection or immediate escalation.

---

**Q4 — Correct Answers: A and C**

**PMI Justification:** A (iterative review checkpoints) and C (phase-end retrospectives) introduce agile principles within the predictive shell without breaching the contract. Option B is unilateral and ignores procurement. Option D and E abandon the committed plan unilaterally.

**Mindset Lesson:** Agile-in-predictive is principle-introduction through checkpoints, not framework substitution.

---

**Q5 — Correct Answer: B (2 → 4 → 3 → 1)**

**PMI Justification:** Kickoff aligns on values, roles, working approach (2). PO initializes backlog with top items (4). Team and PO co-create DoD (3). Sprint Planning then commits the first Sprint Goal against an actual backlog and DoD (1). Reversing order plans against missing inputs.

**Mindset Lesson:** Alignment → backlog → DoD → first sprint — order the foundation before the activity.

---

**Q6 — Correct Answer: E**

**PMI Justification:** The five Scrum events are Sprint, Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective. "Backlog Grooming" / "Backlog Refinement" is an ongoing activity, not one of the five events in the Scrum Guide.

**Mindset Lesson:** Memorize the five events exactly — refinement is an activity, not an event.

---

**Q7 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** PMI Code of Ethics (Honesty) and Scrum's transparency principle require accurate demonstration of work status. Demoing incomplete work as complete to a regulator audience is a Honesty breach with regulatory consequences. Option A misrepresents. Option C hides. Option D is selective disclosure.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sprint Review honesty is non-negotiable — work in progress is shown as work in progress.

---

**Q8 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** PMBOK 7 endorses tailoring communication to audience. The live board is real-time; weekly summary serves the sponsor; monthly formal report serves regulators. Option A pushes raw board content. Option C abandons sponsor and regulator needs. Option D replaces real-time with delayed formal.

**Mindset Lesson:** Hybrid communication is a single source of truth with multiple output formats per audience.

---

**Q9 — Correct Answer: B**

**PMI Justification:** One engagement attempt failed; quality data shows slippage. The Scrum Master briefs the sponsor with the role of the ceremony, the quality data, the executive instruction, and a recommendation. Option A complies with an instruction breaking Scrum. Option C lies. Option D escalates the damage.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sprint ceremonies under executive override are restored through sponsor governance with data — not through compliance or stealth.

---

**Q10 — Correct Answers: A, C, E**

**PMI Justification:**
- **A:** Diagnose stakeholder disengagement before intervention.
- **C:** Restructuring Sprint Review to be outcome-focused on working product against Sprint Goal addresses the value of attending.
- **E:** PO actively engaging stakeholders is part of the role.
- B is wrong: replacing Sprint Review with a written report removes the empirical feedback engine.
- D is wrong: stakeholder non-attendance is not delegation of authority.
- F is wrong: mandatory attendance is coercive and does not produce engagement.

**Mindset Lesson:** Sprint Review engagement is fixed by relevance + PO active inviting + diagnosis — not by elimination, assumption of delegation, or coercion.

## 12. 60-Second Recap Card

**AGILE PROJECT MANAGEMENT AND SCRUM — Exam Morning Card**

- 3 roles (PO, SM, Developers) · 5 events (Sprint, Planning, Daily, Review, Retro) · 3 artifacts (Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment) · 3 commitments (Product Goal, Sprint Goal, DoD).
- PO owns WHAT/WHY · Developers own HOW · SM serves process.
- Sprint Goal is the commitment. Sprint Backlog is adaptable to protect the goal.
- Sprints end on time. Unfinished work returns to backlog. Never extend.
- Definition of Done lives across sprints. Team + PO co-create.
- Velocity is team-specific planning input. Never a productivity metric. Never cross-team comparison.
- Daily Scrum is for Developers. SM facilitates, does not run.
- Backlog refinement = ongoing activity, NOT one of the five Scrum events.
- Kanban: pull-based, WIP limits, no sprints. XP: engineering practices (TDD, pair, CI).
- Hybrid: Scrum at delivery layer + predictive governance for contracts/regulators.

**Escalation one-liner to burn in:** *PMI escalates Scrum violations to the sponsor with framework reasoning, data on cost of the deviation, and a recommended restoration — never with compliance, never with stealth.*
